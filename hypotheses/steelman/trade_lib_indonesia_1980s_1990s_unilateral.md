# Steelman — Indonesia 1985-1995 unilateral liberalisation

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Oil-price-collapse-driven, not policy-driven.** The 1985
   reforms followed the 1985-1986 oil-price collapse, which forced
   currency depreciation and made non-oil exports competitive.
   Attributing the post-1985 trade rise to liberalisation rather
   than to the oil-price-induced exchange-rate adjustment overstates
   the policy contribution.

2. **Crony-capitalist beneficiaries.** Much of the
   manufacturing-export expansion went to politically-connected
   firms; the welfare-distribution pattern is far from the
   Heckscher-Ohlin labour-abundant-country prediction.

3. **AFC erased the gains.** The 1997-1999 collapse reversed much
   of the 1985-1996 progress; the long-run trajectory required
   reconstruction over the 2000s.

## What the framework still captures

The descriptive structural-break test records the 1985-1996
pattern. The AFC dynamics are excluded by the sample boundary
choice. Welfare-distribution and political-economy critiques
deserve separate specs.
