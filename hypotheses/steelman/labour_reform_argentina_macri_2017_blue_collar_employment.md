# Steelman — Argentina Macri 2017 labour reform

The strongest pro-reform reading: the 2018-2019 peso crisis and IMF program drove Argentina into recession and the labour-market response masks any genuine reform effect. Vaca Muerta sector-specific reform did produce measurable employment gains in Neuquén province — sector-level rather than aggregate evidence supports the targeted-reform-works claim, but the spec scores it on aggregate which biases toward the null. Cetrángolo and Curcio 2020 argue the broader labour-package failure was a political-economy outcome not a reform-design failure, and that the Vaca Muerta sector-specific approach validates targeted flexibilisation as a model. A clean refutation pathway exists if a v2 disaggregates by province with Neuquén as treated and other hydrocarbon-producing provinces as control — that within-Argentina event study would isolate the Vaca Muerta reform effect from the macro-collapse channel and may reverse the aggregate null. The current spec's null-leaning prior is a defensible-but-narrow reading, and the v2 should explicitly test the targeted-reform claim that the aggregate spec cannot.
