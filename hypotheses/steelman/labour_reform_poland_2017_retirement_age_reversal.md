# Steelman — Poland 2017 retirement-age reversal

The strongest counter-reading: the magnitude of the labour-supply effect depends critically on the share of 55-64 workers near the new (lower) eligibility ages versus the prior-law track, and the spec's aggregate measure may overstate the effect by lumping together workers who would have retired anyway with marginal-decision workers. NBP and Polish Social Insurance Institution (ZUS) post-implementation evaluations show that early pension take-up was high in 2018-2019 but that some workers re-entered the labour force after taking pensions (combining pension income with continued employment), partially offsetting the headline employment-rate decline. The Polish post-2018 economic boom and labour shortages may also have drawn 55-64 workers back into employment despite the lower eligibility age, weakening the employment-rate gap. The magnitude threshold of -3pp is itself a defensible-but-not-uncontroversial choice. A clean v2 would use a regression-discontinuity on the age cutoff at the implementation date — the workers exactly at the threshold are the cleanest identification, and the aggregate synth-DiD averages over heterogeneous treatment effects.
