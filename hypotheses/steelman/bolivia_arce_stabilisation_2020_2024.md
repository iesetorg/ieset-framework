# Steelman — Bolivia Arce stabilisation 2020-2024

**The strongest argument against the causal claim:**

The hypothesis claims the Arce stabilisation failed by 2024 across reserves, inflation, and per-capita growth. The opposing view is that the spec is too narrow temporally and that an honest stabilisation effort can take 5-7 years to show structural results.

Specific objections:

1. **Window is too short.** Stabilisation programmes typically take years; Argentina 1991, Brazil 1994, Peru 1990 all required multi-year horizons. Cutting at 2024 may misread a transition phase as a failure.

2. **External shocks dominate.** The 2022 global energy-price spike, US Fed tightening cycle, and post-COVID supply chain effects are exogenous shocks that would have stressed any peg regardless of policy quality. The Arce administration's policy effort may have minimised damage relative to a counterfactual of disorderly devaluation.

3. **Capital controls bought time for upstream investment.** YPFB exploration contracts signed 2023-2024 may produce a gas-export recovery in 2026-2028 that retrospectively validates the controls-as-bridge framing.

4. **Inflation control held longer than expected.** Through 2023 Bolivian inflation was below 5% — a striking achievement given the FX-reserve depletion. The peg-defence transmission to consumer prices is slower than orthodox theory predicts, suggesting the textbook framing is incomplete.

5. **Distributional effects of disorderly devaluation would be worse.** A floating boliviano in 2023 would have produced a sharp price jump on import staples, hurting low-income consumers. The administration's choice to ration FX rather than devalue may be defensible on welfare grounds.

**How this should shape the result card:**

(a) Report 2024 endpoint with explicit acknowledgement that 2025-2026 data will be more dispositive.
(b) Decompose the inflation and reserve trajectories from external shocks.
(c) Note the upstream-gas investment lag in commentary.
(d) Allow that "failed by 2024" may be partial — peg held longer than orthodoxy predicted, even if the policy is unsustainable on a 5-year horizon.
