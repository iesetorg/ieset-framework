# Steelman: Stockton SEED Guaranteed-Income Effect

The strongest counter is that n=125 is far too small to support a 12pp employment-rate ATT estimate as causal evidence — the standard error reported in West-Castro 2021 itself shows the effect is barely significant at conventional levels and highly sensitive to sample-attrition assumptions. The RCT also coincided with Stockton's broader economic recovery from its 2012 municipal bankruptcy, with rapidly rising local employment that the matched control may not fully absorb. The selection of treated recipients from a single pre-screened low-income neighbourhood pool restricts external validity; demographic, racial, and household-composition characteristics differ from population-wide UBI candidates. The 12pp full-time employment-rate result was widely amplified in advocacy contexts but has not been replicated in subsequent guaranteed-income RCTs (Vivero 2024 LA County), some of which find smaller or null employment effects. A defensible reading: SEED produced suggestive evidence that small unconditional transfers do not decrease labour supply among low-income working-age recipients, but the headline +12pp employment effect is fragile and the policy-debate weight placed on it has outpaced the underlying statistical power.
