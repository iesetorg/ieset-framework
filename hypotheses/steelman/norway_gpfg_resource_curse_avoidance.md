# Steelman: norway_gpfg_resource_curse_avoidance

The strongest objection is that Norway did not become successful because of the
GPFG. Norway entered the oil era with unusually strong democratic institutions,
high administrative capacity, rule-of-law norms, social trust, and a small,
wealthy population. Those initial conditions may explain both the creation of a
high-quality sovereign wealth fund and the avoidance of the resource curse.

The donor pool is also difficult. Russia, Venezuela, Nigeria, Angola, Algeria,
Kazakhstan, Azerbaijan, Ecuador, Gabon, and Saudi Arabia differ from Norway in
state capacity, conflict exposure, demography, sanctions risk, and oil-sector
governance. Synthetic-control weighting can improve the comparison, but it cannot
turn those countries into a true counterfactual Norway. A positive result should
therefore be read as evidence that Norway's post-1990 resource-rent architecture
is consistent with resource-curse avoidance, not as a clean causal proof that the
GPFG alone produced the growth gap.
