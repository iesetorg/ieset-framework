# Steelman — Lucas expectations anchoring and post-Volcker inflation persistence

The strongest argument against this hypothesis is that the persistence decline is mechanical, not credibility-driven. When a stationary AR process reverts to a low and stable mean, the rolling AR(1) coefficient on its level mechanically declines: the same series with the same shock-propagation parameters but a lower unconditional mean shows up in reduced-form regressions as less persistent because the level is not drifting anywhere. Stock and Watson's own 2007 analysis showed that nearly the entire decline in inflation persistence can be reproduced as a fall in the variance of the stochastic-trend component, which is to say, the world stopped throwing 1970s-magnitude permanent shocks at US inflation, and the conditional process did the rest. A Volcker-credibility story requires showing that, holding shock variance constant, the response to a given shock decays faster post-1985 than pre-1979 — and the evidence on that conditional persistence is weaker. The post-2021 inflation surge is the cleanest natural test the regime has run in 40 years: a large supply-cum-demand shock propagated through the economy, and inflation took 24+ months to return toward target, with substantial wage-price feedback. If expectations were anchored in the Lucas / Goodfriend-King sense, the shock should have decayed within 4-8 quarters; the fact that it did not suggests anchoring is weaker than the canonical reading claims, or that the credibility was always partial. From the post-Keynesian side, the entire framing is wrong because inflation persistence is institutional (wage-bargaining horizons, contract length, sectoral pricing power) rather than expectational, and the post-1985 decline tracks the decline of indexed wage contracts and sectoral union density far more cleanly than it tracks any monetary-regime variable. The hypothesis would be sharpened by reporting impulse responses to identified monetary shocks (Romer-Romer narrative or high-frequency identification) rather than reduced-form AR coefficients, since that is the cleanest test of whether the propagation parameters changed. As specified, the test is vulnerable to the mean-reversion artefact reading.
