# Steelman — Basel III capital buildup 2013-2019

## The strongest opposing argument

Bank capital ratios rose substantially during 2008-2012 in the immediate post-GFC aftermath, before the Basel III phase-in formally began. Pre/post-2013 comparisons risk attributing to Basel III the gains that were already locked in by stress-test-driven recapitalisations under crisis-era national rules (CCAR, EBA stress tests). The "treatment effect" of Basel III over and above the post-GFC recap pulse is much smaller and harder to identify.

Risk-weight optimisation (RWA-density decline) also confounds the headline capital-ratio rise: banks raised CET1/RWA partly by lowering RWA through internal-model parameter shifts, not by raising loss-absorbing equity.

## What the framework still captures

The 3 pp threshold is small enough to be cleared even after acknowledging the pre-2013 recap pulse, but large enough to fail if Basel III had no marginal effect at all. The country-FE structure absorbs country-specific recap timing.
