# Steelman — Bolivia Morales resource nationalism 2006-2019

**The strongest argument against the causal claim:**

The hypothesis frames Bolivia 2006-2019 as a successful resource-nationalism case. The opposing view is that the success was almost entirely a function of the gas-price supercycle and that the long-run damage from under-investment in upstream gas — visible from 2014 onward — falsifies the model on a longer horizon.

Specific objections:

1. **Gas-price tailwind 2006-2014 dominates fiscal accounts.** Bolivian gas-export prices to Brazil and Argentina rose from ~$2/MMBtu (2003) to ~$10/MMBtu (2014). The fiscal surpluses, FX accumulation, and welfare-programme funding during 2006-2014 were almost mechanically a function of price, not policy.

2. **Upstream gas under-investment is a delayed cost.** Foreign IOCs reduced exploration substantially after the 2006 nationalisation. Proven reserves fell; production peaked around 2014 and declined materially through 2019. By 2023 Bolivia became a net gas importer. The "success" frame ignores the foreseeable consequence of expropriation: capital strikes deferred but did not eliminate the upstream-investment shortfall.

3. **Currency peg eventually unsustainable.** The boliviano-USD peg held during the gas-export window but became increasingly costly to defend as gas revenues fell after 2014. By 2023 BCB FX reserves were depleted and parallel-rate dollarisation accelerated. The peg-success story has a 2023 endgame that should be weighted against the 2006-2019 window.

4. **Non-hydrocarbon productivity growth is weak.** Manufacturing share of GDP fell modestly through the period; service-sector and informal-sector employment grew but with low productivity. The poverty reduction is largely a function of cash-transfer redistribution from gas rents, not productivity convergence.

5. **Institutional erosion is uncosted.** MAS-era judicial restructuring, indefinite re-election attempt (2016 referendum), and 2019 disputed-election crisis represent institutional costs the growth-and-poverty frame ignores.

**How this should shape the result card:**

(a) Report 2006-2014 vs 2014-2019 sub-periods separately to distinguish cyclical from structural.
(b) Show the post-2019 trajectory in commentary as a robustness flag.
(c) Decompose poverty reduction into transfers vs labour-income channels.
(d) Acknowledge upstream gas reserve decline.
