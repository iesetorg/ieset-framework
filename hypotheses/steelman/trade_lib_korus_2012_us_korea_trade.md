# Steelman — KORUS 2012

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Pre-existing integration.** Korea's manufacturing exports
   were already deeply integrated with US final demand through
   the global electronics and automotive supply chains; the
   incremental KORUS effect on bilateral volumes is small
   relative to the integration baseline.

2. **Industrial policy dominates.** Korean export performance is
   driven by capital-investment cycles and industrial-policy
   support for chaebol exporters. FTA-attributable variation is a
   small share of total trade-share variance.

3. **2018 renegotiation contaminates.** The Trump-era KORUS 2.0
   renegotiation introduced steel quotas and other non-tariff
   adjustments that cut against the original FTA's effect; mixing
   the original and renegotiated periods in one post-treatment
   window blurs the test.

## What the framework still captures

The synthetic-control gap on trade-openness is a clean first-order
test. The robustness sensitivity for the 2018 renegotiation is
explicit. PARTIAL/REFUTED outcomes are genuinely possible and
the framework records them honestly.
