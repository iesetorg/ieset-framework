# Steelman

Intergenerational mobility may be one of the best arguments for redistribution. Lower disposable-income inequality can reduce neighbourhood segregation, improve childhood stability, and limit the compounding advantage of inherited wealth. Public transfers may also equalize early-life inputs before labour markets ever matter.

The strongest counterprediction is that tax-transfer redistribution and lower Gini explain more mobility variation than education, housing, and rule-of-law controls. If mobility rises mainly with income compression, the opportunity-institutions framing is incomplete.
