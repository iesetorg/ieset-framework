# Steelman - market_dynamism_regulatory_freedom_hightech_exports

Claim tested: Higher market-oriented regulatory freedom predicts stronger high-technology export performance.

The strongest skeptical reading is that high-technology exports often reflect industrial policy, public research, procurement, education systems, and multinational production networks. Regulatory freedom scores may also reward countries already rich enough to sustain complex export sectors. A null or wrong-signed result would not be surprising under developmental-state accounts and would count against simple deregulation-as-innovation claims.
