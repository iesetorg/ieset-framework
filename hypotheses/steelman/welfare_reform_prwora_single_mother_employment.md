# Steelman: PRWORA 1996 Single-Mother Employment Effect

The strongest counter-argument is that the late-1990s single-mother LFP surge is fully explained by EITC expansion (OBRA 1990 + OBRA 1993) and a tight labour market with sub-4% unemployment by 1999, not by TANF work requirements. Meyer-Rosenbaum (2001) decomposition assigns roughly 60% of the pre-2000 single-mother employment rise to EITC and only 25% to welfare reform; subsequent revisions push the EITC share higher when state-level EITC supplements are accounted for. State TANF-adoption timing is also nearly simultaneous (1996-1997 across all states), giving the staggered-DiD design weak identifying variation and making it vulnerable to negative-weight contamination. Finally, the comparator group (single childless women) experienced its own LFP gains over the same period, so the difference-in-differences may understate or overstate the treatment effect depending on which group's secular trend is more sensitive to macro conditions. A more conservative reading: PRWORA contributed at the margin but the headline single-mother employment increase is mostly EITC + cycle, and the claim of a 5pp ATT attributable specifically to TANF rules is fragile.
