# Steelman — Morocco FTA cascade

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Industrial policy drove the auto-sector takeoff.** Renault's
   Tangier plant (2012) and aerospace investments resulted from
   tax holidays, free-zone exemptions, and infrastructure
   subsidies — not principally from FTA preferences. Crediting
   the FTAs misattributes the success.

2. **Comparator weakness.** Tunisia and Egypt experienced
   revolutionary disruption; Algeria is oil-dominated; Jordan is
   small and highly idiosyncratic. The MENA donor pool is thin.

3. **Phosphate-export dependence.** Morocco's trade-openness is
   partly a commodity-price story (phosphate prices peaked
   2008-2014).

## What the framework still captures

The descriptive comparison records the trajectory honestly. The
industrial-policy channel deserves a separate spec; this one
intentionally focuses on the trade-channel observation without
over-claiming causal attribution.
