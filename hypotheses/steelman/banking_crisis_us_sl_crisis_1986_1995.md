# Steelman — US S&L crisis 1986-1995

## The strongest opposing argument

The S&L crisis is sometimes downweighted because the macro impact (real GDP, unemployment) was modest by GFC or Asian-crisis standards. Counting it as a "systemic banking crisis" alongside Iceland 2008 or Korea 1997 stretches the category. The interest-rate squeeze (Volcker disinflation pushing deposit-funding costs above the fixed-rate-mortgage portfolio yields) was the proximate cause, which is a fundamentally different mechanism from credit-boom-bust dynamics.

## What the framework still captures

The metric set deliberately uses institution-count failures and the RTC-creation event in place of the GDP-decline-heavy thresholds used for canonical EM crises. The pre-registered acknowledgment that S&L's macro signature was small (>= 1 pp slowdown threshold) is itself useful — it documents a wide variance of "what counts as a banking crisis" across cases.
