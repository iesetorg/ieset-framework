# Steelman: Credit Gap and REER Export Squeeze

Credit growth and exchange-rate appreciation can both accompany positive
productivity shocks, capital inflows, or commodity booms. In those cases export
growth might remain strong despite apparent overvaluation.

The interaction test is useful because it asks whether credit excess and real
appreciation jointly mark a tradable-sector squeeze after country and year
effects. A null would support the view that competitiveness channels are more
case-specific than the simple macro-financial story implies.
