# Steelman — AfCFTA 2021 short-window evaluation

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Three years is far too short.** The EU single market took a
   decade to produce measurable trade-creation effects. AfCFTA
   ratification was incomplete through 2024, and tariff phase-in
   schedules were still under negotiation. A 2021-2024 evaluation
   window cannot legitimately falsify the long-run claim.

2. **COVID-19 dominates.** The 2020-2021 trade collapse and
   uneven recovery confound any measurement of an AfCFTA effect.
   The sample period overlaps the supply-chain disruption,
   commodity price shocks, and rebound that no FTA-effect
   estimator can cleanly separate from the policy treatment.

3. **Infrastructure is the binding constraint.** Most intra-
   African trade is constrained by cross-border road, rail, port,
   and customs-digitisation gaps. Tariff reduction without
   infrastructure investment cannot produce trade volumes; the
   AfCFTA itself is partly an infrastructure-investment
   coordination mechanism, but those investments materialise on
   a decade-plus horizon.

## What the framework still captures

The descriptive null prediction is correct on the merits. The
spec is honest about its limitations: it records a baseline,
not a final verdict. A follow-on 2025-2030 spec will measure
the implementation phase, and a 2030-2040 spec will test the
mature claim. Pre-registering the baseline now is the
methodologically right move.
