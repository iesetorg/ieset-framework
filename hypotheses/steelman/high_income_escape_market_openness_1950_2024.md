# Steelman

The strongest claim is that high-income escape requires exposure to world prices and competitive pressure. Export promotion can work when it forces firms to meet external market tests, but persistent domestic protection often preserves inefficient incumbents and prevents consumers and downstream firms from accessing cheaper inputs.

The strongest developmentalist response is that almost every successful late industrializer used selective protection at some stage. The fair test is therefore not openness versus any state role, but whether countries that combined export openness with domestic competition outperformed those that kept domestic protection after scale-up.
