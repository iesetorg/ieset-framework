# Steelman

The strongest market-liberal claim is about persistence. Short reforms can be overwhelmed by crises or reversals, but sustained market institutions should compound through investment, entry, trade, and reallocation. A twenty-year horizon is more relevant to prosperity than a short event window.

The strongest opposing view is that reforms are endogenous: countries liberalize after crises, and the apparent payoff may reflect regression to the mean or external anchors such as EU accession. Replication must handle pre-trends and matched controls carefully.
