# Steelman — rent_price_controls_building_permits_eu_panel

The strongest counterargument is that many European rent-control systems exempt
new construction, allow negotiated initial rents, or operate alongside public
housing and tax subsidies. A simple intensity index may label very different
regimes as equivalent.

Building permits also respond to credit conditions, land constraints, migration,
and local planning rules. Rent controls may be adopted in overheated markets
where building activity was already constrained, creating reverse causality.

The test is useful as a supply-warning screen, not a verdict on every tenant
protection. A stronger design would exploit reform discontinuities, split
new-build exemptions from incumbent-tenancy controls, and use city-level
permitting data.
