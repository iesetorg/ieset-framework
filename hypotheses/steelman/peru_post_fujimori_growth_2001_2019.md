# Steelman — Peru post-Fujimori growth 2001-2019

**The strongest argument against the causal claim:**

The hypothesis attributes Peru's 2001-2019 growth premium to institutional persistence. The opposing view is that the China commodity supercycle (2003-2014) is the dominant driver and that institutional persistence is a sufficient but not necessary explanation.

Specific objections:

1. **China supercycle dominates the growth window.** Copper prices rose from $0.80/lb (2001) to $4.00/lb (2011); zinc and gold tracked similar trajectories. Mining royalties and FDI inflows during this period mechanically lift Peruvian GDP regardless of policy framework. Bolivia and Ecuador (less institutionally credible) also grew rapidly during this window.

2. **Institutional persistence claim is fragile post-2016.** The 2016-2022 political crisis (Kuczynski resignation, Vizcarra impeachment, Castillo impeachment, Boluarte succession) demonstrates the constraint on the executive is contingent on Congressional opposition, not durable institutions. The macro framework survived but only because no administration achieved a working majority — a different story than "institutions constrain populism."

3. **Fiscal-balance dispersion is artificially low because of commodity-revenue automaticity.** Mining-canon and royalty inflows during the supercycle produced fiscal surpluses without policy effort; once the cycle ended (2014+), fiscal deficits widened materially.

4. **Inflation control is a benchmark task, not evidence of exceptional institutions.** Multiple Latin American central banks (Chile, Colombia, Mexico, Brazil) achieved single-digit inflation over 2001-2019 with similar inflation-targeting frameworks. Peru's success here is the regional norm, not a Peru-specific institutional premium.

5. **Distributional and informality outcomes are weak.** Peru's informal-employment share remained above 70% throughout the window; gains accrued disproportionately to extractive sectors and Lima. A "growth premium" that does not transmit to productivity outside extractives is not a robust institutional success.

**How this should shape the result card:**

(a) Report results net of commodity-price controls explicitly.
(b) Decompose the institutional-persistence claim from the China-supercycle confound.
(c) Note the post-2016 governance crisis as fragility evidence.
(d) Acknowledge informality and Lima-province concentration in the discussion.
