# Steelman: Education attainment × wage premium

## Strongest case for the claim

Goldin-Katz: technology's race against education has consistently been won by technology
since 1980 in the US, producing a sustained positive trend in the tertiary premium despite
massive expansion in tertiary attainment. The hypothesis tests whether this generalises to
the OECD panel.

## Strongest case against

Credential inflation predicts a falling premium as supply outpaces demand. Some countries
(KOR, ESP) show evidence of premium decline post-2010 with massive tertiary expansion.
The OECD-mean trend may be near-zero rather than positive.

## Key uncertainties

- Premium measurement methodology cross-country.

## What would change my mind

Significantly negative year-FE slope would indicate credential inflation winning, refuting
the SBTC framework.
