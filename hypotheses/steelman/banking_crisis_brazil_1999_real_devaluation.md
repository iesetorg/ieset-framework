# Steelman — Brazil 1999 real devaluation

## The strongest opposing argument

Brazil 1999 is mostly a currency event, not a banking crisis. Including it in a banking-crisis series risks category confusion. The real GDP impact was small (1999 grew slightly), inflation passthrough was muted, and the banking system did not require a 1998-1999 vintage rescue — making the multi-metric "currency-only" framing feel more like a definitional defence than a substantive empirical test.

## What the framework still captures

The deliberately-low multi-metric threshold (3 of 4, currency / programme / output / inflation) is calibrated to the "managed currency crisis" pattern. The deliberate omission of a Laeven-Valencia banking-crisis metric encodes the prediction that PROER-era cleanup means Brazil 1999 is a useful negative-control for systemic-banking-crisis predictors elsewhere in the panel.
