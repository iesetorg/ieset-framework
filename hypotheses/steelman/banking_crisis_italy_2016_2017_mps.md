# Steelman — Italy 2016-2017 MPS

## The strongest opposing argument

Italy 2016-2017 sits between "real systemic crisis" and "structural NPL overhang in low-growth banking system" and the framing decision affects the verdict mechanically. By choosing thresholds (NPL >= 16%, BRRD events) that almost any reasonable reading of Italy 2016-2017 will clear, the multi-metric structure is testing labels rather than empirics. A harder version would require an unemployment-rate or output-decline metric with a non-trivial threshold.

The bank-credit-to-GDP decline is also confounded by demand-side effects (Italian sovereign-yield spike 2011-2012, Eurozone double-dip recession) that have nothing to do with NPL-driven supply restriction.

## What the framework still captures

The deliberately-weak threshold structure (3 of 4 with no output metric) encodes the prediction that Italy 2016-2017 is closer to "managed banking distress" than to a Laeven-Valencia systemic crisis. That negative-control framing is itself useful for the panel of canonical cases.
