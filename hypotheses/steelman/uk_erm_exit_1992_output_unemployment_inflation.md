# Steelman - uk_erm_exit_1992_output_unemployment_inflation

The opposite reading is that ERM exit was not the causal recovery driver: the UK was already exiting recession, lower global rates helped, and unemployment rising into 1993 shows the devaluation did not immediately heal the labour market.

The event-window verdict is descriptive and should not be read as a full structural model. Concurrent policy changes, global shocks, and measurement revisions can all weaken causal attribution.
