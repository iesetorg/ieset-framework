# Steelman — German manufacturing VA decline 2017-2024 decomposition

**The strongest arguments against the framework's "four-channel decomposition of German manufacturing decline" framing:**

1. **Manufacturing VA-share decline is normal secular Baumol-Bowen services-sector growth, not "decline" in a welfare-relevant sense.** Every advanced economy has seen manufacturing-share drift down for decades as services expand disproportionately. Germany's 22% → 18% shift between 2017 and 2024 is mechanically a continuation of a trend visible since reunification. Treating this as a discrete "decline" needing decomposition is to treat a structural-shift pattern as a puzzle. The steelman: the correct comparator is not "Germany kept 22%" but "Germany drifted at 0.3%/year as always." The incremental 2017-2024 shift above the secular trend is smaller than headline numbers suggest.

2. **The four hypothesised channels are not independent — they interact and the SDA will yield order-sensitive shares.** Energy-cost increases raise input costs, which pass through to unit labour costs in electricity-intensive sectors. ULC increases incentivise offshoring, which increases Chinese import penetration. Chinese import penetration in intermediate goods shifts domestic production toward assembly, which reduces energy intensity and attenuates the energy channel. Reporting "channel X explains Y%" with a non-trivial residual risks false precision. SDA share assignments depend on decomposition order and cannot be read as causal weights.

3. **Automotive transition (internal combustion → electric) is a bigger driver than any of the four hypothesised channels.** German automotive is the single-largest manufacturing subsector by VA; its disruption by EV transition (Tesla, BYD), supply-chain re-siting (battery cells in Eastern Europe, China, US), and the 2015+ diesel-scandal regulatory aftermath produced structural impact on German manufacturing VA that the framework's four channels do not capture. If automotive-transition-specific effects dominate, the decomposition's residual will be large and the four-channel story will look misleading.

4. **Unit labour costs in German manufacturing remained disciplined.** German ULC growth 2017-2024 was moderate compared to France, Italy, UK over the same window; the Hartz-reform legacy and sectoral wage moderation in trade-exposed manufacturing are real. Framing "labour cost channel" as a meaningful share of decline risks being disconfirmed by the data — a reader should be prepared for that channel to come back near zero. If so, the prior that all four channels contribute is partly wrong, and the writeup must say so clearly.

5. **US IRA-driven re-siting is plausibly overstated.** The 2022 US Inflation Reduction Act does provide subsidy-incentivised re-siting, but evidence of material German capacity relocating to the US by 2024 is thin — announcements exist, operational production less so. Treating "US IRA-driven production relocation" as a channel may attribute to 2022-2024 what is actually a 2025-2030 effect. Be cautious about reading announcements as realised output shifts.

6. **Measurement artefact in VA share.** Nominal VA is sensitive to relative price movements. A chemicals industry hit by energy-cost inflation may show VA share preserved or growing in nominal terms while physical output contracts — or the reverse. Real-VA measurement requires deflators that may not fully capture the relative-price shocks of 2021-2024. A "VA share declined by 4 percentage points" headline is partly a price-index artefact.

7. **Intra-EU comparators may show the same pattern.** Italian, French manufacturing VA shares have also trended down in the window. If the decomposition is Germany-only without comparators, the "Germany-specific" quality of the decline is unclear. A v2 that runs the SDA for multiple countries would allow assessing whether the channels are Germany-specific or common-EU.

**How this should shape the v1 result card when run:**

- Report the 2017-2024 change in the context of the longer-run 1995-2024 secular trend. A "4 percentage point decline" needs to be compared to the prior trend.
- Report decomposition order sensitivity explicitly. SDA is not causal and should not be presented as such.
- Acknowledge the residual channel. If it exceeds 30%, the four-channel story is partial and the writeup must name what is missing (likely automotive-transition and services-tilt).
- Flag automotive-transition as a likely missing channel and consider a v1.1 with an automotive-specific channel included.
- Be cautious about the US-IRA-channel magnitude. Report announced vs realised relocations separately if possible.
- Frame the finding descriptively and humbly. This is not a causal identification; it is an accounting decomposition with known limitations.
- Do not allow the headline to read "Germany deindustrialised" — the facts are more nuanced, employment is preserved, export competitiveness is mixed by subsector, and the decomposition itself will not deliver a clean one-line story.
