# Steelman — Zoning relaxation and housing-supply response

## The strongest opposing argument

The supply-side / classical-liberal reading of housing economics has substantial recent empirical support but also genuine vulnerabilities:

1. **Selection-on-outcome.** Minneapolis 2040 and Auckland Unitary Plan are canonically cited because they produced visible supply responses. The spec's case selection inherits that bias. Other zoning-relaxation episodes (various California ADU laws, Portland upzoning, Oregon HB 2001) have produced thinner measurable effects.

2. **Auckland counter-evidence.** Murray-Phillips (2024) and several Reserve Bank of New Zealand notes have argued that Auckland's rental-price moderation post-2016 mostly reflects general NZ housing-market cooling and migration shifts, not the Unitary Plan supply response. Greenaway-McGrevy and Phillips reach the opposite conclusion. The empirical case is genuinely contested.

3. **Composition matters.** Minneapolis post-2020 supply response is concentrated in 2-4 unit "missing middle" infill in already-gentrifying neighbourhoods, with modest aggregate citywide effect. The reform may be welfare-improving in expected ways while producing aggregate numbers that look thin in a synthetic-control frame.

4. **Tokyo is a non-replicable case.** Japan's permissive national land-use system pairs with a shrinking population and demographic decline. Stable Tokyo rents are partly the result of net out-migration, not supply elasticity per se. Treating Tokyo as evidence for zoning relaxation in growing cities is a category error.

5. **Houston counter-confound.** Houston's lack of formal zoning is paired with deed restrictions, MUDs, and other private land-use restrictions that act zoning-like. The "no zoning Houston" frame is partly mythology; comparing Houston to "zoned" metros is comparing different mechanisms of land-use control.

6. **Demand-side channels.** Rental-price growth depends on demand (in-migration, employment growth, real wages) at least as much as on supply elasticity. A clean test of the supply-side claim needs to control for demand shocks, which the spec does only imperfectly.

7. **Synthetic-control donor-pool sensitivity.** Especially for Auckland (small set of plausible donors), the synthetic estimate is sensitive to which comparator metros are included. Different defensible donor pools can produce qualitatively different results.

## What the framework still captures

The dual-outcome design (completions AND rents) and the Houston INFORMATIVE check let the heterodox reading win in several ways. The spec is candidate-status precisely because the case selection and donor-pool issues need human review before pre-registration. The hypothesis honestly acknowledges its limitations and lays out the heterodox win paths.
