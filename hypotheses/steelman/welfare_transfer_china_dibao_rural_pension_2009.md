# Steelman: China Dibao + NRPS Rural Poverty Effect

The strongest counter is that CHN's 2009-2014 rural-poverty decline is primarily a growth and migration story, not a transfer story. Real rural per-capita income grew 8-12% annually in this window, urbanisation rates rose 2pp/year transferring rural population to urban occupations, and migration-remittance flows from coastal employment dwarf the per-recipient transfer values from dibao or NRPS. The Golan-Sicular-Umapathi (2017) decomposition attributes less than 20% of the 2009-2014 rural-poverty decline to social-protection channels, with the remainder explained by growth-and-migration. NRPS payouts were also nominally small (RMB 55-100/month basic pension) — too small to substantially shift household consumption among rural elderly with no other income — and dibao coverage thresholds were set extremely tight at province-level discretion, with documented exclusion of eligible households via informal screening. Provincial rollout of NRPS was correlated with fiscal capacity and urbanisation-stage, biasing the staggered-DiD identification. A defensible reading: dibao and NRPS contributed marginally to the rural-poverty decline but the dominant cause was growth, urbanisation, and migration; the 5pp social-protection-channel claim is at the upper bound of plausibility.
