# Steelman — China property-bubble bust 2020-2024

## The strongest opposing argument

The 2020-2024 property contraction is real, but reading it as a "structural drag" understates three counter-considerations:

1. **Deliberate rebalancing, not crisis.** Beijing's "three red lines" policy was an explicit choice to deflate the residential-investment-led growth model in favour of advanced manufacturing, services, and household consumption. A growth slowdown is not a failure of the policy; it is the policy. Measuring the "drag" without netting out the intentional reduction in property investment over-attributes harm.

2. **Ghost-cities thesis is stale.** The 2010s ghost-cities narrative (Ordos, etc.) overstated permanent overcapacity. Many "ghost" districts filled in over a decade as urbanisation continued. The 2021-2023 stress is concentrated in over-leveraged developers, not in physical inventory glut at the national level.

3. **COVID + zero-COVID confound.** Disentangling the property contraction from the 2020-2022 zero-COVID demand shock and the 2022-2023 reopening incomplete recovery is non-trivial. The post-2021 GDP slowdown has at least three parents (property, COVID policy, demographic cliff) and attributing the magnitude to any one is over-determined.

4. **Selective on observables.** China's GFCF share of GDP at ~42% in 2019 was extreme by world standards. Mean reversion toward the global ~25% norm produces a mechanical 10+ percentage point fall regardless of policy. The "drag" is partially convergence to a sustainable investment ratio.

## What the framework still captures

The descriptive structural-break test is deliberately threshold-based — a 2pp/yr growth gap and 3pp GFCF share fall are real macro signals regardless of attribution. Whether the bust is "drag" or "rebalancing," the magnitudes are first-order and the framework records them honestly. Decomposition into intentional rebalancing vs unintended financial-stress is the subject of follow-on hypotheses (LGFV stress, household-consumption-share rebalancing).
