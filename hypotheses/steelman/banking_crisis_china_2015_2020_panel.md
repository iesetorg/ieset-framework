# Steelman — China 2015-2024 financial-stress episode

## The strongest opposing argument

Combining the 2015 stock crash and the 2020-2024 property-sector deleveraging into one "episode" is awkward — they are mechanistically distinct and 5+ years apart. China's data publication practices and the absence of Laeven-Valencia post-2017 coverage make the negative-control claim ("not systemic") under-identified: it is consistent with the crisis being managed under capital controls AND with the Laeven-Valencia methodology not having the access needed to code a CN-specific systemic event if one occurred.

## What the framework still captures

The pre-registered structure (extreme credit run-up pre-2017, large property-price retracement post-2021, NOT Laeven-Valencia coded) tests a specific theory: that capital-control-protected crisis dynamics produce a recognisable macroprudential signature without the canonical sudden-stop / programme-entry markers. If the negative-control fails (Laeven-Valencia eventually codes CN systemic), the hypothesis pivots to refuted — that is a meaningful pre-commitment.
