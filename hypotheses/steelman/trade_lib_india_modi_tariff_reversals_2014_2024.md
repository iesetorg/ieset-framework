# Steelman — India Modi-era tariff reversals

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Industrial policy, not protectionism.** The PLI schemes are
   targeted, time-limited, performance-conditional industrial
   subsidies — closer to East Asian developmentalist models than
   to import-substituting protectionism. Framing them as
   "tariff reversals" misrepresents the policy intent.

2. **Tariff increases are concentrated and modest.** WDI weighted
   tariffs rose by 3-5 pp aggregate, but most of the increase is
   concentrated in specific sectors (electronics components,
   medical devices) where domestic-capability targets are
   articulated. The headline figure overstates the breadth.

3. **Trade-openness ratio is endogenous.** GDP growth lifted the
   denominator faster than trade, so falling trade-share-of-GDP
   may reflect strong domestic-economy growth rather than
   protectionist policy. India's nominal merchandise-trade
   volumes continued to rise.

## What the framework still captures

The descriptive test reports the magnitude of tariff and openness
changes without claiming a welfare verdict. The PARTIAL/REFUTED
paths are clean for the heterodox-developmentalist reading. The
spec is honest that the welfare interpretation is contested; it
records the pattern.
