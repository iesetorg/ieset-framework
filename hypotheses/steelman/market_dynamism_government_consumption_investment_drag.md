# Steelman - market_dynamism_government_consumption_investment_drag

Claim tested: Higher government-consumption shares predict lower investment shares and slower subsequent real GDP per-capita growth.

The strongest skeptical reading is that government consumption includes education, health, administration, infrastructure operations, and stabilization services that can raise private returns. Richer or older societies may choose larger public sectors without causing lower growth, while crisis years can raise spending shares mechanically. This spec is evidence against state-allocation claims only if the negative relationship survives fixed effects and controls; positive estimates would support the view that some public consumption is complementary to long-run growth.
