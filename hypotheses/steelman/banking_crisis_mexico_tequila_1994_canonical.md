# Steelman — Mexico Tequila Crisis 1994

## The strongest opposing argument

The Tequila crisis is often interpreted through a sudden-stop / capital-flight lens: the precipitating event was political (Colosio assassination, Chiapas uprising, current-account financing dependent on dollar-linked Tesobonos) more than it was a structural banking-system breakdown. Counting "Laeven-Valencia banking-crisis coded" as a metric is partly downstream of the currency event rather than independent evidence of pre-existing banking fragility.

Mexico's recovery was also unusually fast (V-shape, 1996 strong rebound) suggesting that the canonical "deep + persistent" banking-crisis signature does not actually fit Mexico as cleanly as it fits Asia 1997 or GFC 2008.

## What the framework still captures

The 5-metric checklist captures both the currency dimension (depreciation, current-account reversal) and the banking dimension (Laeven-Valencia, programme entry) so the test does not require both to be equally severe — only that the canonical multi-metric pattern is recognisable. The 4-of-5 threshold tolerates Mexico's faster recovery without forcing a refutation.
