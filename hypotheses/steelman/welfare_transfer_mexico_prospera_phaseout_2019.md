# Steelman: Mexico Prospera Phase-Out 2019

The strongest counter is that AMLO's restructuring did not eliminate transfers — it replaced Prospera with directly-targeted Becas Bienestar (school grants for low-income students) and the Pensión Universal (elderly), shifting allocation patterns rather than total cash flow to poor households. The unconditional design removed administration-and-corraling costs and the headline transfer-coverage indicator actually rose. CONEVAL 2022 multidimensional-poverty data show Mexico's poverty rate fell from 41.9% in 2018 to 36.3% in 2022 — improvement, not deterioration — reflecting the combined effect of new transfers, minimum-wage rises (16% in 2019, additional double-digit hikes annually), and remittance growth. The CCT literature's RCT identification was for the Progresa rollout, not for the rollback — the symmetry assumption that removing CCT conditionality produces equal and opposite effects is not empirically established and the small-area heterogeneity literature suggests asymmetry. A defensible reading: AMLO's phase-out broke a high-quality administrative architecture but the evidence for measurable poverty/schooling deterioration in the 2019-2022 window is contested and runs against CONEVAL's headline trajectory.
