# Steelman — Peru Fujimori shock therapy 1990-2000

**The strongest argument against the causal claim:**

The hypothesis attributes the 1990-2000 stabilisation and growth recovery to the policy-content of the Fujimori package. The opposing view is that mean-reversion plus terms-of-trade tailwinds plus the post-Sendero-Luminoso security normalisation explain most of the recovery, and the policy-content effect is much smaller than headline numbers suggest.

Specific objections:

1. **Mean reversion from a uniquely depressed base.** Peru's 1990 GDP per capita stood roughly 30% below its 1981 peak, with 7000% inflation and a near-collapsed state. Any moderately competent stabilisation would produce dramatic apparent gains; the counterfactual of "no Fujishock" almost certainly involves some recovery as well, because the hyperinflation equilibrium was not durable.

2. **Terms-of-trade tailwind 1993-2000.** Copper, gold, and zinc prices recovered through the 1990s, lifting Peruvian export receipts independently of policy. A synthetic-control donor pool weighted heavily on Chile risks attributing commodity-price gains to Fujishock.

3. **Sendero Luminoso defeat is non-policy security normalisation.** The 1992 capture of Abimael Guzmán reduced political-violence risk premia substantially. The post-1992 investment recovery may reflect security normalisation rather than economic policy.

4. **Privatisation revenues distorted the 1991-1996 fiscal accounts.** Roughly $9 billion in privatisation receipts allowed apparent fiscal balance without genuine expenditure restructuring; once the privatisation cushion exhausted (1997-1998), Peru's external position deteriorated and 1998-2000 growth was weak.

5. **Distributional cost was severe.** Real wages collapsed in 1990-1992 and recovered slowly; poverty rose before falling. A growth-only frame ignores welfare costs that shaped Peruvian political economy through the 2000s.

**How this should shape the result card:**

(a) Report PER vs synth donor pool with explicit copper/gold price decomposition.
(b) Show 1992 security-normalisation event-study window separately.
(c) Report investment-share gains net of privatisation receipts.
(d) Acknowledge the welfare cost in the discussion even if it is outside the falsification criteria.
