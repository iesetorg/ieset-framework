# Steelman — Australia WorkChoices employment effect

The dominant heterodox reading: Australia's 1996-2007 employment performance was driven primarily by the China-import-driven terms-of-trade shock that lifted real incomes, capital expenditure, and labour demand in mining states and through them the rest of the economy. The Workplace Relations Act and WorkChoices were marginal contributors at most, and the 2007 election repudiation of WorkChoices reflected widespread evidence (HILDA panel, AWIRS surveys) that AWA individual contracts produced wage compression and rising inequality without measurable employment-margin gains. Peetz 2007 and the post-2007 Fair Work review found that abolishing AWAs in 2009 did not slow employment growth, undermining the claim that AWAs were doing the work. The terms-of-trade-controlled specification is the discipline: if the employment-rate gap collapses to under +0.5pp once the commodity channel is partialled out, the reform-channel interpretation fails. A v2 should add a difference-in-differences on the 2009 repeal date as a within-Australia robustness window.
