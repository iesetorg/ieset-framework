# Steelman

High tax revenue does not mechanically reduce private investment. In capable states, tax-financed redistribution can buy social peace, healthier workers, better infrastructure, and lower idiosyncratic risk. Those channels may raise expected private returns enough to offset the cash-flow and after-tax-return costs.

The strongest counterprediction is therefore positive or null: once institutional quality, trade openness, and macro stability are controlled, high-tax countries with credible public services should not show weaker private investment. If the coefficient is flat or positive, the market-incentive mechanism is overstated.
