# Steelman: Singapore high-skill migration policy

## Strongest case for the claim

Singapore's foreign-born share is the highest in the OECD/peer group at ~38%. Per-capita
growth has been consistently in the top decile globally. Tiered migration policy is the
most-cited institutional driver in the SGP-as-model literature.

## Strongest case against

SGP's growth is overdetermined; isolating the migration channel from industrial policy,
savings rates, and governance is hard. The comparator panel is small (only HKG matches
SGP's openness).

## Key uncertainties

- Migration-channel decomposition.

## What would change my mind

If the migration-channel share is < 10% in decomposition, growth is primarily other
factors and SGP migration policy is incidental.
