# Steelman — Spain 2012 dismissal-cost reform

The heterodox reading argues the post-2014 Spanish recovery is overwhelmingly a story of ECB OMT (July 2012), QE (2015 onward), and tourism-cycle rebound — none of which are caused by the labour reform. The synthetic-control donor pool cannot recover the counterfactual because every plausible peer (Italy, Portugal, Greece) was on its own austerity-plus-reform trajectory, and pre-2012 outcome paths are dominated by the asymmetric burst of the construction bubble that Spain experienced and the others did not. Bentolila-Cahuc-Dolado 2012 and CCOO union evaluations argue the reform's binding effect was downward wage-bargaining-coverage rather than upward employment, with the real wage falling 6-8% relative to productivity 2012-2014 — a transfer from labour to capital that is not measured by an unemployment-rate test. The wage-channel paired outcome is the discipline: if real wages fell by more than 2pp relative to the donor pool, the heterodox reader has a clean line that the labour-market clearing happened on the wage margin, not the quantity margin, and Spanish workers absorbed the adjustment cost. A robust spec must report both margins.
