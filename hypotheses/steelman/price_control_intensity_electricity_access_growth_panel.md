# Steelman — price_control_intensity_electricity_access_growth_panel

The strongest counterargument is that Heritage monetary freedom is a broad
macro-institutional proxy, not a direct energy-tariff or cost-recovery measure.
It may capture inflation control, central-bank credibility, or general
administrative capacity rather than electricity price controls.

Subsidized access expansion can work when the state funds connection costs,
cross-subsidizes transparently, or uses public investment to overcome fixed
network costs. A positive price-signal result would not prove that targeted
subsidies are harmful.

The candidate is best read as a first-pass test of whether broad price-control
regimes are compatible with rapid access expansion. A stronger version should
use utility-level tariffs, cost-recovery ratios, outage data, and connection
subsidy design.
