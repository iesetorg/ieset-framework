# Steelman — Nationalisation can preserve output when governance quality is strong

**The strongest arguments for nationalisation and against the production-collapse chain:**

1. **Norway's Equinor/Statoil is a clean counter-case.** Norwegian petroleum policy explicitly nationalised a strategic share (67% state equity in Equinor; full ownership of the NCS through Petoro; the state petroleum fund as a sovereign-wealth vehicle). Production, productivity, and fiscal contribution have been world-class for 40 years. The mechanism "nationalisation produces collapse" is demonstrably wrong; the real mechanism is "nationalisation under weak governance produces collapse," which is a different claim.

2. **Mexico's Pemex decline is not a nationalisation story per se.** Pemex has been nationalised since 1938; the more rapid post-2000s decline is about geological exhaustion at Cantarell, chronic under-taxation of reserves, and political capture of the surplus — factors present under nationalisation but also present under alternative governance. Without a credible counterfactual (which is partly why the 2013 private-sector opening was implemented and then reversed), the nationalisation-vs-governance decomposition is weak.

3. **Argentina YPF 2012 renationalisation appears to have done little to production trajectory.** Pre-renationalisation YPF under Repsol was already under-investing given Argentine fiscal and macro stress; post-renationalisation the Vaca Muerta shale development has continued and in some measures accelerated. Attributing production trajectory to the 2012 event rather than to macro conditions is tenuous.

4. **PDVSA collapse coincided with sanctions.** US sanctions from 2017 onwards dramatically restricted Venezuela's access to US refineries, imported diluents, technology, and financing. Separating the nationalisation-governance effect from the sanctions-and-isolation effect is essential; without this decomposition, the causal chain attributes the collapse to the wrong driver.

5. **Private-governance oil companies have also exhibited governance failure.** Petrobras (semi-private, state-controlled) suffered the Lava Jato corruption scandal; Anglo-Swedish-Iranian-era production collapsed; US shale majors burned cash for a decade. Production collapse and capex-discipline failure are not nationalisation-specific; they are governance-quality-specific.

6. **Fiscal contribution is the wrong outcome for nationalisation's intended purpose.** Nationalisation is often adopted specifically to REDIRECT rents from export-oriented tax receipts to social-spending dividends. A fall in tax-revenue contribution alongside a rise in direct social-spending contribution is what the policy was designed to do; measuring only the former misrepresents success.

7. **Expertise emigration is common in any declining oil province.** North Sea engineers emigrated to the Middle East; Alberta tar-sands engineers relocated during 2015-2020. Attributing Venezuelan or Argentine technical emigration to nationalisation specifically, rather than to the broader collapse and cycle dynamics, is an identification reach.

8. **Synthetic-control donor pool is fragile for national oil companies.** The relevant comparators (Ecopetrol, Statoil, Saudi Aramco, ADNOC) differ in geology, reserves, geopolitics, and capital-market access to such a degree that synthetic control will likely be underpowered and sensitive to donor selection.

**How this should shape the v1 result card when run:**

- Report Norway Equinor prominently in the donor pool; if Norway would be assigned high synthetic weight to VEN absent sanctions, that finding materially qualifies the chain claim.
- Decompose the VEN case into pre-sanctions (2003-2016) and post-sanctions (2017+) windows; if the chain effect is concentrated post-sanctions, attribution is ambiguous.
- Report fiscal contribution AND social-spending-dividend routing combined; do not measure only tax-revenue contribution.
- Flag geological exhaustion effects (Cantarell MEX) as separate from governance effects.
- Be cautious about small donor pools; use permutation-based placebo inference to report RMSPE ratios.
- Distinguish "state ownership" from "political governance" as separate treatments if possible; Norway shows these can diverge cleanly.
