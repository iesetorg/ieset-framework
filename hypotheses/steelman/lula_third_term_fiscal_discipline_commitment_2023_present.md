# Steelman — Lula third term fiscal discipline commitment (2023-present)

**The strongest arguments against coding the Marco Fiscal as a clean fiscal-institution success:**

1. **The PT's prior record includes the Rousseff nova matriz econômica (2011-2016), which produced the pedaladas fiscais accounting manipulation and the 2015-2016 recession.** A framework assessment that treats Lula 3.0 in isolation from this PT institutional memory risks assuming away the erosion-under-pressure pattern that post-Keynesian and institutional critics identify. The honest test is whether the 2023 framework holds through a real growth shock, not just through a tailwind period.

2. **The Bolsonaro-era counterfactual is not a fiscal-conservative baseline.** Pre-Lula Brazil had already breached the Teto de Gastos multiple times (PEC dos Precatórios, PEC da Transição) and institutionalised the Orçamento Secreto emendas mechanism that structurally raises non-discretionary spending. A realistic no-Lula 2023 scenario is not cleaner than the Marco Fiscal; it is messier. Comparing Marco Fiscal to a stylised fiscally-conservative baseline overstates the framework's achievement relative to the realistic counterfactual.

3. **The Rousseff-era muddle (2011-2016) is analytically distinct from Lula 2003-2010 AND from Lula 2023+.** The PT is not a monolith; internal economic-wing heterogeneity (Palocci-Meirelles market-orthodox wing vs. Mantega-developmentalist wing vs. Haddad current modulation) means that "PT fiscal discipline" is a category label, not a stable policy content. A test focused on the 2023 institution is appropriate; a test interpreted as "PT governance" in general is a category error.

4. **Real interest-rate tightening by the BCB through 2024-2025 forced de-facto fiscal compression independent of the statutory framework.** Market-driven yields and BCB Selic path compressed the effective primary-balance constraint more binding than the LC 200/2023 band would have required alone. Attributing observed compliance to the statutory framework rather than to market discipline + CB tightness is an identification problem.

5. **Industrial-policy commitments via BNDES and Nova Indústria Brasil are off-book relative to the primary-balance framework.** Concessional-credit subsidies, state-bank operations, and parafiscal channels (FAT, FNDCT) can grow while the statutory aggregate stays compliant. A narrow primary-balance test misses whether the off-book fiscal expansion is absorbing the resource flows that the statutory discipline is containing.

**How this should shape the result card:**

- Report the primary-balance and real-spending-growth outcomes against the statutory band transparently. Pre-registered binary compliance indicator is the headline test.
- Separately report off-book fiscal flows (BNDES subsidy-equivalent, FAT operations, parafiscal net spending) as context. The statutory framework might hold while total public-sector flows expand.
- Publish the BCB real-rate trajectory alongside the fiscal outcome. Compliance achieved at 10%+ real rates is different in character from compliance achieved at 4% real rates.
- Explicitly note whether the framework was amended statutorily during the observation window. Any amendment widening the band is coded as erosion, per the falsification rule.
- Compare the 2023-2026 primary-balance path to the Rousseff-era 2011-2015 path for institutional-memory context, clearly labelled as descriptive historical comparison rather than counterfactual.
- Acknowledge identification limits: whether the framework caused compliance or merely codified market-driven compliance cannot be cleanly separated in a single-country observation.
