# Steelman — EPL is not the dominant cause of Southern European youth unemployment

**The strongest arguments for strong EPL and union coverage, against the causal chain:**

1. **The Nordic counter-pattern refutes EPL-alone causation.** Denmark, Sweden, Netherlands all combine relatively strong EPL and high union coverage with LOW youth unemployment and high employment rates among women and young people. The mechanism "EPL produces insider-outsider duality" is falsified by these cases: EPL interacts with complementary institutions (active labour market policy, apprenticeship systems, flexicurity unemployment insurance). The chain claim survives only as "EPL + weak ALMP + dual contract law," which is a much narrower and more defensible claim.

2. **Youth unemployment in Southern Europe is primarily a macroeconomic-demand story.** Spain's youth unemployment peaked at 56% in 2013 during a demand collapse driven by the eurozone sovereign crisis; Greece at 60%; Italy at 43%. The share attributable to EPL vs to the demand collapse is highly contested; Blanchard-Summers "hysteresis" and Bentolila-Dolado-Jimeno literature finds the demand component dominant.

3. **Dual contract law, not EPL level, is the real mechanism.** Spain's pre-2012 labour market had permanent contracts (high EPL) AND unlimited temporary contracts (low EPL for hiring) — the classic "dual" structure. The damage was from the INTERACTION: firms hired only on temporary contracts because the permanent-contract gap was too large. Blaming EPL generally misses that a flat EPL structure (either uniformly high or uniformly low) can be fine; it is the gap that traps youth.

4. **Brain drain is about wage levels and opportunity, not EPL.** Spanish, Italian, Greek youth emigrated 2011-2019 primarily to higher-paying UK, Germany, Ireland. The wage gap and opportunity differential between origin and destination dominates the migration decision; EPL is at most a secondary driver.

5. **Fertility decline is a pan-European phenomenon with multiple drivers.** Italy, Spain, Greece TFR declines pre-date their high-EPL era and continue through both high-EPL and reformed periods. Housing costs, contraceptive availability, female labour-force participation, and cultural shifts are all operating; attributing TFR decline to a causal chain running through EPL-induced youth-unemployment is a multi-step identification argument with weak instruments.

6. **The 2012 Spanish reforma and 2015 Italian Jobs Act did not produce the expected gains.** Spanish youth unemployment remained elevated through 2019 despite significant EPL reduction in 2012. Italy's Jobs Act produced modest effects on temporary-permanent conversion but not large changes in youth-unemployment trajectory. If reducing EPL is supposed to reverse the chain, the weak post-reform rebound is uncomfortable for the hypothesis.

7. **Insider wage protection is a feature, not a bug, for stability of middle-class incomes.** The "first-order success" of stable incumbent wages through the 2008-2012 crisis is substantial; Spanish-Italian wage compression at the bottom kept working-poor shares lower than they would be in a more flexible market. The cost of not having this stability in the US during the 2008-2012 period was acute working-class real-wage collapse.

8. **Productivity drag attribution is confounded with manufacturing-share decline.** Southern European productivity growth has stagnated in parallel with deindustrialisation toward low-productivity services. Attributing productivity-growth failure to a third-order effect of EPL rather than to sectoral composition changes is an identification reach.

**How this should shape the v1 result card when run:**

- Report Nordic countries as the critical counter-pattern; if the EPL coefficient is driven entirely by South vs North differences, the "EPL-alone" mechanism is wrong.
- Interact EPL with ALMP spending and apprenticeship-system indicators; report the coefficient on the interaction.
- Decompose the insider-outsider channel: measure the permanent-temporary contract GAP explicitly and show effects separately.
- Flag the macro-demand collapse as a confound for ESP/GRC/PRT/ITA during 2011-2013; report results with and without that window.
- Be conservative on fertility and productivity third-order claims: these are multi-step causal arguments with weak instruments.
- Report incumbent-wage protection benefits alongside outsider costs; do not headline findings without both.
