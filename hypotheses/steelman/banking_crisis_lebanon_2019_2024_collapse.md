# Steelman — Lebanon 2019-2024 banking collapse

## The strongest opposing argument

Lebanon's data quality during 2019-2024 is deeply impaired: official statistics agency capacity was disrupted, official exchange rates diverged from parallel-market rates by 50x at peak, and CPI sub-indices have known censoring on subsidised goods. Multi-metric checklists relying on WDI-grade data risk locking in measurement-period biases. The IMF Article IV mission to Lebanon was suspended for an extended period.

Lebanon's collapse also has political-economy roots that fit poorly into a "banking-crisis" framework: BdL's "financial engineering" was a quasi-fiscal operation funding sovereign deficits through bank balance sheets, and the unwind reflects sovereign-debt sustainability failure as much as bank-supervision failure.

## What the framework still captures

Even with measurement caveats, the metric values are so extreme (>90% depreciation, default, capital controls, GDP halved) that data-quality concerns do not threaten the canonical-pattern verdict. The 5-of-6 threshold builds in tolerance for one metric being unreadable without forcing a refutation.
