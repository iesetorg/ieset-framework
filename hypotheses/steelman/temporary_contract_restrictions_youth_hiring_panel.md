# Steelman: Temporary Contract Restrictions Youth Hiring Panel

The strongest counterargument is that temporary-contract liberalisation can create segmented labour markets where young workers cycle through unstable jobs without acquiring bargaining power, training, or permanent contracts. Restrictions on fixed-term work may force firms to internalise training costs and offer durable jobs rather than disposable entry slots.

Youth employment rates are also influenced by education enrolment, housing, migration, and aggregate demand. If strict temporary-contract rules coexist with apprenticeships, wage subsidies, or strong school-to-work systems, they may not reduce entry. The hypothesis should be rejected if strictness lowers temporary work but raises permanent youth employment, improves job tenure, or leaves total youth employment unchanged after controlling for permanent-contract EPL and macro conditions.
