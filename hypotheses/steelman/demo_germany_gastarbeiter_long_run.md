# Steelman: Germany Gastarbeiter long-run effect

## Strongest case for the claim

The Gastarbeiter cohort is a well-documented long-run demographic shock with persistent
labour-market and integration-policy implications. The descriptive case is straightforward:
foreign-born share rose from <2% (1955) to >15% (2020), with sustained second-generation
labour-market gaps to natives.

## Strongest case against

Gastarbeiter contribution is partly absorbed into the broader EU-15 free-movement migration
trajectory; isolating the Gastarbeiter cohort effect from later inflows is difficult.

## Key uncertainties

- Phase-by-phase decomposition.

## What would change my mind

Working-age share contribution from migrant cohort below 0.5pp would refute the
"transformative" framing.
