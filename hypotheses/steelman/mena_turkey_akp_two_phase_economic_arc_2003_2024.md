# Steelman — Turkey AKP two-phase economic arc 2003-2024

**Strongest arguments against the framework's framing:**

1. **The 2003-2013 outperformance is partly post-2001-crisis recovery.** Turkey's 2001 banking crisis collapsed GDP; the subsequent recovery is mechanical base-effect that any government would have presided over. Attributing 2003-2008 boom to AKP policy content vs IMF-mandated reforms (which AKP inherited) requires a clean counterfactual the synthetic-control may not provide.

2. **EU accession momentum is the institutional-reform driver.** The 2003-2007 Turkish reform agenda was largely EU-pre-accession-conditioned. Once EU accession stalled (2007+), the institutional-reform momentum dissipated. This is correctly attributed to AKP-era timing but the AKP's role was as transmission belt rather than independent reform driver.

3. **The phase-2014-break date is contestable.** Some accounts place the doctrine break at the 2018 currency crisis or the 2016 coup-attempt aftermath. Others see continuous deterioration from 2013 Gezi protests onwards. The spec's clean 2013/2014 split may force a sharper break than the underlying institutional regression actually had.

4. **Real-GDP-pc trajectory was good in unorthodox phase too — until late 2018.** Turkey grew strongly through 2017 even with monetary heterodoxy emerging; the August 2018 currency crisis was the first major rupture. The "late-phase regression" framing may be over-stylised: 2014-2017 looked OK, 2018-2024 looks bad. A more honest framing has three phases or a continuous decline starting later.

5. **External-environment cycles matter.** EM credit cycle peaked 2007-2013; 2014-2024 EM peers also struggled with US-rate-cycle stress (Fed taper 2013, US tightening 2018, US tightening 2022-2023). Turkey's poor 2018-2023 performance partly reflects EM-wide stress, not Turkey-specific policy unorthodoxy. The synthetic-control donor pool should absorb common shocks, but the lira's 90%+ depreciation 2018-2024 is much larger than peer depreciation, indicating Turkey-specific factors dominate.

6. **The 2023-2024 partial-orthodoxy return (Mehmet Şimşek) complicates phase 2 endpoint.** Post-May 2023 election, CBRT raised policy rates from 8.5% to 50%; orthodox stabilisation reasserted. The 2024 data may show partial recovery; cumulative 2014-2024 gap masks the within-phase reversal. The hypothesis should report 2014-2022 and 2023-2024 separately within phase 2.

**How this should shape the v1 result card:**

- Report multi-phase decomposition: 2003-2007 EU-accession boom, 2008-2013 post-GFC recovery, 2014-2018 early heterodoxy, 2019-2022 acceleration, 2023-2024 partial orthodoxy.
- Acknowledge EM-cycle-versus-Turkey-specific decomposition.
- Report lira depreciation magnitude vs EM peer median as a key validation metric.
- Frame as "Turkey diverged sharply from EM peers in unorthodox direction with recent partial reversal" rather than monotone two-phase narrative.
