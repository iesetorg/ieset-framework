# Steelman — M2 expansion correlates with asset price inflation

**The strongest argument against this hypothesis:**

The apparent post-2008 correlation between M2 growth and asset price inflation is substantially a confounded relationship driven by the zero-lower-bound policy environment. Central banks expanded balance sheets precisely in response to weak aggregate demand and financial stress; asset prices recovered from genuinely depressed post-GFC levels for reasons having little to do with monetary mechanics per se. Attributing the co-movement to a direct M2 → asset-price causal channel commits the standard correlation-is-causation error at a macro level.

Specific objections the framework must engage with:

1. **Reverse causation via policy reaction function.** Central banks expand balance sheets when markets stress; markets then recover as stress abates. The correlation is real but the causal direction runs partly through the policy reaction function, not from money to assets.

2. **Endogenous demand for safe assets.** Post-2008 safe-asset shortage (Caballero-Farhi-Gourinchas) explains equity and real estate re-rating independently of M2 aggregates. The demand curve shifted; M2 is not doing the explanatory work critics imagine.

3. **M2 is a poor measure of the relevant monetary variable.** Reserves, credit growth, duration-weighted asset purchases, and shadow-banking liquidity measures all tell different stories. A cointegrating relationship with M2 specifically may be spurious once more relevant aggregates are examined.

4. **The 2021-2022 inflation episode challenges the lag story.** If M2 expansion reliably leads asset inflation, the 2020-2021 M2 spike should have produced asset-price blow-off followed by mean-reversion; instead both asset prices and consumer prices rose contemporaneously, suggesting a different mechanism (fiscal-monetary interaction) dominated.

5. **Sample selection.** The hypothesis samples 2008-2025 — a regime of unprecedented central bank activism. Pre-2008 panels show weaker or absent relationships. If the finding holds only under a specific policy regime, it is not a structural feature of money-asset relations.

**How this steelman should be handled in the result card:**

If the hypothesis is supported in the panel as specified, the result card must note that the finding is consistent with either a direct money-to-asset channel OR the policy-reaction-function confound, and that the spec as written cannot distinguish between them. A follow-up hypothesis with an identification strategy (e.g., high-frequency event studies around pure QE announcements isolated from stress response) would be needed to separate the mechanisms.
