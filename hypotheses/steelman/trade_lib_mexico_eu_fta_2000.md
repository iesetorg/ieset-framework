# Steelman — Mexico-EU FTA 2000

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **NAFTA dominance.** Mexico's trade is overwhelmingly with the
   US; the EU bilateral effect is too small relative to NAFTA
   flows to detect cleanly in aggregate trade-openness.

2. **EU competitive disadvantage.** Mexican exports to the EU are
   constrained by transport costs and competition from
   geographically-closer EU enlargement-cohort suppliers. The
   FTA cannot overcome these structural factors.

3. **Diversification was rhetorical.** The post-2000 period saw
   continued US-share concentration in Mexican exports rather than
   real diversification.

## What the framework still captures

The descriptive comparison records a small positive expected
effect honestly. The PARTIAL/REFUTED paths handle the modal
"no detectable effect" outcome.
