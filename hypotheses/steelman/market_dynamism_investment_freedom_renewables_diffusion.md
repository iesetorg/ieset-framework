# Steelman - market_dynamism_investment_freedom_renewables_diffusion

Claim tested: Higher investment freedom and private-credit depth predict faster renewable-electricity capacity diffusion.

The strongest skeptical reading is that renewable diffusion has often depended on feed-in tariffs, auctions, grid planning, public guarantees, and state-backed transmission investment. Private finance may follow public mandates rather than cause deployment. The hypothesis is useful because it is falsifiable both ways: support would show that open private-capital channels are compatible with rapid clean-energy buildout, while null or negative results would strengthen state-capacity and planning explanations.
