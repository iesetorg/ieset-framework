# Steelman — UK Brexit 2020 trade response

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **COVID confounding.** The 2020-2022 COVID period made every
   country's trade ratios volatile in idiosyncratic ways; isolating
   a Brexit signal from COVID supply-chain effects is genuinely
   hard.

2. **Service-sector resilience.** UK financial services, legal
   services, and creative industries have proved more resilient
   than the goods-trade pessimists predicted. Aggregating
   services and goods together obscures the heterogeneity.

3. **Donor-pool contamination.** Most European comparators
   experienced Russia-Ukraine-war energy shocks of varying
   severity; the synthetic counterfactual is itself shocked,
   biasing the estimated SC gap in unpredictable directions.

## What the framework still captures

The synthetic-control test is the right tool. The COVID-sensitivity
analysis explicitly addresses the first critique. PARTIAL/REFUTED
verdict paths cleanly handle the modal "small or noisy effect"
outcome.
