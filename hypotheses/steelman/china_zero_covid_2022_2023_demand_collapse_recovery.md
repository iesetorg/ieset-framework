# Steelman — China zero-COVID demand collapse & incomplete recovery 2022-2023

## The strongest opposing argument

Attributing China's 2022-2023 weakness primarily to zero-COVID + reopening response misreads the deeper story: a multi-year property-sector deleveraging crisis whose timing happens to overlap COVID, plus a structural growth-model exhaustion (debt-financed investment + export-suppressed consumption) that no NPI regime change could mask.

**Key opposing objections:**

1. **The property-debt unwind is the real story.** Evergrande default 2021-12, Country Garden 2023-08, Vanke 2024 — China was undergoing the inevitable end of the 2008-2020 property/credit super-cycle that contributed ~25% of GDP at peak. New-home sales declined 35-50% 2021-2024 from pre-2021 peak; this alone explains most consumption and youth-unemployment weakness. Zero-COVID is a 2-year overlay on a decade-long structural unwind. Disentangling them is hard, but the property channel quantitatively dominates.

2. **Pent-up-demand was always overstated.** The "China will V-shape on reopening" narrative was a Western projection of post-COVID-US consumer behaviour onto a different consumption regime. Chinese household-savings buffer was smaller than headline (concentrated in top quintile), social-safety-net thinner, and confidence weakened by property losses. The "incomplete recovery" finding may actually reflect that the V-shape was never the right counterfactual.

3. **The 2022 stringency data overstates lockdown intensity.** Oxford OxCGRT scoring averaged "high" because zero-COVID maintained perimeter restrictions and contact-tracing year-round, but actual mobility data (Apple, Baidu) shows most cities operated near-normally between flare-up clusters. National-aggregate stringency masks bimodal city-level pattern.

4. **The data-quality concern cuts both ways.** If NBS smoothed 2022-2023 data, the actual demand collapse may have been worse — but so may the recovery. Building a multi-metric test on smoothed sources risks false support and false refutation simultaneously.

## What the framework still captures

Even granting the property-unwind dominates, China's zero-COVID + abrupt reopening is the largest demand-side natural experiment of the 2020s, and the cross-source pattern (PMI sub-50, youth unemployment >20%, NBS suspending its own publication, growth missing IMF projections, stringency genuinely OECD-leading) is too coherent to dismiss. The hypothesis is best read as: zero-COVID and reopening contributed *materially* to 2022-2023 demand weakness, on top of a deeper structural story. The multi-metric design with cross-source independence (IMF, OWID, ILOSTAT, satellite-based PMI) provides robustness against any single source's smoothing. A clean falsification would require all six metrics to come in benign — which would itself be a major prior-update against most macro analysts.
