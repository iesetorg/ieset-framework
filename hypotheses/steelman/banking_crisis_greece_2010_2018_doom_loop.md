# Steelman — Greece 2010-2018 sovereign-banking doom loop

## The strongest opposing argument

Greece is sometimes characterised as a sovereign-debt crisis with banking spillovers rather than a banking crisis with sovereign spillovers. The causal arrow ran primarily from fiscal-statistics revisions in late 2009 (the "Greek statistics scandal") through sovereign-yield blowout to bank-balance-sheet impairment via GGB holdings — not from credit-boom-bust dynamics in the Schularick-Taylor sense. Counting "Greece 2010" as a banking crisis next to Iceland 2008 or Ireland 2008 conflates two distinct mechanisms.

The 25% real-GDP decline is also partly attributable to the Troika fiscal-adjustment programmes (austerity multiplier debate, Blanchard-Leigh 2013). Whether that magnifies or qualifies the banking-crisis severity classification is contested.

## What the framework still captures

The doom-loop framing is the bridge: Greece is a canonical case of how sovereign-debt distress propagates through bank balance sheets in a monetary union without a fiscal backstop. The 6-metric checklist deliberately includes both fiscal (debt > 175%, PSI) and banking (NPL > 40%, capital controls) metrics so the hypothesis is testable as the joint sovereign-banking pattern, not as one or the other in isolation.
