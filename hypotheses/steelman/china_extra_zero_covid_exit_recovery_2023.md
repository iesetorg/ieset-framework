# Steelman — China zero-COVID exit recovery 2023

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Pent-up demand is real but lagged.** Household savings accumulated during 2020-2022 lockdowns were estimated at 7-10 trillion yuan above pre-COVID trend. These should release over 2023-2025, not in a single-quarter spike. Reading 2023H2 softness as "fading" may be too early — the recovery could re-accelerate in 2024-2025 once balance-sheet repair completes.

2. **2017-2019 baseline is the wrong counterfactual.** Pre-COVID China was already decelerating (6.95% 2017 → 5.95% 2019). The IMF April-2023 5.5% forecast was a recovery to that decelerating trend, not to a higher pre-2015 trajectory. Missing 5.5% by 0.5pp is a tighter test than the framework treats it as.

3. **Confounded by exogenous demand.** 2023 weakness in Chinese trade reflects global goods-demand normalisation post-COVID-stimulus, not domestic recovery failure. Disentangling external (global goods bust) from internal (zero-COVID exit failure) requires a peer-economy benchmark that this hypothesis does not formally include.

## What the framework still captures

The descriptive test is honest: it measures growth against the pre-COVID trend. Whether the gap is attributable to zero-COVID exit, property, or external goods demand, the magnitude is what it is. Multi-series cross-check (consumption, exports) gives some sub-channel readout. Attribution is left to follow-on hypotheses; the trajectory test is the unit of analysis here.
