# Steelman — Taiwan SME Competition vs State Planning

## Strongest version of the claim
Taiwan exceptional long-run productivity growth is driven less by top-down state planning (which was present but relatively modest compared to Korea or Japan) and more by a dense ecosystem of small and medium enterprises competing in export markets under hard budget constraints. Export-market discipline forced continuous efficiency improvements, while SME flexibility allowed rapid sectoral switching without the rigidities associated with state-champion heavy industry.

## Key evidence the claim would need
1. Time-series or panel evidence that SME share of employment or output positively predicts subsequent productivity growth, while state-planning intensity does not.
2. Sectoral evidence that Taiwan high-productivity sectors (electronics components, machinery, bicycles) were dominated by SMEs rather than SOEs or state-designated champions.
3. Comparative evidence that Taiwan outperformed comparators with stronger state planning but similar initial income and human capital.

## Best counterarguments
- **ITRI and Hsinchu park:** Taiwan state-funded Industrial Technology Research Institute and the Hsinchu Science Park were critical to the semiconductor and electronics cluster; attributing success to SMEs ignores the public-goods infrastructure that enabled them.
- **Selection bias in coding:** Taiwan state planning may be coded as weak precisely because it was indirect (technology diffusion, land provision, tax holidays) rather than direct (credit allocation, production targets); the coding may understate the state role.
- **SMEs as subcontractors to state champions:** Many Taiwan SMEs were subcontractors to larger export firms or state-supported projects; their success may reflect demand from state-linked champions rather than autonomous competition.
- **Export discipline is still state-mediated:** Taiwan export promotion offices, trade fairs, and technology diffusion were state-funded; export-market discipline was not a purely private mechanism.

## Boundary conditions
- Claim applies most clearly to manufacturing and technology sectors where Taiwan SME ecosystem is densest; services and construction may show different patterns.
- Post-2000 Taiwan saw greater state direction in biotechnology and green energy; the claim may be era-specific.
- SME definition varies across sources (employee thresholds differ); robustness to alternative SME coding is needed.

## Relation to existing hypotheses
Tensions with \`taiwan_itri_frontier_capability_effect\` and \`industrial_policy_developmentalist_states_growth\` if state institutions are found to be primary drivers. Aligns with \`east_asia_export_discipline_vs_domestic_protection\` if export-market discipline is the common factor across East Asian success cases.
