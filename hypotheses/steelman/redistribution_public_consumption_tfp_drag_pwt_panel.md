# Steelman

Public consumption is not only current redistribution. It can include administration, education-adjacent services, health systems, and public order. These may improve human capital, trust, coordination, and diffusion of technology, all of which can raise measured productivity.

The strongest counterprediction is that government consumption has a null or positive association with TFP growth after controlling for capital deepening and institutions. If high-consumption states sustain productivity growth, the fiscal-drag mechanism is not general.
