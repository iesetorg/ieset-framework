# Steelman: Female LFP × growth panel

## Strongest case for the claim

Mechanical: rising female LFP raises labour-input per capita, contributing to per-capita
output. Bloom-Kuhn-Prettner cross-country evidence finds positive associations. The IMF
gender-and-growth literature converges on positive coefficients.

## Strongest case against

Goldin's U-curve predicts non-linear LFP-development relationship; linear specifications
mis-specify. Reverse causation: growth pulls women into LFP rather than LFP causing growth.

## Key uncertainties

- Linear vs U-curve specification.
- Reverse causation under panel FE.

## What would change my mind

If middle-income subsample shows insignificant or wrong-signed coefficient (consistent with
Goldin's U-trough), the linear specification fails and the channel becomes income-dependent.
