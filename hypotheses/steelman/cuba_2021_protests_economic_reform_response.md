# Steelman — Cuba 2021 protests / reform response 2021-2024

**The strongest argument against the descriptive claim:**

The hypothesis is that post-July-2021 Cuban reforms partially responded to protest demands
(MIPYME legalisation, FX adjustment, MLC expansion) but failed to restore real living
standards, with the social cost expressed in unprecedented emigration. The opposing view
is that the reforms have produced a measurable formal-sector expansion that, given enough
time, will yield real-wage and food-supply improvements; that the emigration outflow is
substantially driven by the US Title 42 / CHNV-parole pull rather than Cuban push; and that
the food-import-dependency metric is dominated by world food-price dynamics rather than
Cuban policy.

Specific objections the hypothesis must engage:

1. **MIPYME formal-sector expansion is real and substantial.** The 9,000+ MIPYMEs registered
   2021-2024 represent a structural change in the Cuban economy — the largest formal-private-
   sector expansion since 1959. Treating this as "partial reform without recovery" understates
   the medium-term significance. A pro-reform reading would emphasise that the formal-sector
   expansion is a 5-10 year process and the 2024 metric is mid-cycle.

2. **Real-wage measurement is contaminated by FX-rate distortions.** Cuban real-wage measurement
   depends on which CPI deflator is used (official, MLC-implied, parallel-market). The
   Tarea Ordenamiento nominal-wage adjustments raised baseline state wages 5-10x but the
   relevant comparison is wage / parallel-FX, which collapsed. Different deflator choices
   produce 30-90% real-wage erosion estimates. The hypothesis's threshold (< 60% of 2019)
   is achievable on most deflator choices but is not a clean-measurement result.

3. **Emigration is partly driven by US pull factors.** The Biden CHNV humanitarian-parole
   programme (January 2023-) opened 30,000/month parole slots with disproportionate Cuban
   uptake. Title 42 expulsion exemptions for Cubans (2021-2023) created a unique window.
   Treating the 2022-2024 emigration outflow as a pure Cuban-push signal overstates the
   internal-failure case. A counterfactual of identical Cuban conditions with closed US
   border would have produced different emigration paths.

4. **Food-import share is heavily commodity-cycle dependent.** Cuban food-import dependency is
   structural (~70% pre-2021); the 5-pp rise threshold could be met by a single bad sugar
   harvest or a global food-price spike. The 2022-2023 global food shock (Russia-Ukraine
   wheat, fertiliser disruption) produced food-import dependency rises across Caribbean
   states — a Cuban-specific signal needs comparison to regional comparators (Haiti, DR,
   Jamaica) which the hypothesis does not include.

5. **Author's high prior is acknowledged but is a framework-validation risk.** The 0.75
   prior framing acknowledges that this is partly a sanity-check on the framework's ability
   to detect well-documented patterns. A "supported" verdict here is informative but does
   not update much beyond the consensus narrative.

**How this steelman should shape the result card:**

The v1 result card must:

(a) Report MIPYME formal-sector expansion alongside its 2019-2020 baseline (zero) so the
    structural-change magnitude is clear, while avoiding overstatement of medium-term
    welfare effects (formal-sector expansion is a productivity-channel investment, not a
    consumption recovery).

(b) Report real-wage erosion on multiple deflator choices (official CPI, MLC-implied,
    parallel-FX-implied) with explicit ranges, not a single number.

(c) Decompose emigration outflow into US-pull (CHNV parole, Title 42 dynamics) and
    Cuban-push (collapse of consumer-goods supply, electricity rationing, MLC stratification)
    components where possible. Source-comparison with Haitian and Venezuelan parallel
    outflows over the same window grounds the Cuba-specific signal.

(d) Compare Cuban food-import dependency rise to Caribbean regional benchmarks (CARICOM
    average) so the global-commodity-cycle component is visible.

(e) Accept the verdict will likely be "supported on 4 of 4 metrics" but flag explicitly
    that the strong prior makes this case a framework-validation check rather than a
    high-information-content empirical update.
