# Steelman — Japan Abenomics retrospective 2013-2023

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **GDP-pc growth understates the win.** Japan's working-age population shrank ~9% over 2013-2023. GDP-per-worker (rather than GDP-pc) grew comparably to peers when adjusted. Reading "no growth acceleration" as failure ignores the demographic headwind.

2. **2020-2022 COVID drag confounds.** The full Abenomics retrospective should include 2020-2022, but the framework excludes them as "COVID distortion." Including them might either help (no Japan-specific COVID failure) or hurt (post-2022 inflation surge tests the inflation channel).

3. **Third arrow takes longer.** Structural reforms (corporate governance, women's labour-force participation, foreign-worker visa expansion) build slowly. A 7-year window may be too short.

## What the framework still captures

The mixed-pattern structure is the steelman: it grants the inflation and labour wins while withholding the growth-acceleration claim. If a stronger claim ("real-growth acceleration") is correct, the framework is calibrated to detect that (>+1pp/yr threshold). Demographic headwind is acknowledged; a sister hypothesis (demo_japan_ageing_deflation_link) handles the demographic-attribution decomposition.
