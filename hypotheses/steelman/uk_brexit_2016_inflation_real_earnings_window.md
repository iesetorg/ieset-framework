# Steelman - uk_brexit_2016_inflation_real_earnings_window

A strong counterargument is that the UK had low inflation before the vote and rising nominal pay afterward, so the referendum may explain prices but not a clean real-earnings decline in the narrow ONS window.

The event-window verdict is descriptive and should not be read as a full structural model. Concurrent policy changes, global shocks, and measurement revisions can all weaken causal attribution.
