# Steelman — Monetary finance at the ZLB is non-inflationary

**The strongest argument against this hypothesis:**

The 2021-2024 inflation episode is the natural rival reading: even if 2008-2014 monetary expansion did not generate CPI inflation, the cumulative effect of 2008-2021 monetary-fiscal expansion plausibly did, with the lag explained by anchored expectations finally dislodging once the COVID supply shock fired the cylinder. Excluding 2022-2024 from the primary test is the central methodological move and the central vulnerability.

Specific objections the hypothesis must engage with:

1. **The slack-regime restriction is a get-out-of-jail card.** The hypothesis defines slack as short-rate ≤ 0.50% AND unemployment > NAIRU + 0.5pp. By construction, this excludes the periods in which the Fed/BoJ/ECB were tightening (2015-2019) and the 2022+ inflation episode. The functional-finance claim is then tested only on the windows in which the rival mainstream story also predicts low inflation (slack + ZLB = textbook deflationary forces dominant). Both schools can claim victory on the slack window; the contested case is the post-slack period, which the test excludes.

2. **The 2022-2024 inflation is partly endogenous to the prior expansion.** The supply-shock decomposition (energy, food, supply chains) does not exhaust the 2022-2024 episode. Core CPI ran at 5-7% across USA/EUR for 18+ months, durable-goods inflation tracked the 2020-2021 fiscal transfers, and rents and services inflation reflected broad demand strength built up by the prior expansion. Reading 2022-2024 as purely supply-side is a strong assumption that the BIS/Fed staff decompositions do not unanimously support.

3. **Inflation expectations did move in the late sample.** The 5y5y forward measure rose meaningfully in 2021-2022 before central banks responded, indicating that the anchored-expectations regime is not a permanent feature. If the anchored regime is itself an outcome of credible inflation-targeting central banks, then the slack-regime non-inflation finding documents the operation of inflation-targeting, not the harmlessness of monetary finance.

4. **Counterfactual unobserved.** What the test cannot show is what would have happened had the monetary-fiscal expansion been larger by, say, a factor of 2. The functional-finance claim is that the inflation constraint binds when the economy reaches full employment; the test cannot speak to the full-employment counterfactual because the actual stimulus was sized to be smaller than the output gap. A larger stimulus might have produced inflation; the spec cannot say.

5. **Cross-country generalisation.** Only USA, JPN, and Eurozone are in the sample because only there did monetary finance happen at scale. Emerging-market currency-issuers attempting the same in 2020-2024 (Turkey, Argentina, post-2022 Egypt) experienced inflation outcomes consistent with the textbook prediction. The MMT/PK schools have responses (currency-user vs issuer distinction; this is the topic of the companion currency_user_vs_issuer_hyperinflation_classification spec), but the hypothesis as stated does not engage them.

**How this steelman should shape the result card:**

The result card must:

(a) Report 2008-2014 as primary (cleanest slack regime); 2020-2021 as secondary; 2022-2024 as INFORMATIVE-context only.

(b) Decompose the 2022+ inflation contribution between supply-side and demand-side using the BIS/IMF/Fed staff frameworks; report the demand-side share as a robustness check.

(c) State that the test does NOT speak to whether monetary finance would be inflationary at full employment.

(d) State explicitly that the hypothesis applies only to currency-issuers (USA, JPN, Eurozone). Currency-user cases are tested in the companion spec.

(e) Note the inflation-expectations channel: 5y5y forward rose ahead of realised CPI in 2021. The non-inflation finding for 2008-2014 is consistent with anchored expectations; the 2022 episode shows that anchoring is not unconditional.
