# Steelman: Korea fertility cliff 2015-2024

## Strongest case for the claim

Korea's TFR collapse from 1.24 (2015) to ~0.7 (2023) is the most dramatic peacetime
fertility decline ever recorded. UN-DESA, OECD, and Korean Statistics Office data converge
on the magnitude. The cohort effects implied by sub-1.0 TFR for a decade are arithmetic and
will deliver working-age contraction faster than any prior demographic transition.

## Strongest case against

This spec is largely descriptive; the substantive risk is that the cliff partly reflects
delayed fertility (tempo effect) rather than completed-cohort fertility decline. If 30+
year-old fertility recovers, completed-cohort numbers will be less catastrophic than
period-TFR suggests.

## Key uncertainties

- Tempo vs quantum decomposition.
- Whether other late-marriage East Asian societies (TWN, SGP, HKG) follow Korea down.

## What would change my mind

A cohort-fertility analysis showing completed-cohort TFR for Korean women born 1985-1990
above 1.2 would substantially weaken the cliff narrative, even though period TFR fell sharply.
