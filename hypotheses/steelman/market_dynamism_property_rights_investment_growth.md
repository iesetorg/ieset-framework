# Steelman - market_dynamism_property_rights_investment_growth

Claim tested: Higher security of property rights predicts higher investment shares and faster subsequent real GDP per-capita growth.

The strongest skeptical reading is that property-rights scores may proxy general state capacity, income level, colonial/legal history, political stability, or investor optimism rather than a separable market mechanism. Marxist or developmental-state accounts could accept secure use rights while arguing that public ownership, land reform, or planned investment caused the same growth outcomes. A null or negative coefficient after country and year fixed effects would therefore be meaningful evidence against treating property rights as a standalone long-run growth driver.
