# Steelman — Ethiopia Tigray war 2020-2022 economic collapse

**Strongest arguments against the framework's framing:**

1. **Pre-war growth was already decelerating; the war is being credited with too much of the slowdown.** Ethiopia's real GDP growth fell from 10%+ in mid-2010s to 6% by 2019 due to FX shortages, weak industrial-park performance, and political turbulence after Abiy's 2018 ascension. Attributing the 2020-2022 slowdown entirely to the war over-credits the war shock. A clean test must establish the pre-war counterfactual was upward-sloping or stable, not already declining.

2. **COVID is the dominant 2020 shock for SSA peer economies.** Tanzania denied COVID; Kenya's tourism collapsed; Rwanda's services-led economy contracted sharply. The synthetic-control donor pool was hit by COVID at the same time Ethiopia was hit by COVID + war. Disentangling war from COVID effects on the Ethiopia trajectory requires assumptions the data cannot fully support.

3. **Ethiopian official statistics during 2020-2022 are partial and politicised.** NBE published annual GDP estimates that may have understated the war damage to maintain external-credit access. IMF Article IV missions had limited country access. The "real-GDP gap" measurement uncertainty is large.

4. **Humanitarian aid inflows complicate the BoP measurement.** Ethiopia received substantial emergency assistance from EU, US, multilateral; this stabilised the FX accounts artificially. The official-rate-vs-parallel-rate gap widened sharply (parallel rates 100% premium at peak) but the official rate depreciation is dampened by aid-financed FX availability. The hypothesis's birr-depreciation test using the official rate may understate the true currency damage.

5. **The war's economic damage was geographically concentrated in Tigray, Amhara, Afar.** National-level GDP averages mask regional collapse. A more rigorous test would use sub-national outcomes (night-lights, regional consumption surveys) — which the spec does not currently include but should as a robustness layer.

6. **Pretoria Agreement reconstruction is a confound for the post-war window.** The 2022Q4 cessation produced a sharp rebound in late-2022 / early-2023 indicators. Cumulative-loss measurements through 2023 incorporate the rebound and may understate the war-period damage. The spec should report cumulative loss to 2022Q4 and the post-war rebound separately.

**How this should shape the v1 result card:**

- Distinguish 2020 (COVID dominant) from 2021-2022 (war dominant) windows.
- Report parallel-vs-official birr rate gap as a memo item.
- Acknowledge data-quality limitations in NBE 2020-2022 series.
- Flag night-lights / regional outcomes as a v1.1 robustness layer.
