# Steelman — Nigeria naira redesign 2023 cash crisis

**Strongest arguments against the framework's framing:**

1. **The 2023 election cycle is the dominant political context.** The naira redesign was widely interpreted as politically motivated to constrain vote-buying ahead of the February 25 election. Treating it as a monetary-policy intervention abstracts from the political-economy purpose. The economic effects are downstream of an electoral-engineering action, not a monetary-stability action.

2. **The Supreme Court reversal makes the treatment ambiguous.** The original December-2022 deadline was extended to January-2023 then March-2023, then partially overruled by Supreme Court in March-2023. The treatment is non-binary, time-inconsistent, and partially abandoned. Event-study identification requires a clean treatment date which this episode lacks.

3. **The fuel-subsidy removal three months later is a much larger shock.** Subsequent Q2-Q4 2023 macro indicators are dominated by the subsidy-removal effect. Attributing any post-March 2023 movement to the naira redesign over-attributes. The spec's exclusion of the May-2023 sub-period helps but cannot fully isolate.

4. **The retail/informal-sector damage was primarily distributional, not aggregate.** Cash-dependent traders (markets, transport, rural informal) suffered disproportionately while urban formal-sector workers had digital-payment alternatives. The aggregate-GDP test understates the welfare cost which fell on the informal poor. A more honest framing would emphasise the distributional channel.

5. **NIBSS payment volume was rising structurally before the redesign.** Nigerian fintech (Flutterwave, Paystack, OPay, Palmpay) expanded rapidly 2020-2024 independently of the redesign. The "50% above trend" event-study test must use a properly fitted trend that incorporates the secular fintech adoption growth, or the headline result will be biased upward.

6. **Comparable cross-country event is hard to find.** India's 2016 demonetisation is the natural comparator but that was a much larger shock (86% of currency); the 2017 Eurozone €500-note phaseout was much smaller. No clean cross-country control exists. The single-country event-study is unavoidably weak inferentially.

**How this should shape the v1 result card:**

- Report effect on cash-intensive vs digital-intensive sectors separately if NBS sector data permit.
- Use a properly de-trended NIBSS series.
- Acknowledge the treatment-ambiguity from the Supreme Court reversal.
- Frame as "transitory shock to cash-intensive economy" rather than "monetary policy success/failure".
