# Steelman — Sweden 1990s employment recovery

The strongest counter-reading: Sweden's 1995-2002 employment recovery is essentially a story of the November-1992 currency float (SEK depreciated 30%+ against ECU), the 1995 EU accession trade gain, and the late-1990s tech-sector boom in which Stockholm was a major beneficiary. The labour-market reforms were marginal at the edges and largely preserved the high-tax, high-spending social-democratic model the OECD believed should have produced persistent unemployment. Calmfors and Ferla 2008 argue the post-crisis institutional retention (collective bargaining, generous benefits, ALMP scaling rather than cutting) is what is doing the work, not flexibilisation. The currency-controlled specification is the discipline: if the employment-rate gap collapses from +2pp to under +0.5pp once SEK REER is partialled out, the reform-channel claim fails and the cyclical/exchange-rate channel wins. The spec deliberately includes that control so the failure mode is clean.
