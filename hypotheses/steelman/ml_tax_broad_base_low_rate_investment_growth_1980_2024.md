# Steelman - ml_tax_broad_base_low_rate_investment_growth_1980_2024

Claim tested: countries that shift toward broader tax bases and lower statutory marginal rates achieve higher 10- to 25-year private investment growth without lower total revenue ratios than comparable countries relying on narrow bases and high rates.

The strongest skeptical reading is that this first-pass spec may use a
broad proxy for the underlying policy mechanism, and the measured
relationship may be confounded by income level, state capacity, crisis
timing, or omitted institutional changes. A fair test should therefore
check lag structure, sample composition, and whether the treatment is
measuring the proposed mechanism rather than a correlated development
outcome.

This candidate should be treated as run-triage until a reviewer confirms
the source semantics. A supported first-pass result is not enough for
scoreboard conversion by itself; it needs replication review and a
careful claim-mapping pass.
