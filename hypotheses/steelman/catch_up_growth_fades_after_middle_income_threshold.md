# Steelman

The strongest version of this claim accepts the developmentalist win in poor countries: roads, power, literacy, export platforms, and coordinated investment can generate large early gains. The disputed question is whether that same model keeps working after the economy reaches middle-income complexity. Market liberals argue that frontier growth then depends more on competition, entry, price signals, and institutional constraints than on continued state selection of sectors or firms.

The strongest opposing view is that South Korea, Taiwan, Singapore, and China show that disciplined developmental states can keep upgrading beyond basic catch-up. A fair test must separate failed patronage-based industrial policy from high-capacity export-disciplined developmentalism.
