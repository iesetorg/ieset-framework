# Steelman - market_dynamism_private_credit_productivity_growth

Claim tested: Deeper private credit markets predict faster subsequent total-factor-productivity and real GDP per-capita growth.

The strongest skeptical reading is that private credit can finance asset bubbles, household leverage, or incumbent protection instead of productive experimentation. State-led banks have sometimes funded industrial catch-up, and successful development may create both deeper finance and higher productivity rather than finance causing productivity. The test is strongest only if private credit predicts future TFP after income, investment, and state-spending controls; otherwise it should be treated as weak screening evidence.
