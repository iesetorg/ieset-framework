# Steelman — Vietnam Doi Moi follow-on growth 1995-2024

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Catch-up base effect.** Vietnam in 1995 had GDP-pc-PPP of ~$1,800 (in 2017 USD) — well below most peers. Convergence dynamics alone produce above-peer growth from a low base. The +0.6 log threshold may be reached mechanically without policy contribution.

2. **China FDI spillover.** Much of Vietnam's manufacturing-FDI inflow 2017-2024 reflects firm relocation from China driven by US-China trade tensions, not Vietnam-specific reforms. Crediting Vietnam's policy choices for what is partly a China-displacement effect overstates Vietnamese institutional quality.

3. **State-led, not market-led.** Vietnam remains a single-party state with extensive SOE presence. Reading the success as a market-liberal vindication mischaracterises the actual model — it is closer to the developmental-state pattern (export-oriented industrial policy, state-directed credit, capital controls).

## What the framework still captures

The peer-pool excludes THA/MYS (richer comparators where catch-up wouldn't apply equivalently) and uses LDC-tier peers. The +0.6 log-gap threshold is large enough that catch-up alone is insufficient. Channel decomposition between FDI-attraction, institutional reform, and developmental-state targeting is the subject of separate hypotheses.
