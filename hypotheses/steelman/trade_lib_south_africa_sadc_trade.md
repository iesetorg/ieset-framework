# Steelman — South Africa SADC trade

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Commodity-price dynamics dominate.** ZAF trade ratios are
   driven by gold, platinum, and coal price cycles. The 2003-2014
   commodity boom and subsequent bust contaminate any window-
   based descriptive comparison.

2. **Asymmetric phase-down means small ZAF tariff change.**
   ZAF's tariff schedule reductions under SADC were small (it
   was the asymmetric "developed" partner liberalising more
   slowly); the marginal opening from ZAF's side was modest.

3. **Bilateral composition is the right test.** Aggregate trade-
   share-of-GDP misses the bilateral SADC-share-of-ZAF-trade
   shift, which is the direct treatment outcome.

## What the framework still captures

The descriptive band [-5, +20] pp accommodates both the modest-
effect and null readings while ruling out an unrealistic large
positive. The bilateral-composition critique deserves a separate
spec.
