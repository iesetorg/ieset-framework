# Steelman: Gender pay gap evolution OECD

## Strongest case for the claim

OECD median gender pay gap has indeed narrowed over 1990-2023; descriptive panel data is
consistent with the narrative. The hypothesis tests the magnitude (>5pp reduction) and
the post-2010 slowdown (consistent with parenthood-penalty residual).

## Strongest case against

The "slowdown" may be statistical noise; pay-gap data is volatile by year and country.
Specification choice (full-time only vs all workers) substantially changes the trajectory.

## Key uncertainties

- Specification sensitivity.

## What would change my mind

If post-2010 slope-change is not significant at p<0.10, the slowdown narrative is
unsupported by the data.
