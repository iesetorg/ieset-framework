# Steelman: France pied-noir absorption 1962

## Strongest case for the claim

Hunt (1992) established that the pied-noir inflow caused only modest, transitory
labour-market disruption — small wage and unemployment effects in destination regions, with
convergence within a decade. This is a canonical natural experiment in migration economics.

## Strongest case against

National-level data may not detect what Hunt found at the regional level. The single-country
time series is underpowered; the spec is more useful as a documentation effort than a
discriminating test.

## Key uncertainties

- Pre-1991 unemployment data quality.

## What would change my mind

A substantial unemployment persistence (>3pp rise lasting 10+ years) attributable to
1962 would refute Hunt's finding at the national level — but the data probably can't
support such an inference.
