# Steelman — Ordoliberal anti-cartel and the German economic miracle

## The strongest opposing argument

The Wirtschaftswunder is one of the most over-claimed events in economic history. A serious heterodox reading credits the growth pattern to factors mostly orthogonal to the ordoliberal claim:

1. **Pure reconstruction physics.** A war-ravaged industrial economy with intact human capital, surviving managerial knowledge, and substantial residual physical capital will recover at very high rates regardless of policy regime. The convergence-catch-up rate of growth is a function of the gap to steady-state, not of competition policy.

2. **Marshall Plan and external finance.** US transfers and external capital availability were large and exogenous; they relaxed the constraint that would otherwise have throttled investment. The currency-reform credibility came partly from the implicit US guarantee of the new DM.

3. **Common Market 1958.** The EEC Treaty entering force in 1958 coincides almost exactly with the GWB. Trade liberalisation across what became the Inner Six produced large measurable trade-creation gains (Balassa, Eichengreen) that would have boosted German growth absent any domestic competition reform.

4. **Demographic supply shock.** 12 million expellees from the East provided a large labour-supply shock, depressing wage costs and enabling rapid capital accumulation at high marginal returns. This effect is independent of competition policy.

5. **Retrospective narrative construction.** The "ordoliberal miracle" framing was built by Erhard's political allies (Müller-Armack, Eucken's posthumous editors) into a stylised reading that elides how dirigiste actual German policy was (heavy-industry investment guidance, sectoral subsidies, codetermination 1951-1976). Treating the period as a clean ordoliberal experiment misreads the institutional history.

6. **GWB enforcement was weak in practice.** The Bundeskartellamt was understaffed and the GWB itself contained significant exemptions (export cartels, rationalisation cartels). The actual cartel-prevention bite of the law was modest in the 1958-1965 window the spec studies.

## What the framework still captures

Even granting all of the above, the multi-metric checklist's metric 5 (pre-vs-post-GWB growth ratio) gives the heterodox "pure reconstruction" reading a clean win condition: if growth 1958-1965 collapses to <50% of 1949-1957 pace, the reconstruction-only reading is consistent with the data and the GWB-channel claim is not sustained. The hypothesis is a genuine test, not a celebration. The ordoliberal contribution is identifiable only on the narrow margin where growth persistence beyond reconstruction-catch-up exhaustion is observed; if it isn't, the spec returns refuted.
