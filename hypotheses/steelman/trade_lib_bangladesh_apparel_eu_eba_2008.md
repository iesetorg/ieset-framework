# Steelman — Bangladesh apparel and EBA

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Pre-existing capability mattered more than EBA.** Bangladesh
   apparel exports began their takeoff in the late 1980s under
   the MFA quota system (which actually favoured Bangladesh as a
   non-quota-binding supplier). The post-2005 acceleration partly
   reflects pre-existing capability accumulation, not the EBA
   alone.

2. **Pakistan-as-comparator weakness.** Pakistan's apparel sector
   was constrained by domestic political instability and energy
   shortages, not by EU market access (Pakistan also had GSP+
   access from 2014). The BGD-vs-PAK gap may reflect Pakistan's
   underperformance rather than Bangladesh's EBA-induced gain.

3. **Welfare costs are excluded.** Apparel-sector growth came at
   the cost of severe labour-safety failures (Rana Plaza 2013,
   Tazreen 2012) and wage suppression. The export-share metric is
   silent on welfare.

## What the framework still captures

The descriptive comparison documents a real and large pattern.
The Pakistan-comparator weakness is acknowledged in the spec
(reporting both BGD absolute change and BGD-minus-PAK gap as
distinct thresholds). Welfare is explicitly out of scope — a
follow-on labour-conditions spec is the right place for that.
