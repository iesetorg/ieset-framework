# Steelman: Biden CTC 2021 Child Poverty Effect

The strongest skeptical reading is that this is closer to transfer-accounting than clean causal identification. The Supplemental Poverty Measure counts refundable tax-credit resources directly, so a large fall in SPM child poverty during the expanded Child Tax Credit year partly follows mechanically from the measure's construction. The 2021 window also overlaps with pandemic recovery, SNAP emergency allotments, stimulus payments, and unusually fast labour-market tightening. A sharper causal design would separate the CTC from those simultaneous supports using household eligibility, state variation in take-up, or monthly administrative microdata. This descriptive test is still useful because the same official Census series should show the pre-expansion drop and post-expiration rebound if the measured poverty reduction was real, but it should not be read as isolating the full structural effect of permanent CTC policy.
