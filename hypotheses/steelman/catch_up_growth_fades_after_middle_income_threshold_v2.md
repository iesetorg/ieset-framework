# Steelman — catch-up threshold v2

The strongest counter is that the 40 percent threshold is too coarse. Some
countries above the threshold may still have large sectoral catch-up potential,
while some below it are blocked by conflict, governance, or geography. Maddison
coverage also mixes long historical reconstructions with modern national
accounts, so a small 10-year-window premium could reflect measurement smoothing
rather than a true fade in catch-up dynamics.
