# Steelman — Turkey unorthodox doctrine 2021-2024

## The strongest opposing argument

The "Erdogan was uniquely wrong" narrative obscures genuine theoretical heterodoxy and over-attributes the lira crisis to monetary policy alone, ignoring fiscal-expansionary, geopolitical, and balance-of-payments forces that would have produced FX/CPI stress regardless of TCMB stance.

**Heterodox-and-still-defensible objections:**

1. **Cost-channel transmission is real (Barth-Ramey, Gaiotti-Secchi).** In economies with high pass-through from input costs to consumer prices and large dollar-denominated corporate liabilities, rate hikes can raise marginal costs and FX-balance-sheet distress before they tighten demand — short-run inflation rises before falling. Turkey's 70%+ currency-pass-through coefficient and large external corporate dollar debt make it the textbook case where the cost channel could dominate the demand channel for several quarters. Erdogan's claim wasn't *only* religious; it had thin but non-zero theoretical support.

2. **Fiscal dominance was the real story.** Per Sargent-Wallace and Cochrane FTPL, when fiscal policy is on a non-stationary path (Turkey's pre-election spending, KKM FX-protected deposit scheme implicit liabilities ~10% of GDP), monetary policy alone cannot stabilise prices — inflation is determined by the present value of fiscal surpluses regardless of policy rate. The post-2023 disinflation tracked Simsek's *fiscal* tightening at least as much as the rate hikes; attributing it cleanly to "orthodox monetary reversal" is too tidy.

3. **The 2018-2021 baseline shows orthodox failure too.** Turkey ran 18-24% policy rates 2018-2019 with double-digit inflation; the orthodox stance didn't anchor expectations either. Inflation expectations in Turkey appear weakly tied to the policy rate independent of the "doctrine" — a structural feature of high-pass-through, low-CB-credibility economies, not a unique 2021-2023 disaster.

4. **Counterfactual ambiguity.** Russia's invasion of Ukraine 2022, global energy spike, and EM tightening cycle all hit Turkey simultaneously with the unorthodox cuts. The lira would have weakened materially regardless; the question is by how much, and a clean counterfactual is unavailable.

## What the framework still captures

Even granting all the above, the magnitude is overwhelming: a >60% currency depreciation, 75%+ headline CPI, real policy rate at -50pp, and a clean reversal-and-disinflation signature once orthodox policy returned. No serious cost-channel theory predicts a -50pp real rate is sustainable; no fiscal-dominance theory predicts the post-reversal disinflation pattern is purely fiscal. The case is canonical for orthodox monetary failure precisely because the doctrine was explicit, the rate-cut sequence was deliberate, and the reversal-and-recovery provides a rare clean within-country control. The multi-metric design (5 independent layers) makes the test robust to any single channel's confounding.
