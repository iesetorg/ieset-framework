# Steelman — Pakistan IMF programme cycle 1988-2024

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Security shocks dominate.** Pakistan's 1990s nuclear-test sanctions, 2001-2014 Afghan-war spillover, 2009-2014 internal counter-insurgency operations are exogenous shocks that any economy would struggle to navigate. The IMF cycle is a response, not a cause.

2. **Demographic challenge.** Pakistan's TFR remained high (>4) into the 2000s, producing rapid working-age-population growth without commensurate job creation. The macro outcomes reflect demographic over-burden as much as policy.

3. **Selection bias on programmes.** Countries that repeatedly take IMF programmes are by selection in chronic distress. Comparing PAK to SAARC peers without conditioning on initial conditions overstates the cycle as cause vs symptom.

## What the framework still captures

The hypothesis is non-causal — it tests whether the chronic-stabilisation pattern produced peer-relative outperformance (the IMF reform-and-recovery story) or peer-relative stagnation (the chronic-cycle story). Either reading is consistent with security-shock confounds, but the differential test still discriminates between "IMF programmes worked" and "IMF programmes propped up a chronically-distressed economy without convergence."
