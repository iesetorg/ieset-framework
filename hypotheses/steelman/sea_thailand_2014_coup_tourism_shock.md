# Steelman — Thailand 2014 coup + tourism dependence shock

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Middle-income trap, not coup.** Thailand reached upper-middle-income in the 2010s; growth deceleration is consistent with the textbook middle-income-trap pattern (rising labour costs, no frontier-tech transition). Attributing the slowdown to the coup confounds with this structural deceleration.

2. **Tourism-dependence is a benefit in normal times.** Pre-2020 Thailand benefited from rising Chinese outbound tourism; the same dependence that hurt in 2020 helped in 2010-2019. Cherrypicking the COVID year overstates the cost.

3. **Demographic ageing.** Thailand's TFR fell below replacement in the 1990s; working-age population started declining in the late 2010s. The growth slowdown has a demographic component independent of the political shock.

## What the framework still captures

The pre-COVID 2014-2019 differential test isolates the political-institutional channel from COVID. The 2020 contraction test isolates the tourism-amplification channel. Both being separately falsifiable lets the framework attribute correctly. Demographic confound is acknowledged; THA-specific demographic test is reserved for a follow-on hypothesis.
