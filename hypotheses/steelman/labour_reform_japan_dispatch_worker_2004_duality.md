# Steelman — Japan dispatch-worker 2004 duality

The strongest counter-reading: Japan's non-regular-worker share rise predates 2004 and reflects the entire post-1995 post-bubble adjustment, so attributing the 2004-2008 increase to the manufacturing dispatch liberalisation specifically requires a difference-in-differences identification on the manufacturing/non-manufacturing margin that the spec cannot perform with country-level data. Genda-Kondo-Ohta 2010 show non-regular share rose continuously from 1990 onward, with no clear 2004 inflection point in the aggregate series, weakening the event-study identification. The spec's defence is that the synthetic-control gap controls for the underlying trend by donor-weighting on pre-2004 outcome paths, but the donor pool of high-income peers is unlikely to share Japan's specific demographic and institutional dynamics. The dispositive sharpening: a v2 should use the manufacturing-vs-services within-Japan event study, exploiting that manufacturing was the new-treatment sector in 2004, with services as control. This is the cleaner identification and should be the v2 follow-up.
