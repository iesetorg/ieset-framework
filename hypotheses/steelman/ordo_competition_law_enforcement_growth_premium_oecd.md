# Steelman — Ordoliberal competition-law enforcement and productivity premium

## The strongest opposing argument

Heterodox readers (post-Keynesian, evolutionary, Galbraithian) will resist this hypothesis on several fronts:

1. **PMR is not a Eucken proxy.** The OECD Product Market Regulation index aggregates state-control of enterprises, barriers to entry, and trade/investment openness. Two of those three components (entry barriers, trade openness) reflect classical-liberal deregulation rather than rule-bound cartel enforcement. A country that abolishes its competition authority but also liberalises foreign entry can score "better" on PMR than a country with vigorous DG-COMP-style merger blocking and modest entry liberalisation. Reading PMR as Wettbewerbsordnung enforcement smuggles a libertarian deregulation premium into a Freiburg-school spec.

2. **Productivity premium runs through scale economies, not competition.** Galbraith's "New Industrial State" and the evolutionary literature (Nelson, Winter) argue that productivity growth in modern economies comes from large R&D-intensive firms with market power, not from atomised competition. The European-vs-US productivity-growth divergence post-2000 is consistent with that reading: the US has weaker enforcement but stronger productivity growth, partly because its tech giants are not broken up.

3. **Rule of law confound.** Countries with strong competition-law enforcement also have strong general institutional quality. The PMR coefficient may identify off the rule-of-law factor, not competition policy specifically. The hypothesis explicitly gates on this; if the RL-attenuation collapses the coefficient, that is a valid heterodox win.

4. **Endogeneity.** High-productivity sectors lobby for competition-friendly regulation; the causality may run productivity → enforcement, not enforcement → productivity.

## What the framework still captures

Even granting these objections, the cross-country panel-FE design with the RL-attenuation gate gives the heterodox reading a clean way to win: if competition-rule enforcement is "really" just rule of law, the coefficient will collapse and the hypothesis is refuted. The Eucken claim is testable as a distinct channel, not an article of faith, and the spec deliberately makes it falsifiable.
