# Steelman: France RSA + Prime d'Activité Employment Effect

The strongest counter is that France's labour-market trajectory 2009-2022 is dominated by macro shocks (GFC, eurozone debt crisis 2011-2012, gilets-jaunes 2018, COVID 2020) that swamp any transfer-design effect. RSA's "activité" component had documented take-up problems — IGAS 2011 found roughly 35% take-up among eligibles — and Prime d'Activité was designed in part to address this rather than as an additional incentive layer. The synthetic-control donor pool (BEL, ITA, ESP, PRT) experienced their own crises (Spanish unemployment near 26% in 2013, Italian double-dip) so the comparator weighting is unstable. Bargain-Carcillo (2014) argues that RSA's marginal-incentive effect is small and concentrated on lone parents, with most of the in-work-poverty reduction attributable to minimum-wage indexation rather than the activity premium. A more defensible reading: RSA/PA simplified administration and improved take-up at the margin, but the 2-4pp employment claim is well outside the precision of the available identification.
