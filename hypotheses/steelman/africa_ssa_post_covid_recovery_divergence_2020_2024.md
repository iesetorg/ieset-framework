# Steelman — SSA post-COVID recovery divergence 2020-2024

**Strongest arguments against the framework's framing:**

1. **Country-specific shocks may dominate structural variables.** The post-2020 SSA window includes: Tigray war (2020-2022), three Sahel coups (Mali 2020/2021, Burkina 2022, Niger 2023), Sudan civil war (2023+), Mozambique LNG security collapse, Zambia debt default, Ghana DDEP. These are country-specific institutional shocks. The "structural-variables explain 35%" framing may be defeated by shock heterogeneity.

2. **Tourism is over-weighted as a recovery driver in the early framing.** Mauritius, Seychelles, Cabo Verde recovered faster than commodity-dependent peers, which the framework attributes to tourism rebound. But the actual driver may be small-economy fiscal-buffer flexibility (sovereign wealth funds, lower debt, faster vaccine access). Disentangling tourism-recovery-as-cause from fiscal-buffer-as-cause requires careful controls.

3. **The IMF's RST and SDR allocation provided differential support.** Countries that secured IMF financing (Ghana, Senegal, Côte d'Ivoire, Kenya) had access to substantial concessional financing that countries outside programmes did not. The "recovery divergence" story may be partly an "IMF-programme access divergence" story.

4. **Vaccine access and lockdown stringency varied widely.** Mauritius achieved high vaccination by mid-2021; Nigeria stagnated below 30% through 2024. Lockdown stringency (South Africa strict, Tanzania denialist) shaped the 2020 baseline shock. Pre-COVID structural variables don't capture these COVID-policy heterogeneities.

5. **Commodity-cycle volatility 2022-2024 is a separate post-shock trajectory.** The 2022 Russia-Ukraine commodity-price spike helped oil exporters and hurt food importers; 2023-2024 commodity-price stabilisation reversed some effects. Coding the entire 2020-2024 window as "recovery from COVID" mis-specifies — there were two distinct shocks.

6. **The 35% R-squared threshold is essentially arbitrary.** With 30+ countries and 5 years, the model has many degrees of freedom; the threshold is achievable mechanically with overfit. A more rigorous test would use cross-validation or hold out a SSA sub-region.

**How this should shape the v1 result card:**

- Report shock-affected vs non-shock-affected sub-pools separately.
- Distinguish "fiscal-buffer" from "tourism-rebound" channels.
- Report IMF-programme access as an additional explanatory variable.
- Acknowledge the descriptive (associational) rather than causal nature of the claim.
- Frame the variance-explained as a stylised-facts summary, not a causal partition.
