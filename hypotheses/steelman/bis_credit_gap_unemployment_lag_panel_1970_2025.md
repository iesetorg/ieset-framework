# Steelman - bis_credit_gap_unemployment_lag_panel_1970_2025

The strongest version of the claim is: High BIS credit-gap episodes predict unemployment increases over the following two years.

The skeptical case is that BIS macro-financial variables may proxy for omitted local policy, banking structure, terms-of-trade shocks, or measurement changes. A fair test therefore needs broad country coverage, fixed effects, transparent thresholds, and a result card that does not treat predictive association as causality.
