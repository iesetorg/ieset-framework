# Steelman — Nuclear revival 2023-2026 Western construction starts

**The strongest arguments against the framework's "rhetoric vs concrete pour" framing:**

1. **The 2022-2026 window is too short for nuclear-construction signals.** Large-reactor projects from policy commitment to concrete pour take 5-10 years (siting, licensing, FOAK financing structure negotiation, supply-chain mobilisation). France's loi accélération du nucléaire 2023 cannot reasonably deliver concrete pour on EPR2 before 2027-2028. UK Sizewell C had FID 2024; main civils 2025-2026. Coding the absence of concrete-pour by end-2026 as "revival is rhetoric" mistakes a window-too-short for an outcome-too-weak. The honest test horizon is 2030.

2. **Lifetime extensions and phase-out reversals are the more-rapid revival channel and may be the binding climate-relevant lever.** Existing nuclear capacity in OECD is ~280 GWe; lifetime extensions of 60-80 years (US NRC SLR licences, French ASN visite décennale, Japan NRA 60+) preserve more low-carbon capacity than any plausible new-build addition by 2030. The "revival" that matters operationally happened in extension-of-existing rather than new-build. Treating lifetime extension as one metric of five undercounts its actual share of revival-relevant capacity.

3. **Vogtle-4 is end-of-streak, not start-of-revival.** Vogtle 3+4 finished a decade late, 2.5x over budget, with Westinghouse bankruptcy along the way. It is the LAST-of-a-kind for the AP1000 in the US, not the first-of-a-revival. Crediting it as a revival data-point misreads its causal role; it is a legacy completion, not a forward signal. The honest revival metric should exclude Vogtle.

4. **SMR cancellations (NuScale CFPP Nov 2023) reflect cost discipline, not failure.** The NuScale Carbon-Free Power Project was cancelled because its forecast cost hit $89/MWh by 2023, exceeding the off-take ceiling municipal customers would tolerate. This is a market-discipline outcome (project that did not pencil out was cancelled), not evidence of revival failure. The right SMR revival test is whether DIFFERENT projects (BWRX-300 Darlington, X-energy Dow Seadrift, KSMR Cernavoda) reach concrete pour, not whether NuScale survives.

5. **Phase-out reversals are politically real and often-irreversible.** Belgium's 2023 lifetime extension (Doel-4, Tihange-3 to 2035), Sweden's 2023 abandonment of phase-out target, Netherlands' 2024 new-build commitment, Italy's 2024 lifting of the 1987 ban, and Switzerland's 2024 lifting of the 2017 ban together represent a substantive policy revival distinct from concrete pour. Coding revival as physical construction misses the institutional precondition that has to come first; phase-out reversal is the signal that revival is real, not the absence of construction in 24 months.

6. **Construction starts are not the right outcome — final investment decisions (FIDs) are.** Once a reactor reaches FID with financing structure complete, construction follows on a deterministic 4-7 year timeline. The 2023-2026 measurable revival outcome is FID count, not concrete-pour count. Sizewell C 2024, Bruce C announced 2024, OPG Darlington B (BWRX-300) FID 2024, and a number of EPR2 financing decisions in process are FIDs that the hypothesis's "construction start" metric undercounts.

**How this should shape the v1 result card when run:**

- Distinguish FID count (forward-looking) from construction-start count (concrete pour). Both are revival metrics; they live on different timelines.
- Treat lifetime extensions as a primary revival channel, not a secondary metric.
- Exclude Vogtle-4 from the post-2022 revival count — it is a pre-2022 commitment finishing late.
- Treat NuScale CFPP cancellation as cost-discipline outcome, not revival failure; check non-NuScale SMR projects separately.
- Report phase-out reversals as institutional revival signal independent of construction.
- Commit to v2.0 update at 2030 for the construction-start test; the 2026 reading is an interim.
- Discuss climate-cost-of-CO2-abated comparison vs renewables as out-of-frame; this hypothesis tests the supply pivot, not the climate optimisation.
