# Steelman: China one-child policy → long-run growth drag

## Strongest case for the claim

China's TFR fell faster than would have occurred under voluntary fertility transition,
producing an artificially compressed working-age bulge that peaked around 2010-2015. Post
2015 the working-age population began to contract, and per-capita growth decelerated even
as catch-up convergence opportunities remained. Coercive policy added measurable distortion
on top of the natural transition, and the synthetic-control donor pool of comparable
middle-income Asian states allows rough quantification.

## Strongest case against

(1) China's fertility had already begun falling sharply pre-1980; one-child policy may have
locked in trends that were happening anyway. (2) Comparable middle-income East-Asian states
(KOR, TWN, SGP) experienced even steeper voluntary fertility transitions, suggesting OCP's
marginal effect on TFR was modest. (3) Donor pool selection is contentious; including India
biases toward large policy effect, while including Korea biases toward small.

## Key uncertainties

- Marginal contribution of OCP vs voluntary transition.
- Donor-pool composition sensitivity.

## What would change my mind

A donor pool excluding India and including KOR/TWN/SGP yielding zero working-age-share gap
would refute the OCP-specific causal claim, leaving only the broader fertility-transition
effect.
