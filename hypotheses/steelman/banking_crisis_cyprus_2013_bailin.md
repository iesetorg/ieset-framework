# Steelman — Cyprus 2013 bail-in

## The strongest opposing argument

Cyprus 2013 was small (GDP ~EUR 18bn) and the precipitating shock was largely external (Greek sovereign restructuring impairing Cypriot bank holdings of GGBs, plus Russian-deposit flight-risk politics). Treating it as a canonical bail-in case obscures that the resolution method was driven by political constraints (the Eurogroup's reluctance to bail out a banking system seen as a Russian-money offshore hub) rather than by a clean policy choice between bail-in and bail-out.

The 100+ pp credit-to-GDP decline metric is partly mechanical: deposit flight + asset write-downs + a shrinking GDP denominator combine to produce a large change number that overstates the underlying economic event in an economy of Cyprus's size.

## What the framework still captures

The bail-in event itself is binary and uniquely identifying — Cyprus 2013 is the first eurozone case of imposed losses on uninsured depositors at a systemic bank, and the precedent shaped the BRRD design. The 4-of-5 metric pattern tests whether the headline canonical signature (output decline, unemployment rise, programme entry) accompanied the unique resolution event.
