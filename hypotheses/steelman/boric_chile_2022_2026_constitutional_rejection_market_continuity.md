# Steelman — Boric / Chile constitutional rejection + market continuity 2022-2026

**The strongest argument against the causal claim:**

The hypothesis is that Chile's voter rejection of both constitutional alternatives preserved
the 1980 framework and that macro stability tracks this institutional continuity rather than
the Boric-administration political signal. The opposing view is that institutional continuity
is illusory — the 2019 social uprising and the 2022-2023 constitutional process both
revealed that the 1980 framework has lost legitimacy, and that the 2024-2026 macro outcomes
are only superficially stable while underlying political-economy fragility builds.

Specific objections the hypothesis must engage:

1. **2019 social uprising signal is unresolved.** The October 2019 estallido social showed
   that the 1980 framework's perceived legitimacy is contested. The 2022-2023 double-rejection
   does not resolve the underlying social demand for change; it only blocks two specific
   alternative texts. A "stable macro" verdict in 2024-2026 may mask growing political-
   economy fragility that surfaces in the 2025 election or beyond. The hypothesis's
   measurement window may end before the next inflection.

2. **Boric administration policy moves are real and consequential.** Even without a new
   constitution, Boric implemented a tax reform attempt (2022, defeated), a pension reform
   (2024, partially passed with capitalisation-pillar preserved), and a 40-hour-week labour
   reform. Treating "constitutional rejection" as the only treatment ignores the
   below-the-constitutional-level policy package which is meaningful and partly successful.
   A clean test of constitutional-continuity-versus-policy-continuity is hard to design
   because both vary contemporaneously.

3. **Copper-cycle dominance.** Chile's macro outcomes are heavily driven by the copper
   price. 2022-2024 copper prices were elevated (transition-metal demand, supply constraints).
   A "stable macro" verdict may reflect favourable terms-of-trade rather than institutional
   continuity. The control for commodity_terms_of_trade is essential and the result is
   sensitive to its specification.

4. **Synthetic counterfactual specification matters.** Comparing Chile to Colombia/Brazil/Peru/
   Mexico assumes those are good comparators, but Chile is structurally different (higher
   per-capita income, more open capital account, deeper financial markets). A "non-negative
   CATT" verdict could simply reflect Chile's structural-advantage gap rather than a
   constitutional-continuity treatment effect. Donor-pool weighting matters.

5. **Direction-asymmetric threshold may bias toward support.** The author's pre-registered
   threshold ("non-negative CATT" as support) is asymmetric: it counts "no worse" as
   support. This is theoretically defensible (the hypothesis is about preservation, not
   improvement) but it does set the bar low. A symmetric test would require some positive
   divergence, not just non-deterioration. The result-card should report both versions.

**How this steelman should shape the result card:**

The v1 result card must:

(a) Decompose the Chilean macro outcomes into copper-cycle component, US-rate-cycle component,
    and idiosyncratic component. A "supported" verdict on idiosyncratic component would be
    much stronger evidence than a "supported" verdict on raw outcomes.

(b) Report the result with multiple donor-pool specifications: standard LatAm 4, Pacific
    Alliance only (PER, COL, MEX), Pacific-Alliance+Brazil, etc. Sensitivity to donor pool
    is informative.

(c) Distinguish the constitutional-rejection treatment from the Boric-policy-package treatment
    by exploiting the timing differences (rejection 2022-09 and 2023-12; policy reforms
    2024-2026). Event-study windows around each event give cleaner identification than the
    single post-2022Q3 indicator.

(d) Track political-economy fragility indicators (V-Dem electoral-democracy, Freedom House,
    public-opinion polls if accessible) alongside macro outcomes so that the "stable macro,
    fragile politics" possibility is observable.

(e) Accept that the 2025 Chilean presidential election outcome is the natural endpoint. A
    market-liberal successor to Boric would make this a "clear support" case; a further-left
    shift would prompt a v2 spec testing whether the 2022-2023 rejection-window stability
    was a temporary equilibrium.
