# Steelman — Mexico 2019 outsourcing reform

The strongest counter-reading: Mexico's formal-employment growth 2019-2023 is dominated by the nearshoring/USMCA-enforcement effect on northern manufacturing FDI inflows, which would have produced formalisation gains regardless of the domestic labour-reform changes. The outsourcing prohibition's main effect was reclassification (subcontracted workers moved onto the principal-firm payroll) rather than new employment creation, so the formal-share rise is partly an accounting shift. Bensusán and Middlebrook 2022 and OIT-México evaluations show subcontract-firm closures spiked in 2021 as expected, confirming the reclassification channel. The state-level concentration test is the discipline: if the gain is uniformly distributed rather than concentrated in northern manufacturing states, the nearshoring story fails — but if it is concentrated, the labour-reform-channel claim is also weakened because nearshoring would predict the same pattern. A clean refutation pathway needs an event-study on USMCA-enforcement actions specifically (rapid-response complaints filed) rather than the labour-reform date.
