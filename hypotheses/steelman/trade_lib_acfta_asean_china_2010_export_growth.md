# Steelman — ASEAN-China FTA 2010

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Supply-chain-integration vulnerability.** The export gains
   came at the cost of dependency on Chinese final-assembly
   demand cycles. When the US-China trade war disrupted those
   chains from 2018, ASEAN exporters faced disproportionate
   adjustment costs. The static-gain measurement misses this
   dependency-creation channel.

2. **Heterogeneous within-ASEAN effects.** Vietnam, Indonesia,
   Malaysia gained substantially; the Philippines and Singapore
   gained less. The aggregate ATT masks this heterogeneity.

3. **Confound with prior bilateral and multilateral
   liberalisation.** The 2010 effective date is preceded by a
   decade of bilateral tariff phase-outs and ASEAN-internal
   integration. Pre-trends are strong; identification rests on
   the inflection of the trend at 2010.

## What the framework still captures

The merchandise-exports outcome is the right first-order test.
The robustness check dropping Singapore handles the financial-
centre outlier. Heterogeneous effects are a feature of the
Callaway-Sant'Anna estimator output (cohort-specific ATTs), not
a bug, and a follow-on spec can disaggregate by country.
