# Steelman — Korea chaebol reform attempts 2017-2024

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Concentration is not WGI Regulatory Quality.** WGI measures perceived business climate, not industrial concentration. Chaebol reform that shifts intra-group governance without changing aggregate concentration could leave WGI unchanged. The test is mismatched to the claim.

2. **Cumulative pressure has effects.** Samsung's 2017 Lee Jae-yong arrest, multiple chaebol leadership convictions, and ESG pressure from foreign investors have shifted chaebol behaviour at the margin. Cross-shareholding unwinding has accelerated.

3. **Yoon administration partial rollback.** The 2022 Yoon administration partly reversed Moon-era policies. Measuring through 2022 catches the Moon era; the post-2022 rollback should not be expected to add reform momentum.

## What the framework still captures

The non-acceleration test is honest about the limits of the WGI proxy. If chaebol reform DID improve regulatory quality measurably, WGI percentile would move (Korea has room — currently ~80th percentile globally, not at the ceiling). The test is biased toward finding "no major improvement" but the +10pp refutation threshold is reachable for a genuine reform success.
