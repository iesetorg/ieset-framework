# Steelman: SSA demographic-dividend window

## Strongest case for the claim

SSA fertility transition is well underway in many countries; working-age shares are rising
2000-2020 and projected to continue. East Asian experience suggests substantial dividend is
available conditional on institutional capacity. Cross-country variation in governance
quality should produce a clear interaction effect with working-age share.

## Strongest case against

SSA growth has been uneven, with commodity-boom-dependent economies (NGA, AGO) volatile
and governance scores correlated with development levels — making causal interpretation
hard. Female LFP and youth-employment outcomes vary widely; working-age share alone may
not translate to dividend without education and employment-creation capacity.

## Key uncertainties

- Governance-effectiveness measurement quality.
- Female LFP as separate moderator.

## What would change my mind

If high-governance SSA countries also fail to translate working-age gains to per-capita
growth, the institutional-moderation thesis fails and a more demanding precondition story
is needed.
