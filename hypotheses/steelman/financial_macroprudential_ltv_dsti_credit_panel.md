# Steelman — Macroprudential LTV/DSTI panel

## The strongest opposing argument

LTV / DSTI introduction dates are endogenous to the credit cycle: regulators introduce them when credit growth is elevated and house prices are rising. The post-introduction "deceleration" is therefore confounded with cycle reversion that would have happened without intervention. CS-DiD with country FE absorbs some but not all of this; truly random-assignment evidence is impossible at the country level.

The macroprudential tools also frequently arrive in bundles (LTV + DSTI + countercyclical buffer + limits on FX-denominated lending), so isolating any single tool's effect is hard.

## What the framework still captures

The pre-registered ATT thresholds (-1.5 pp/year on household credit, -1.0 pp/year on house prices, both at h=2) are sized for plausible effect magnitudes from the IMF iMaPP literature. The CS-DiD design is the best-available staggered-treatment estimator that does not rely on shared parallel trends after staggered adoption.
