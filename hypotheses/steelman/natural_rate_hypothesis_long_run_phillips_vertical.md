# Steelman - Cross-country natural-rate / long-run Phillips VECM

The strongest objection is that a country-level Johansen screen on headline CPI inflation and unemployment may overstate evidence for a stable long-run Phillips relation. CPI and unemployment are both highly persistent macro series, so cointegration tests can reject rank zero because of shared regime shifts, oil shocks, exchange-rate pass-through, or monetary-policy reaction functions rather than a structural exploitable unemployment-inflation tradeoff.

The opposing post-Keynesian and hysteresis reading is also serious: unemployment is not independent of demand history. Long downturns can reduce labour-force attachment and matching quality, while high-pressure labour markets can pull workers back into employment. If that is true, the "natural rate" moves endogenously, and a long-run unemployment-inflation relationship can appear without implying a simple policy menu.

The registered VECM result should therefore be read narrowly. A REFUTED result against the Friedman natural-rate framing says the no-stable-long-run-tradeoff claim is not supported by this particular cross-country cointegration screen. It does not by itself prove a stable policy-exploitable Phillips curve, because identification still needs structural shocks, expectations, wage-setting institutions, and break tests.
