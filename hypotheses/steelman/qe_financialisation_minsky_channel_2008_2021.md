# Steelman — QE financialisation / Minsky channel

**The strongest argument against this hypothesis:**

The Minsky-channel reading describes the surface pattern accurately but mistakes the channel. The portfolio-rebalancing prediction of standard New Keynesian QE models *also* predicts the asset-price increase and the wealth distribution shift; the labels "Minsky" vs "portfolio-balance" attach to the same observed effect via different theoretical pathways. The financialisation framing adds value only if it predicts something the orthodox framing misses, and the three-gate test does not isolate such a prediction.

Specific objections the hypothesis must engage with:

1. **Mechanism vs label.** Bernanke's portfolio-balance model and Minsky's financialisation model both predict that QE reduces term premia, raises long-duration asset prices, and redistributes wealth toward asset holders. They differ on the mechanism (rational portfolio re-optimisation vs endogenous financial-sector expansion) but they coincide on the prediction. The three-gate test cannot distinguish them; supporting the hypothesis is also supporting the orthodox prediction.

2. **The asset-wage gap is partly a denominator effect.** Real wage growth was weak in advanced economies 2008-2021 for reasons unrelated to QE (productivity slowdown, China shock lag, labour-share decline). Reading the asset-wage gap as caused by QE attributes the wage stagnation to QE, which is too strong.

3. **Financial-sector profit share is volatile and partly cyclical.** The 2008-2021 window includes the post-crisis financial-sector deleveraging (2008-2012) and the late-cycle expansion (2013-2021). The cumulative profit-share comparison aggregates these phases; sub-period analysis would show a different picture in the early years. Reading the cumulative pattern as Minsky-confirming requires the late-cycle expansion to dominate, which it does, but the early-cycle compression is the cleaner counter-evidence.

4. **The household financial-asset Gini ought to widen anyway.** When asset prices rise, the asset-holding distribution mechanically widens the wealth Gini even if portfolios are unchanged. The HFCS waves show that household participation in equity markets is concentrated in upper quintiles regardless of QE, so the QE-induced asset-price-increase mechanically transmits to wider Gini. The empirical pattern is correct; the causal attribution to QE specifically is partial.

5. **Late-cycle policy reversal is not in the test.** The 2022-2024 tightening cycle reversed parts of the financialisation pattern: real estate prices declined in EUR, asset-vs-wage gaps narrowed in some countries, financial-sector profit shares compressed. The test cuts at 2021 and does not engage the reversal, which is the cleanest test of whether the pattern is QE-driven or secular.

**How this steelman should shape the result card:**

The result card must:

(a) Report the three-gate test result alongside an explicit acknowledgement that orthodox portfolio-balance models predict the same surface pattern.

(b) Run sub-period analysis (2008-2014 vs 2015-2021) to show the financial-sector profit-share trajectory.

(c) Report the 2022-2024 reversal as INFORMATIVE-context: gates that closed during tightening are confirmation of QE-driven directionality.

(d) Note that the asset-vs-wage gap aggregates secular wage stagnation and QE-driven asset inflation; partial attribution.

(e) State that the hypothesis SUPPORTS the descriptive financialisation pattern but does not adjudicate between Minsky and portfolio-balance theoretical readings.
