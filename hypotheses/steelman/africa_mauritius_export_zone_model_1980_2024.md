# Steelman — Mauritius EPZ model 1980-2024

**Strongest arguments against the framework's framing:**

1. **The Multi-Fibre Arrangement quota access is the actual treatment.** Mauritius's 1980s textile boom was largely a function of MFA quotas redirected from Asia. When MFA expired in 2005, Mauritian textile employment fell sharply. The "EPZ as institutional innovation" framing under-credits the quota-rents channel, which was a global trade-regime gift, not Mauritian policy ingenuity.

2. **Offshore-financial-centre rents are a tax-base-shifting story.** Mauritius's role as Indian-investment routing hub (Mauritius-India DTAA loophole) generated substantial financial-services GDP that is partially income-shifted from India rather than genuinely created. The 2016 GAAR amendment and 2017 DTAA renegotiation reduced these flows. Counting offshore-financial-centre GDP as Mauritian developmental success has accounting-honesty problems.

3. **Sugar-protocol legacy preferences mattered.** EU/UK sugar-protocol prices were 2-3x world prices through 2008. Mauritius's protected sugar income built the capital base that funded EPZ expansion. The "policy bundle" framing under-credits the colonial-legacy preference-rents.

4. **Population, geography, and human capital are the hidden treatment.** Mauritius is small, multi-ethnic with strong Indian/Chinese diaspora networks, English/French legal heritage, and high literacy at independence. These structural conditions are not replicable in continental SSA. The "Mauritian model" cannot be exported in its pure form, which limits the framework-policy implication.

5. **Madagascar received MFA quotas and did not converge.** This is the strongest Mauritius-as-genuine-outlier argument and the spec leverages it. But Madagascar's failures (political instability, weak property rights, geographic isolation) may make it a poor donor unit for any synthetic-control. The donor pool is structurally weak.

6. **The 2009-2010 financial crisis and 2020 COVID hit Mauritius hard.** Tourism, financial services, and offshore activities all collapsed. The cumulative-growth measurement is sensitive to whether 2020-2022 outliers are excluded or included. The synthetic control's pre-2020 weights may not generalise to the post-COVID regime.

**How this should shape the v1 result card:**

- Decompose Mauritian GDP growth into (a) pre-2005 textile-quota era, (b) 2005-2015 services pivot, (c) post-2015 GAAR-impacted era.
- Report the offshore-financial-services accounting adjustment as a memo item.
- Flag the structural-preconditions argument and resist drawing universal-policy conclusions.
- Frame as "small-economy specialisation case with replicable lessons in trade-policy sequencing" rather than universal model.
