# Steelman — Hungary 2019 overtime law

The strongest counter-reading: the "slave law" raised the legal cap but did not change actual practice for most firms, because annual overtime above 250 hours requires individual employee consent that many workers withheld in response to public mobilisation. MSZOSZ and Helsinki Committee data document substantial worker resistance, and KSH labour-statistics releases for 2019-2020 show only marginal changes in average annual hours actually worked at the population level. The COVID interruption of 2020 truncates the post-period catastrophically. The synthetic-control donor pool weights are sensitive to inclusion of Czechia and Slovakia which faced similar Tier-1-supplier auto-sector capacity-utilisation pressures and arguably ran de facto overtime increases without legal change. A v2 should use within-Hungary firm-level data on overtime usage by sector and firm-size to identify which firms actually used the new cap, and what share of the workforce was affected — the aggregate synthetic-control test will likely be underpowered.
