# Steelman: US Hart-Celler 1965 legacy

## Strongest case for the claim

Hart-Celler is the structural break in US migration policy of the 20th century. Pre-1965
quotas froze the immigrant population at ~5% of total; post-1965 inflows took it to ~14%
by 2020. The cumulative working-age and per-capita-output contribution is large and
identifiable in long time series.

## Strongest case against

The reform passed at a moment of falling US fertility; the post-1965 inflow may partly
have been accommodated as replacement migration that would have happened under any
permissive regime. The structural break in foreign-born share is real, but causal
attribution to Hart-Celler vs broader policy environment is contestable.

## Key uncertainties

- Counterfactual immigration trajectory under continued national-origin quotas.

## What would change my mind

A counterfactual simulation showing only modest deviation from realised foreign-born
trajectory under counterfactual rules would weaken the policy-attribution claim.
