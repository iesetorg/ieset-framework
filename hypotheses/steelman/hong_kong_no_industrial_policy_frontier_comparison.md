# Steelman — Hong Kong No Industrial Policy Frontier Comparison

## Strongest version of the claim
Hong Kong achieved income convergence toward the frontier over 1960-2024 through laissez-faire trade policy, strong rule of law, and minimal industrial policy, matching or exceeding the performance of East Asian developmentalist comparators (Korea, Taiwan, Singapore, Japan) after controlling for initial income and human capital. This suggests that the developmentalist policy package was not necessary for East Asian success and that market institutions alone could achieve comparable outcomes.

## Key evidence the claim would need
1. A panel FE or synthetic control showing that Hong Kong growth trajectory is statistically indistinguishable from or above the developmentalist comparator average after controlling for initial GDP per capita.
2. Evidence that Hong Kong sectoral composition (finance, trade, services) achieved productivity levels comparable to developmentalist manufacturing-led economies.
3. Counterfactual evidence that Hong Kong lack of directed credit, SOEs, or sector targeting did not impede its convergence.

## Best counterarguments
- **British institutional heritage:** Hong Kong inherited British legal system, civil service, and trade networks; these are not replicable policy choices but historical accidents.
- **Refugee human capital:** Post-1949 refugees from mainland China brought entrepreneurial talent and capital that independently drove growth.
- **China spillover:** Hong Kong growth accelerated after 1978 due to mainland China opening; the comparator countries did not have this massive neighbour effect.
- **Small city-state bias:** Like Singapore, Hong Kong small size and geographic position as a China entrepot create structural advantages that make it a poor comparator for larger developmentalist states.
- **Manufacturing hollowing out:** Hong Kong deindustrialisation after 1980 may have been faster than optimal; the absence of industrial policy may have contributed to excessive concentration in finance and real estate.

## Boundary conditions
- Claim is about convergence performance, not about industrial structure or resilience; Hong Kong may have grown fast while becoming structurally fragile.
- Post-1997 political changes and 2019 protests introduced new constraints not present in the earlier period.
- The claim does not argue that Hong Kong model is superior, only that it achieved comparable convergence without classic industrial policy.
