# Steelman — Classical-liberal property rights and FDI premium

## The strongest opposing argument

The Smithian property-rights-attract-FDI claim is the kind of textbook-classical proposition that has substantial empirical support but also large counter-examples that any honest framework must acknowledge:

1. **China.** The single largest FDI recipient over the 2000-2022 window scores poorly on WGI Rule of Law and Political Stability throughout, especially relative to its market size. Market access, scale, and supply-chain integration explain Chinese FDI inflows; property-rights security is at best a third-order factor. A theory that predicts "secure property rights drive FDI" must explain this case in a way that doesn't reduce to "China is a special case."

2. **Tax-haven flow-through.** WDI's FDI net inflow series counts intra-firm financial transactions through low-tax jurisdictions (Ireland, Luxembourg, Netherlands, BVI). These flows are large and respond to corporate-tax design, not to property-rights variation. The hypothesis tries to mitigate by trimming FDI > 25% of GDP, but the residual contamination is real.

3. **Resource-FDI is rent-capture.** Extractive FDI (oil, mining) flows to where the resources are; property-rights variation matters mainly through expropriation risk, which is partly insurable. The natural-resource-rents control mitigates this but doesn't eliminate it; the INFORMATIVE check (effect larger in low-resource-rents sub-sample) is the spec's concession.

4. **Bukele case ambiguity.** El Salvador post-2019 saw a large FDI uptick under a regime that scores worse on PV (state-of-exception, mass detention) and better on Rule of Law (security improvements). The composite hides the divergence and flattens a genuinely interesting case.

5. **FDI quality vs quantity.** The series cannot distinguish productive greenfield investment from speculative real-estate flows or tax-driven restructuring. A property-rights premium that shows up only in headline FDI/GDP can be a transfer-pricing phenomenon rather than a productive-capital phenomenon.

## What the framework still captures

The dual gate (panel-FE + cross-sectional) and the resource-rents-conditional INFORMATIVE check give the heterodox reading several clean win paths. The Chinese counter-example is real but is one observation; the panel-FE coefficient identifies off within-country variation across many countries. If property-rights variation is genuinely orthogonal to FDI flows once tax-haven and resource-rents effects are stripped, the panel-FE will report it. The hypothesis is honestly testable.
