# Steelman: Demographic dividend (working-age share × growth)

## Strongest case for the claim

Bloom & Williamson (1998) attributed roughly one-third of East Asian per-capita growth
1965-1990 to the working-age-share rise. The mechanism is mechanical (per-capita = per-worker
× working-age share) plus behavioural (savings rates peak at the working-age bulge). The
relationship should generalise globally because the arithmetic component is identity-true.

## Strongest case against

(1) Africa's demographic transition has not delivered Asian-style growth despite working-age
expansion, suggesting institutional preconditions dominate. (2) The arithmetic-identity
component is small (a few tenths of a percentage point); larger estimates conflate the
dividend with capital deepening and TFP gains during the same window. (3) Savings-effect
channel may be largely an Asian-cultural artefact rather than a universal demographic mechanism.

## Key uncertainties

- Magnitude of pure demographic-dividend effect vs. correlated TFP gains.
- Institutional moderators that gate the dividend.

## What would change my mind

A null result on the post-2000 sub-panel (where many countries are mid-transition) would
substantially weaken the claim that the dividend is a generalisable mechanism.
