# Steelman — Brazil Mercosur tariff persistence

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Effective protection is lower than nominal.** Brazil's
   weighted applied tariffs are partly compensated by free-trade-
   zone schemes (Manaus), drawback regimes for export-oriented
   manufacturers, and bilateral deals. Effective protection
   facing tradeable inputs is materially lower than the headline
   weighted tariff suggests.

2. **Industrial policy, not protectionism.** Brazilian developmentalist
   economic policy (CEPAL tradition) deliberately retained tariff
   space to protect industrial capability. Framing this as
   "Mercosur preventing liberalisation" misreads it as a constraint
   when it was a chosen policy.

3. **Mercosur is endogenous.** Brazil chose Mercosur partly because
   of its tariff-preservation properties; the agreement reflects
   Brazilian preferences rather than imposing them.

## What the framework still captures

The descriptive comparison records the magnitude of the tariff
differential without normative framing. The PARTIAL/REFUTED paths
accommodate the heterodox-developmentalist reading that the
persistence is policy-rational rather than welfare-suboptimal.
