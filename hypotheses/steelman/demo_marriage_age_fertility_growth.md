# Steelman: Marriage age × fertility × growth

## Strongest case for the claim

The marriage-age → fertility → working-age share → growth chain is a well-established
demographic-economic transmission. Cross-country evidence shows substantial co-movement;
female tertiary attainment is the canonical mediator.

## Strongest case against

Marriage age and tertiary attainment are highly correlated and may be capturing the same
underlying construct (women's autonomy / labour-market integration). Mediation
decomposition may be statistically unstable.

## Key uncertainties

- Marriage age vs tertiary attainment as separate constructs.

## What would change my mind

If tertiary-attainment fully absorbs the marriage-age coefficient, marriage age is a proxy
rather than independent channel.
