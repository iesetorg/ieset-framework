# Steelman — Korea IMF 1998 reform

The strongest heterodox reading: Korea's post-crisis duality is the central failure mode of IMF-conditioned flexibilisation. The 8pp+ non-regular-worker share increase is not a transition gain but the institutionalisation of a two-tier labour market in which 35-45% of workers receive sharply lower wages, weaker protection, and limited training — a structural inequality that has persisted into the 2020s. Cheon-Lee 2014 and Korea Labour Institute work show non-regular-to-regular transition probabilities are below 15% per year, meaning entry to non-regular status is largely permanent. An aggregate-employment recovery is therefore not a clean welfare claim if the recovered employment is durably low-wage and excluded from social insurance — the wage and protection margins absorbed the adjustment cost that the quantity margin avoided. A spec that scores Korea's reform on aggregate employment alone risks the same error as the Spain 2012 spec: claiming victory on the quantity margin while the wage and protection margins paid the bill. The duality-channel test is the discipline.
