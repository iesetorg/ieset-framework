# Steelman — Negative rates in the eurozone 2014-2022

## The strongest opposing argument

The "negative rates didn't break deposits" descriptive finding masks a less benign reality: bank net interest margins compressed substantially during 2014-2022, weighing on bank profitability and contributing to the sustained underperformance of eurozone bank equities. Calling the experiment "passed through smoothly" understates the cost to the banking-sector capital base. Some banks (Switzerland) imposed retail-deposit fees on large balances, which is functionally a deposit-rate cut even if not a headline run.

## What the framework still captures

The hypothesis is deliberately framed as descriptive, not as a normative defence of NIRP. Pre-registering the three specific pass-through and deposit-stability conditions tests whether the most-cited "negative rates work mechanically" claim holds, which is the substantive question. NIM compression is a separate test for a separate spec.
