# Steelman: Activation Sanctions Reemployment Duration Panel

The strongest counterargument is that sanctions can reduce registered unemployment without increasing real employment. Recipients may leave benefits for poverty, informal work, disability rolls, or family dependence, which would make caseload and duration metrics misleading. Strict monitoring can also crowd out useful job search by forcing applications to unsuitable jobs.

Conditionality may work only when paired with high-quality placement services, training, childcare, and strong labour demand. A sanctions-only regime can worsen match quality and increase repeat unemployment. The hypothesis should be overturned if stricter conditionality lowers benefit receipt but fails to raise employment-to-population, increases poverty, or lengthens unstable job cycling.
