# Steelman — trade_openness_patent_diffusion_panel

The strongest counterargument is reverse causality: innovative countries may
choose openness because their firms are already competitive internationally.
Country fixed effects do not eliminate time-varying reforms, education gains,
industrial clusters, or R&D spending that jointly raise patents and trade.

Patent counts are also an imperfect diffusion measure. They overstate formal IP
systems and understate process innovation, imitation, open-source diffusion, and
learning-by-doing in firms that do not patent. A null result would not prove
trade is irrelevant to technology transfer.

A supported result should be treated as evidence that openness and innovation
diffusion move together after basic controls. It should be upgraded only with
tariff-cut, WTO-accession, or trade-agreement event designs that establish
timing more cleanly.
