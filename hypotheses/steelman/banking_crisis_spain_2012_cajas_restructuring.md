# Steelman — Spain 2012 cajas restructuring

## The strongest opposing argument

Spain's banking crisis was concentrated in the unlisted regional cajas — politically governed savings banks with weak corporate governance — while the listed commercial banks (Santander, BBVA) emerged with manageable losses. Treating "Spain 2012" as a single canonical case obscures that the failure mode was governance-and-political-economy specific to the cajas system, not a generic property-credit boom-bust. A counterfactual Spain with stronger caja governance might have had a similar property-price decline without the bank-recapitalisation event.

The 25% peak unemployment rate is also partly structural (dual labour market, segmentation) rather than crisis-attributable; using "rise from baseline" rather than "peak level" helps but does not fully separate the crisis impulse from the underlying labour-market dysfunction.

## What the framework still captures

The 4-of-5 multi-metric pattern still tracks the canonical real-economy and fiscal signature of a systemic banking crisis, even granting the cajas-governance qualifier. The pre-registered thresholds make the test falsifiable: if Spain's house-price decline had stopped at 20% or unemployment had peaked at 18%, those metrics would not breach.
