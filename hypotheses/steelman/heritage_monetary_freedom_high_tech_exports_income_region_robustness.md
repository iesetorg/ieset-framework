# Steelman - heritage_monetary_freedom_high_tech_exports_income_region_robustness

The strongest pro-market interpretation is that Heritage monetary freedom captures an institutional or policy-regime feature that matters even among countries with similar income levels and within similar broad regions, so it should still predict high-technology export share after those controls.

The strongest objection is over-control and omitted-variable instability: GDP per capita is itself partly downstream of institutions, while region fixed effects are coarse and can absorb meaningful historical/institutional variation. A causal version needs panel timing, reform episodes, instruments, or synthetic comparisons.
