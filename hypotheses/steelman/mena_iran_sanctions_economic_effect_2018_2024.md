# Steelman — Iran sanctions economic effect 2018-2024

**Strongest arguments against the framework's framing:**

1. **Iran's pre-2018 trajectory was already unfavourable.** The 2014-2017 Rouhani-era recovery from earlier sanctions plateaued by 2017; structural problems (banking-sector NPLs, water/agriculture stress, demographic pressure on labour markets, dollar-shortage from poor BoP management) were already biting before Trump withdrew from JCPOA. Attributing the 2018-2024 contraction primarily to sanctions overcredits the external shock.

2. **Sanctions evasion is substantial and partially measured.** Iran has built shadow-banking arrangements with Russia, China, and various intermediaries. Discounted oil sales to China (~1-1.5m bpd at variable discount) have continued despite sanctions. Dark-fleet shipping, ship-to-ship transfers, and front-company structures have moderated the headline trade impact. The "50% decline" threshold may be met in IEA estimates but understated in actual flows.

3. **Domestic policy choices amplified the sanctions cost.** Subsidised foreign-currency allocation (Imam Khomeini Relief 4200 rate), price controls on essentials, and inflationary monetary financing of the budget were domestic choices that worsened the sanctions impact. Disentangling external-shock and domestic-amplification channels is necessary; the spec does not currently structure this decomposition.

4. **The 2022 protests and political-instability shock is a confound.** Mahsa Amini protests Sep 2022; Russian-Ukraine war geopolitical realignment 2022+; nuclear-program escalation. These institutional/political shocks affect 2022-2024 measurements independent of the sanctions intensity.

5. **Synthetic-control donor pool is weak.** Russia became sanctioned in 2022 (excluding from controls); Venezuela was sanctioned earlier; Iraq has its own institutional fragility; Algeria has different policy regime. The clean donor pool reduces to 2-3 effective units. Permutation inference is weak.

6. **Iranian official statistics have known measurement issues.** Inflation series have been criticised; GDP data is published with lags; informal-sector measurement is poor. The headline real-GDP-pc gap may have substantial measurement-error noise that does not reflect true underlying trajectory.

**How this should shape the v1 result card:**

- Report sanctions-evasion-adjusted oil-export estimates alongside official.
- Report dose-response from the sanctions-intensity ordinal as a separate test.
- Distinguish 2018-2021 (pre-Mahsa) from 2022-2024 windows.
- Acknowledge Iranian-statistics uncertainty bands.
- Frame as "sanctions caused major economic damage but with substantial evasion and domestic-policy amplification" rather than clean external-shock attribution.
