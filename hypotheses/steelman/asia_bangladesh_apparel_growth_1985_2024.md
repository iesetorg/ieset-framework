# Steelman — Bangladesh apparel-led growth 1985-2024

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Race to the bottom.** Bangladesh's labour-cost advantage rests on suppressed wages and weak labour standards (Rana Plaza 2013 collapse, recurring factory fires). Calling this a "success" without netting out worker-welfare costs is incomplete.

2. **Pakistan instability counterfactual.** Pakistan's underperformance reflects political instability, military rule cycles, and Afghan-conflict spillover — not specifically the absence of an apparel sector. The BGD-PAK differential overstates the apparel-policy contribution.

3. **Climate vulnerability undermines the model.** Bangladesh is one of the most climate-vulnerable economies. The 1985-2019 success may be eroded by sea-level rise, monsoon variability, and Brahmaputra-flood frequency. Forward-looking sustainability is uncertain.

## What the framework still captures

The manufacturing-share test captures the structural shift independent of welfare cost; welfare-side cost is a separate hypothesis (labour-standards and apparel-sector worker outcomes). The Bangladesh-Pakistan comparison is acknowledged as confounded by Pakistan's idiosyncratic instability; the SAARC peer pool absorbs some of this. The test is calibrated to detect a magnitude (+1pp/yr) that exceeds PAK's normal cyclical underperformance.
