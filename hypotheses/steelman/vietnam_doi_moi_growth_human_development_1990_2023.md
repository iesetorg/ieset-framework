# Steelman: Vietnam Doi Moi growth and human development

The strongest caution is that a four-metric descriptive checklist can
overstate causal clarity. Vietnam's gains may reflect favourable demography,
catch-up growth, remittances, global supply-chain relocation, and regional
trade conditions as much as domestic policy design. WDI health and employment
series are broad national aggregates; they do not resolve distributional
variation or informal-sector measurement issues. A supported checklist should
therefore be read as confirmation of a development pattern, not proof that any
single reform package caused it.
