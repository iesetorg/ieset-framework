# Steelman: Yield-curve inversions and unemployment

Yield-curve inversions are not mechanical recession switches. They can reflect
term-premium compression, global savings demand for Treasuries, or central-bank
credibility rather than future labour-market contraction. Episode boundaries are
sensitive to whether daily, weekly, or monthly spreads are used, and the 2022-2024
episode is right-censored in real time. A supported result should therefore be read
as a timing regularity, not proof that inversion itself causes unemployment to rise.
