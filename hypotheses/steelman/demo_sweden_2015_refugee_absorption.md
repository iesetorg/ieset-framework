# Steelman: Sweden 2015 refugee absorption

## Strongest case for the claim

A 1.6% population shock in one year, with refugee-source rather than skill-selected
composition, should produce measurable employment gaps and fiscal effects. Sweden's high
labour-market entry barriers compound the integration challenge.

## Strongest case against

Sweden's pre-2015 social-protection trajectory was already rising; attribution to refugee
inflow is contestable. Foreign-native employment gap data has compositional confounds.

## Key uncertainties

- Decomposing 2015-cohort effect from broader trends.

## What would change my mind

If gap-widening is < 1pp or social-protection break is undetectable, the absorption was
much smoother than expected.
