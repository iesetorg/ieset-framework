# Steelman: Mexico fertility decline × wages

## Strongest case for the claim

Mexico completed a rapid fertility transition; if any LATAM economy should show working-age
× real-wage dividend, it is Mexico. The single-country time-series test asks whether the
demographic channel registers above background productivity and US-migration noise.

## Strongest case against

Mexico's real wages have been notoriously stagnant 1990-2020 despite falling fertility.
Migration to the US absorbed the prime-age cohort; productivity stagnation capped wages.
The demographic channel may be present but undetectable in single-country data.

## Key uncertainties

- ILO real-wage series quality.
- Migration-control specification.

## What would change my mind

A null finding here is the most likely outcome and would not refute the broader dividend
thesis; it would only document Mexico's specific failure to convert demographics to wages.
