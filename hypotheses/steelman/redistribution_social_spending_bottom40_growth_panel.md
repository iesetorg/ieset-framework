# Steelman

The bottom 40 percent may benefit most from redistribution because market income growth often bypasses workers with low bargaining power, low assets, or weak access to formal jobs. Social spending can lift disposable incomes immediately and can create durable gains if it improves nutrition, schooling, and health.

The strongest counterprediction is that tax and government-consumption channels dominate employment and GDP growth in predicting persistent bottom-40 gains. If that pattern appears, redistribution-heavy systems are doing exactly what their proponents claim.
