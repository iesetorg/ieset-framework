# Steelman — Xiomara Castro / Honduras ZEDE repeal 2022-2026

**The strongest argument against the libertarian-prediction claim:**

The hypothesis tests two competing predictions: a libertarian one (ZEDE repeal triggers FDI
collapse and spread widening) and a heterodox one (no aggregate effect because ZEDE base was
small). The strongest case against the libertarian prediction — which the v1 result must
take seriously — comes from the structural composition of Honduran FDI.

Specific points the hypothesis must engage:

1. **ZEDE FDI base was small in absolute terms.** Próspera (Roatán) is the only ZEDE with
   significant publicly-reported investment, with cumulative committed capital reportedly
   in the $100-150m range and actual deployed capital materially smaller. Honduras's annual
   FDI inflow runs $700m-$1.2bn. Even if 100% of ZEDE-related FDI ceased post-repeal, the
   aggregate effect on national FDI-share-of-GDP would be small (<0.5 pp). The libertarian
   prediction of 1+ pp annualised divergence is hard to reconcile with the base-rate
   arithmetic.

2. **Honduran FDI is dominated by maquila + remittance-driven banking + telecom.** The
   sectoral mix of Honduran FDI (textile maquila, telecoms, banking, energy) is not
   ZEDE-related. Maquila FDI is driven by US-trade-policy + USMCA dynamics. Banking FDI is
   driven by remittance-volume growth. ZEDE-channel FDI was a small marginal addition, not
   the structural base. A repeal-driven decline in the structural base would require
   transmission via institutional-quality / arbitration-risk channels, which is the testable
   secondary claim.

3. **ICSID claim is a contained financial liability, not a cumulative spread driver.** The
   Próspera $11bn claim, even if ICSID rules in Próspera's favour, is a one-time financial
   judgement that gets priced in at announcement, not an ongoing institutional-deterioration
   signal. Sovereign credit-spread evolution post-2022 will largely track US-rate cycle
   and Honduran fiscal trajectory under Castro; the ICSID-specific component is small
   relative to those macro drivers.

4. **Castro government has signalled pro-investor framework outside the ZEDE channel.**
   Beyond the ZEDE repeal, the Castro administration has continued maquila tax incentives,
   maintained USMCA implementation, and not nationalised any industry. The total
   institutional-quality signal is mixed: ZEDE-repeal is anti-investor, but other
   indicators are not. The libertarian prediction relies on the ZEDE-repeal dominating the
   total signal; this is not obvious.

5. **HND pre-treatment WGI rule-of-law was already low.** Honduras's WGI rule-of-law was
   in the bottom quartile pre-2022 (Hernández administration). A ZEDE-repeal-driven
   institutional-quality decline has a smaller marginal effect when the baseline is already
   low. By contrast, a ZEDE-repeal in a high-rule-of-law jurisdiction would be more
   informationally damaging.

**How this steelman should shape the result card:**

The v1 result card must:

(a) Report HND FDI-share-of-GDP trajectory alongside the donor pool's, with explicit
    decomposition into ZEDE-attributable (manual estimate from Próspera disclosure)
    vs maquila / banking / telecom / energy components. Aggregate-level results may obscure
    a decomposition that supports the libertarian prediction at the ZEDE-channel level
    only.

(b) Track the Próspera ICSID claim trajectory (filing, hearings, possible award) with
    explicit event-study windows around each milestone — the bond-market reaction, if any,
    is the cleanest test of arbitration-risk pricing.

(c) Include "post-Castro-elections-2025-2026" as a potential structural break. If Castro
    is re-elected (or a successor with similar policy), the ZEDE-repeal becomes more
    durable; a return to a pro-ZEDE government would partially reverse the treatment.

(d) Compare HND outcomes to the Central American peer pool with explicit weighting —
    Costa Rica is a poor comparator (much higher rule-of-law baseline), Panama is similarly
    poor (offshore-banking driven). Donor-pool weighting choice affects the verdict.

(e) Accept that the most likely outcome is "heterodox supported" or "not informative" given
    the small ZEDE-FDI base, and that a "libertarian supported" verdict would require
    transmission via institutional-quality / arbitration channels that the data may not
    cleanly identify.
