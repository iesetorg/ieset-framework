# Steelman - market_dynamism_tariff_reduction_consumption_pc

Claim tested: Lower applied tariff rates predict faster real private-consumption-per-capita growth.

The strongest skeptical reading is that tariff cuts often occur inside crisis packages, exchange-rate reforms, or accession processes, so short-run consumption can fall even if prices improve later. Protectionist and Marxist-industrial accounts may argue that tariffs preserve domestic employment, bargaining power, or learning-by-doing capacity that later raises consumption. This screen can support a consumer-welfare openness mechanism, but it cannot settle distributional incidence or industrial upgrading without complementary tests.
