# Steelman — South Africa African Bank 2014

## The strongest opposing argument

African Bank was a sub-systemic monoline (unsecured-consumer-credit specialist), not a representative case of South African banking-system stress. Counting its 2014 curatorship as a "case" risks elevating an idiosyncratic event to canonical status. The deliberately-narrow 4-metric checklist mostly tests events the framework already predicted (curatorship occurred, GB/BB split occurred, no systemic coding) — a tautological structure.

## What the framework still captures

The point of including a deliberately-low-stakes single-institution case is to anchor the negative end of the systemic-vs-managed spectrum. If the framework cannot cleanly reject "systemic" classification for African Bank 2014, it is over-trigger-happy elsewhere.
