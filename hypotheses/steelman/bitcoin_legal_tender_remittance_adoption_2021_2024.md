# Steelman — Bitcoin legal tender remittance adoption 2021-2024

**The strongest argument against the null-result claim:**

The hypothesis is framed as a null: Bitcoin legal tender produced no material change in remittance volume or cost. The steelman for the opposing view — that BTC adoption did produce adoption, cost reduction, or volume effects — must engage the proponents' strongest arguments.

Specific objections the hypothesis must engage:

1. **Aggregate data understate infrastructure-channel effects.** The World Bank Remittance Prices Worldwide (RPW) quarterly survey measures advertised costs for the highest-volume corridors — US-to-SLV via Western Union, MoneyGram, Remitly, Wise. It does not capture the shadow-rail BTC flows that Bukele proponents argue are happening outside the formal corridor survey. Strike CEO Jack Mallers and the Bitcoin Office publicly state that meaningful BTC remittance volume flows peer-to-peer outside Chivo, which would not appear in either the BCR "remesas via billetera digital" category or the RPW corridor survey. The null may be a measurement artefact rather than an adoption failure. A steelmanned test would incorporate on-chain Lightning Network volume data (via Voltage, Strike public disclosures) as a corroborating mechanism variable, not only the BCR digital-wallet share.

2. **The cost-reduction channel operates via competitive pressure, not BTC-rail volume.** Even if BTC rails themselves carry <5% of remittance volume, the demonstration effect could have pressured Western Union and MoneyGram to reduce corridor pricing to SLV specifically, as a retention response. If SLV corridor costs fell relative to HND and GTM corridors (which were not treated) between 2021 and 2024, that pattern is consistent with the competitive-pressure mechanism even with low BTC-rail adoption. The synthetic-control estimator should report the corridor-specific cost differential as a separate falsification input.

3. **The Alvarez et al 2022 NBER paper measured early adoption (2021-2022), not steady-state.** The NBER 29968 paper documented Chivo's adoption collapse in the first 12 months, but it could not measure late-2023 and 2024 adoption patterns after Lightning Network infrastructure matured and after Chivo's operational issues were partly addressed. A steelmanned test must use the most recent BCR data (through 2024Q4) and not rely on the early-window NBER evidence as the dominant prior.

4. **The $30 buy-in was too small to produce a behavioural shift.** Chivo's $30 per-adult BTC incentive was approximately 0.15% of average annual remittance volume for a recipient household. The incentive was too small to overcome switching costs (new wallet, KYC, volatility concerns). A steelmanned proponent argument would say the experiment was underspecified, not that the underlying BTC-rail thesis is wrong — and that a $300 per-adult or a merchant-side subsidy would have tested the thesis better. The null result is therefore evidence that this specific policy design failed, not that BTC remittance rails are empirically unviable.

5. **Network-effects takeoff may be nonlinear and delayed.** The BTC-remittance-adoption dynamic is the classic two-sided platform problem: senders need enough merchants and receivers to accept BTC; receivers need enough senders to use BTC. A 2021-2024 window may be too short to observe the takeoff. Kenya M-Pesa took six years to achieve mass adoption. The null result at 3-year horizon is uninformative about steady-state adoption. A steelmanned test would extend the window and incorporate a delayed-effect specification via local projections.

**How this steelman should shape the result card:**

The v1 result card must:

(a) Report the corridor-specific cost differential (SLV-US corridor cost minus HND-US and GTM-US corridor cost) as a separate outcome, not only the SLV absolute cost. A competitive-pressure effect could show up there even with null BTC-rail volume.

(b) Incorporate Lightning Network on-chain volume data (Voltage, Strike disclosures) as a supplementary mechanism variable where available.

(c) Extend the outcome window as far as BCR data permit (through 2024Q4 minimum, 2025 if available at run time).

(d) Report the Chivo-share-of-remittances and BCR billetera-digital share as headline mechanism variables, alongside aggregate volume.

(e) Accept that a 3-year null does not refute the steady-state BTC-remittance thesis, only the specific policy as implemented. The result card language must reflect this conditional interpretation rather than claiming a general-purpose BTC-infrastructure falsification.
