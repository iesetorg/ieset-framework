# Steelman — Botswana post-2014 diamond dependency

**Strongest arguments against the framework's framing:**

1. **Botswana's institutional quality remains best-in-class for SSA.** WGI government effectiveness, control of corruption, and rule-of-law scores 2014-2024 remain in the top decile of SSA and outperform many upper-middle-income peers globally. Reading the post-2014 slowdown as institutional decay is unsupported; the slowdown is commodity-driven, not institutional.

2. **Diamond-cycle volatility is a feature not a bug of the Botswana model.** The country's sovereign-wealth-fund equivalent (Pula Fund), counter-cyclical fiscal policy, and historical commodity-buffer track record have absorbed prior diamond cycles successfully. The 2014-2024 episode is a normal cycle with normal stabilisation responses, not a model failure.

3. **The synthetic-control donor pool is fragile.** Namibia is structurally similar but has its own diamond exposure; Chile is a much larger and more diversified economy; Zambia has had political turbulence; Ghana has a different commodity mix. Permutation inference with a 4-unit donor pool is weak. The result will likely be wide confidence intervals consistent with a wide range of "true" gaps.

4. **Diversification is hard everywhere; failing to diversify is the modal outcome.** Resource-rich middle-income economies almost universally fail to diversify into manufacturing. Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, Qatar, Norway, Australia all retain significant resource-sector dependence after decades of diversification policy. Botswana is not unusual; setting a 2-ppt diversification threshold may be unrealistic.

5. **Lab-grown-diamond competition story is real but recent.** Lab-grown-diamond market share rose meaningfully only post-2020. Treating 2014-2020 as already-affected over-states the timing. The hypothesis would be sharper if the treatment date were 2020 (post-Botswana-Diamonds-Rough sales index break) rather than 2014.

6. **COVID and tourism collapse are confounds.** Botswana's tourism sector (Okavango, Chobe high-end) collapsed in 2020 and recovered slowly. This is a non-diamond-related shock that depresses 2020-2024 GDP measurement independent of the diamond channel. The synthetic control's COVID treatment may not adequately separate the two.

**How this should shape the v1 result card:**

- Run the synthetic-DiD with both 2014 and 2020 treatment dates and report both.
- Report tourism-sector contribution to GDP separately.
- Acknowledge the institutional-quality scores remain strong despite growth slowdown.
- Frame as "commodity-cycle pressure on a still-functioning institutional model" rather than "model failure".
