# Steelman — Dominican Republic tourism + free-zone model 1980-2024

**The strongest argument against:**

The hypothesis credits the DOM model with a Caribbean growth premium. The opposing view is that the apparent success is heavily debt-financed and that the late-window fiscal and current-account positions are precarious.

Specific objections:

1. **Tourism + free-zone is debt-financed.** Public debt as a share of GDP rose materially through the 2010s; external debt grew faster than tourism receipts. The "growth premium" rests on a financing trajectory that is not durable indefinitely.

2. **Free-zone employment is low-wage assembly.** The 1990s textile boom collapsed after 2005 (post-MFA expiration); the apparent employment gains were not durable. The current free-zone composition (medical devices, electronics) is more sophisticated but still offers limited wage-growth pathways.

3. **Tourism is climate-vulnerable.** Caribbean hurricane intensification (Hurricane Maria 2017, Beryl 2024) imposes recurring infrastructure costs. The DOM tourism model is structurally exposed to this risk; the apparent stability of the 1980-2019 window may not generalise to a higher-frequency-storm future.

4. **Haitian-migrant labour subsidy.** A meaningful share of DR tourism and construction labour is provided by Haitian migrants under exploitative conditions. The growth model has a regional-spillover exploitative component the headline numbers do not capture.

5. **Inequality and informality remain high.** Despite formal-sector growth, the DR's gini index stayed above 0.45 throughout the 2010s; informal employment exceeds 50% of total.

**Result-card implications:**

(a) Report public debt trajectory alongside GDP-pc growth.
(b) Show free-zone employment series 2005-2024 to expose the MFA-expiration shock.
(c) Note climate-risk exposure in commentary.
(d) Frame as "growth-with-fragility" rather than unconditional success.
