# Steelman — China demographic cliff 2020-2024

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Quality, not quantity.** Working-age population decline is offset by rising educational attainment per worker. China's tertiary enrolment rose from 8% (2000) to ~60% (2022). Per-worker human capital is rising faster than headcount is falling.

2. **Robotics and automation substitute.** China's industrial robot installations are the world's largest (>50% of new installations). Automation can substitute for the labour-supply shrinkage at the manufacturing frontier; the demographic constraint is binding only for non-tradable services.

3. **Migration headroom remains.** Rural-to-urban migration still has a runway (urbanisation rate ~64% in 2022 vs OECD ~80%). The "working-age population" decline includes rural-resident workers who have not yet been urbanised; effective urban labour supply growth is positive.

## What the framework still captures

The test is on level demographic outcomes (population peak, dependency ratio) — these are realised facts independent of attribution. The downstream growth-impact attribution is acknowledged as confounded with property and zero-COVID; that decomposition is reserved for separate hypotheses. The demographic cliff being "observable" does not by itself imply a quantitative growth drag — it only flags that the dividend phase has ended.
