# Steelman: Japan ageing × deflation

## Strongest case for the claim

Older cohorts have lower marginal propensity to consume; rising old-age dependency depresses
aggregate demand and wage-growth pressure, producing persistent below-target inflation.
Japan's combination of the world's most-aged demographics and three-decade deflation is
prima facie consistent. A VECM detecting cointegration between OADR and CPI for Japan with
significantly more-negative loading than peers would make the case.

## Strongest case against

Goodhart-Pradhan ("The Great Demographic Reversal") argue ageing is inflationary: older
cohorts dis-save and consume from accumulated wealth while working-age supply shrinks,
producing wage-push. Japan's deflation may instead reflect BoJ policy mistakes, balance-sheet
recession (Koo), or zombie-firm capital misallocation — none of which is intrinsically
demographic. The cross-country test is the discriminating evidence.

## Key uncertainties

- Whether other ageing economies (DEU, ITA) show similar OADR-CPI relationship.
- Confounding from monetary regime and BoJ policy choices.

## What would change my mind

If Germany and Italy show similar OADR-CPI loadings to Japan, the Japan-specific
explanation collapses and the demographic-deflation channel earns standing. If they don't,
Japan's deflation needs a non-demographic primary explanation.
