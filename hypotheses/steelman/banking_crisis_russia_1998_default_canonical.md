# Steelman — Russia 1998 default

## The strongest opposing argument

Russia 1998 is over-determined as a "canonical case" because the underlying transitional macro environment (incomplete tax administration, oligarchic privatisation, oil-price shock to commodity exports in 1997-1998) makes attribution of the bank failures to standard banking-crisis mechanisms (credit boom, maturity mismatch, regulatory weakness) ambiguous. Many "banks" in 1998 Russia were not banks in the OECD sense; they were captive financing arms of industrial groups. Counting Russia next to Mexico-Tequila or Asian-1997 risks treating institutionally heterogeneous events as comparable.

## What the framework still captures

The 5-metric checklist is tuned to the joint sovereign-default + currency-collapse + banking-coding pattern, which Russia 1998 unambiguously displays. The hypothesis is not that Russia's banks behaved like Korean chaebol-affiliated banks; it is that the canonical multi-metric signature held.
