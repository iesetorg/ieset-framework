# Steelman — Nord Stream sabotage 2022 European energy-security pivot

**The strongest arguments against the framework's "supplier pivot is verified, broad, and stable" framing:**

1. **The pivot is partially reversible and the new dependencies are not obviously safer.** Replacing Russian pipeline dependence with Norwegian pipeline dependence concentrates EU gas supply on a single non-EU operator (Equinor) at terminal capacity. Replacing Russian gas with US LNG creates dependence on a transactional commercial relationship subject to US export-policy decisions, which can be politicised (Biden's January 2024 LNG export-permit pause was a signal that this is a real lever). "Diversified" supply with three concentrated suppliers (Norway, US, Qatar) is structurally better than monocular Russian dependence but is not the "low-concentration resilient" outcome the headline implies.

2. **FSRU additions are bridging infrastructure, not permanent.** Most of the 2022-2024 EU regasification additions are floating-storage-and-regasification-units (FSRUs) on 5-10 year charter contracts, not fixed onshore terminals. As charters expire (2027-2032), some FSRUs will leave the EU; the EU could face a regasification-capacity cliff in the late-2020s if onshore terminal projects do not progress. The descriptive inventory of "capacity added" can mask that some of it is temporary; the structural-pivot framing requires permanent infrastructure that has not yet been built.

3. **Russian gas continues to flow to the EU via residual channels.** TurkStream pipeline (entering EU via Bulgaria-Serbia-Hungary) supplies HUN, AUT, ITA. Russian LNG (Yamal LNG, Sakhalin) continued to be imported by ESP, FRA, BEL through 2024 — at volumes 2-4x higher than pre-war LNG flows. The headline "Russia share fell to <15%" is true on aggregate but disguises continued substantial Russian LNG exposure for some member states. A more honest framing: Russia pipeline-gas dependence was eliminated, Russia LNG dependence persisted.

4. **The cost of the pivot was substantial and is borne by EU consumers.** TTF gas prices remained 3-5x higher than US Henry Hub through 2024, structurally embedded by the LNG arbitrage (US LNG must clear at TTF-Henry Hub spread that covers shipping + regas). The "energy security" gain came at a permanent cost differential vs the US that did not exist with Russian pipeline gas. A descriptive inventory should report this explicitly: pivot was achieved, but at a price.

5. **Norwegian gas production has a finite shelf life.** Norwegian gas production is at or near plateau (110-120 bcm/yr); the geological resource cannot scale meaningfully above current levels and is projected to decline post-2030 absent new field discoveries. Treating Norway as a permanent substitute for Russian gas is a 5-10 year solution, not a 20-30 year one. The structural-pivot framing has a temporal expiry that the descriptive framing does not surface.

6. **Hidden imports (energy-intensive intermediates) substitute for direct gas imports.** EU gas demand fell partly because energy-intensive products (ammonia, urea, hot-rolled steel, aluminium) are now imported rather than produced domestically. The "we need less gas" narrative rests partly on outsourced gas demand. This is a real climate-perverse outcome the pivot inventory does not capture.

**How this should shape the v1 result card when run:**

- Distinguish Russian pipeline gas (eliminated) from Russian LNG (continued, in some member states).
- Report FSRU vs onshore terminal capacity separately. The bridging-infrastructure share is a fragility the headline obscures.
- Report TTF-Henry Hub spread persistence as an explicit cost of the pivot.
- Project Norwegian production trajectory — it is not infinite.
- Note the hidden-imports caveat from eu_gas_storage_winter_2022_2023 hypothesis.
- Acknowledge that the pivot is a fact, but the "structural energy security" narrative oversimplifies dependencies and timelines.
