# Steelman — Swiss cantonal jurisdictional competition as Hayekian discovery

## The strongest opposing argument

The Swiss cantonal "discovery process" framing dresses up a tax-competition race-to-the-bottom in Hayekian language. The high-growth low-tax cantons (Zug, Schwyz, Nidwalden) are not discovering efficient policy bundles — they are extracting rents from the broader Swiss federation by offering tax-base relocation to firms and individuals whose productive activities continue to depend on cross-cantonal infrastructure, federal-level rule-of-law, and Zurich/Geneva financial-services hubs that they free-ride on.

**Key social-democratic / heterodox objections:**

1. **Tax-base relocation is not productive growth.** Zug's corporate tax base swelled by attracting holding companies and Russian/global commodity-traders' headquarters that continue to operate physically elsewhere. The "GDP growth" of low-tax cantons partly reflects accounting relocation of profits booked in the canton, not new productive activity created there. The Swiss canton-of-residence/canton-of-activity distinction is precisely designed to make tax-base relocation easy.

2. **Free-riding on federal public goods.** Cantonal autonomy operates within a Swiss federation that provides shared rule-of-law, banking infrastructure, education quality (ETH/EPFL), and defence/diplomacy as common goods. Low-tax cantons benefit from these without paying their share, and high-tax cantons subsidise the system. This is not a Hayekian discovery process — it is a coordination problem about who finances the public goods.

3. **High-earner migration concentrates inequality.** The "discovery" process Hayek predicts requires that successful policy bundles spread to other jurisdictions. In practice, Swiss cantonal tax migration concentrates millionaires in Zug/Schwyz without those tax bundles being replicable elsewhere — they only work because they are exceptions within a high-tax federation. If every canton adopted Zug's tax rates, the federal budget would collapse.

4. **Industry composition does most of the work.** Pharma cluster (Basel), banking (Zurich, Geneva), watchmaking (Vaud, Neuchâtel), commodity trading (Zug, Geneva) — Swiss cantonal income variation is overwhelmingly explained by which centuries-old industry cluster a canton inherited, not by recent regulatory choices. Attributing growth differentials to tax policy mistakes industry-cluster path dependence for policy-bundle discovery.

## What the framework still captures

Cantonal autonomy is a real, large, and rare institutional feature, and the cantonal data are unusually high-quality. The pre-registered three-outcome test (GDP, business formation, migration) deliberately requires the business-formation channel — which does not allow accounting relocation — to support the Hayekian reading. If business-formation rates do not co-vary with cantonal regulatory burden, the discovery-process claim fails even if accounting GDP and migration patterns look favourable to it. The framework is honest that Swiss cantonal results may not generalise outside the high-trust, high-quality-rule-of-law Swiss institutional environment.
