# Steelman — Milei shock-therapy output-recovery trajectory

**The strongest arguments against coding a recovery trajectory as vindication:**

1. **Recession costs in 2024H1 were severe and distributionally regressive.** Peak-to-trough real GDP contraction of ~3-4% year-on-year plus a poverty headcount surge to ~52% (UCA Observatorio) are not incidental. The pre-Keynesian / post-Keynesian reading argues these costs are scarring rather than transient: skill depreciation, firm exit, balance-sheet damage to small businesses, and delayed investment commitments can produce hysteresis that headline GDP recovery masks. A trajectory that reaches the pre-treatment level by 2026 might still leave the productive-capacity frontier permanently lower than the no-shock counterfactual.

2. **Commodity-price and external tailwinds were favourable during the recovery window.** Argentina's terms of trade improved 2024 via stable-to-rising soybean and corn prices, low-volatility Brazilian real, and supportive US real rates late 2024. Attributing recovery to Milei policy content without controlling for this external environment overstates the policy share of variance. A clean synthetic control must match on commodity-exposure dynamics, which is precisely why the LatAm donor pool is specified.

3. **The recovery comparison is sensitive to donor-pool choice.** If Brazil, Chile, and Peru are included, the donor pool includes economies with mild slowdowns 2023-2024; if Mexico-AMLO is heavily weighted, the comparator itself had policy-driven softness. Synthetic-control weights can be dominated by one or two donors with specific policy trajectories. Transparency on which donors receive non-trivial weights is critical; a positive CATT driven by comparators that had their own policy disturbances is not clean.

4. **Peso liberalisation sequencing is uncertain through 2025.** Lifting FX controls (cepo) is expected to produce a transient output disturbance — either a confidence-driven rebound or a new adjustment shock depending on whether the underlying balance of payments is sustainable. The output trajectory through 2025 is entangled with this regime-change event; attributing net recovery to "shock therapy" without distinguishing the stabilisation from the liberalisation sequencing is reductive.

5. **The 2026Q4 pre-treatment-level threshold is demanding.** If Argentina's pre-Milei path was itself declining (secular stagnation under Fernández), then returning to 2023Q4 level merely means resuming the prior sickly trajectory. A more stringent test would require return to a counterfactual trend level, not the pre-treatment level. The pre-registered hypothesis uses pre-treatment level which understates how much growth would constitute genuine recovery.

**How this should shape the result card:**

- Report the synthetic-control donor weights transparently. If 1-2 donors dominate, the finding is narrower than a pooled LatAm comparison.
- Present both "return to pre-treatment level" and "return to pre-treatment linear trend" metrics. The pre-registration commits to level; the trend comparison is a pre-registered secondary outcome.
- Display GDP alongside unemployment, real wages, poverty headcount, and Gini. Recovery in GDP headline without recovery in distributional indicators is a partial result.
- Explicitly decompose the recovery into (a) rebound from inventory / one-off devaluation shock, (b) fiscal-credibility-driven investment recovery, (c) commodity-cycle tailwind. The policy attribution is strongest where commodity cycle is neutral.
- Acknowledge the regime-sequencing uncertainty: cepo liberalisation is an event within the observation window whose effects cannot be cleanly isolated from stabilisation effects.
