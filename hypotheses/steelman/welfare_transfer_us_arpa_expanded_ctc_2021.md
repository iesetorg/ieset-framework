# Steelman: US ARPA Expanded CTC 2021 Effect

The strongest counter is that the Columbia CPSP monthly poverty series uses an imputation methodology that mechanically produces large coefficient changes at policy-implementation dates because the monthly-poverty estimate itself incorporates the policy-counterfactual structure. Independent SIPP and ASEC verification produces smaller estimates of the CTC poverty-reduction effect (Burns-Fox-Wimer 2022 Census), in the 2-3pp range rather than 4pp+. The monthly-disbursement design also bundled with simultaneous SNAP emergency allotments, expanded EITC, stimulus payments, and the broader pandemic-recovery labour-market — making clean event-study identification of the CTC expansion specifically hard. On the labour-supply arm, Corinth-Meyer-Stadnicki-Wu (2021) at AEI/UChicago argued the long-run labour-supply response would be larger than the 6-month horizon captures, with extensive-margin reductions of 1.5M workers in steady state — the hypothesis's claim of less than 1pp absolute change is biased downward by the short post-window. A defensible reading: ARPA-CTC reduced child poverty in the short term, but the 4pp+ effect is at the upper bound of plausibility and the labour-supply null is methodology-conditional.
