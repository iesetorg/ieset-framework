# Steelman — Friedman PIH consumption-smoothing on microdata

The strongest argument against this hypothesis is that the Kaplan-Violante "wealthy hand-to-mouth" finding has structurally undermined the PIH's headline prediction. Roughly one-third of US households at any given time hold substantial illiquid wealth (housing, retirement accounts) but very little liquid wealth, and these households respond to transitory income shocks like liquidity-constrained households even though their lifetime-income permanent measure is high. The Parker-Souleles-Johnson-McClelland 2001 and 2008 rebate studies find MPC on non-durables of 0.25-0.35 within 3 months and 0.5+ over 6 months including durables, in samples that include large fractions of homeowners with positive net worth. This is much higher than a strict PIH predicts and inconsistent with Friedman's original framing that transitory windfalls should be saved. The hypothesis's threshold (MPC_transitory <= 0.3 in the unconstrained sub-sample) is borderline — it might survive in the strictest definition of unconstrained (positive liquid assets above some cutoff) but fails in any broader definition. From the behavioural-economics side, the salience and mental-accounting literature (Thaler, Shefrin) shows that consumption response depends on framing of the shock (lump-sum vs spread, expected vs unexpected, labelled vs unlabelled) in ways that the PIH cannot accommodate, so the headline MPC(permanent) > MPC(transitory) gap is real but the magnitudes depend on framing rather than on the permanent/transitory distinction itself. Imbens-Rubin-Sacerdote's lottery-winner study found long-run MPC on lottery winnings of roughly 0.11 per year (close to PIH-consistent), but their sample is selection-biased toward high-income lottery players. Buffer-stock theory (Carroll, Deaton) provides a cleaner alternative reading: there is no permanent-vs-transitory distinction in the agent's optimisation problem; there is a target wealth-to-permanent-income ratio and consumption tracks a target-driven adjustment process. Buffer-stock predicts MPC_transitory rises with the gap between actual and target wealth, which generates exactly the heterogeneous MPC pattern Kaplan-Violante document. The hypothesis's pre-registered moderator analysis on pre-shock assets is the load-bearing element, but the PIH's framing (clean permanent-transitory dichotomy) is already a degraded version of the original Friedman claim, and a "supported" verdict at the moderated thresholds is consistent with buffer-stock theory rather than vindicating Friedman per se. The cleanest test of the strict PIH would require MPC_transitory close to zero in the unconstrained sub-sample, which the literature already rules out.
