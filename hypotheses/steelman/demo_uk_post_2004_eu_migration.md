# Steelman: UK post-2004 EU migration

## Strongest case for the claim

The UK's open-door policy on A8 accession produced the largest peacetime migration inflow
in UK history. Wadsworth-Dustmann find small wage effects on natives concentrated in the
bottom of the distribution. The hypothesis tests both the magnitude and the
small-effect-on-wages reading.

## Strongest case against

The bottom-decile wage effect may be detectable in regional data but invisible in national
time series; many other shocks (financial crisis 2008, Brexit pre-shock 2016+) overlap with
the inflow window.

## Key uncertainties

- Crisis confounding 2008-2010.

## What would change my mind

A negative wage deviation > 1.5pp/year sustained for 5+ years would refute the
"small effects" consensus.
