# Steelman — Greece troika internal devaluation

The strongest counter-reading: Greece's reforms were partially implemented, repeatedly reversed, and operated in a banking-collapse environment that swamps any labour-market signal. The synthetic-control donor pool cannot recover a meaningful counterfactual because no donor experienced a 25%+ GDP contraction or a 2015 capital-controls episode, and pre-2010 outcome paths cannot match a country whose post-treatment macro environment is so unprecedented in the donor set. IMF reflective evaluations (2013, 2016) acknowledge that the program's wage-channel adjustment overshot by design but that quantity adjustment was choked off by demand collapse, so a finding of "wage fell, quantity didn't move" is observationally consistent both with "internal devaluation doesn't work" and with "internal devaluation didn't get a fair test in a demand-suppressed environment." A clean refutation pathway exists if a reader argues — credibly — that real-wage adjustment would have generated employment gains absent the demand-suppression compounded by Grexit-fear and capital-flight. The spec must report the demand-control sensitivity (government-consumption gap) for that reason.
