# Steelman: REER Appreciation and Trade-Open Disinflation

Exchange-rate appreciations do not always pass through to consumer prices:
pricing-to-market, hedging, administered prices, and monetary-policy reactions
can absorb currency movements.

This makes the trade-open interaction valuable. If pass-through is real, the
effect should be more visible where trade exposure is high. If not, the result
should be weak or unstable.
