# Steelman — Malaysia 1MDB economic effect 2015-2024

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Oil-price collapse 2014-2016 dominates.** Brent fell from $115 (June 2014) to $30 (Jan 2016). Malaysia's oil + gas + palm-oil exports are large fractions of fiscal revenue and current account. Attributing growth softness to 1MDB rather than the commodity shock is mis-specified.

2. **Self-correcting institutions.** The 2018 GE14 victory by Pakatan Harapan unseating Najib was a peaceful transition triggered by the scandal — evidence that Malaysian institutions worked. Reading 1MDB as institutional damage understates the response.

3. **Growth recovery 2017-2019.** Malaysia's growth recovered to 4.7-5.7% in 2017-2018 alongside oil-price normalisation. The "underperformance" is partly cyclical not structural.

## What the framework still captures

The two-test structure separates governance (WGI) from growth (peer-differential). The WGI test is a credible direct measure of the institutional channel that the oil-price story cannot explain. The growth test is acknowledged as confounded with commodity cycle but the magnitude threshold (-0.5pp/yr) is calibrated to detect a genuinely-larger-than-peer-cycle gap. If WGI test passes but growth doesn't, the institutional channel still validates.
