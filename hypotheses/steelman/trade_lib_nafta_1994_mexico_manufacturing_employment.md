# Steelman — NAFTA 1994 effect on Mexican manufacturing

## The strongest opposing argument

The synthetic-control reading credits NAFTA with Mexico's
manufacturing-export deepening, but the genuinely hard objections
come from the structuralist and labour-economics traditions:

1. **Maquila-shell critique.** Mexican manufacturing-export growth
   was concentrated in low-value-added assembly maquiladora
   operations with weak domestic linkages. Headline export shares
   rose without a corresponding deepening of domestic technological
   capability or productivity, so the "manufacturing gain" is
   compositional rather than developmental. A spec that uses
   manufacturing value-added passes a partial test of this
   objection but does not fully resolve it.

2. **Asymmetric labour outcomes.** Mexican real manufacturing wages
   stagnated or fell relative to the US through the 1994-2007
   window, contradicting the simple Heckscher-Ohlin prediction
   that Mexico (the relatively-labour-abundant partner) should see
   wage convergence upward. Hanson, Robertson and others document
   regional wage gains concentrated in northern border states with
   significant losses elsewhere.

3. **Agricultural collateral damage.** NAFTA opened Mexican corn
   markets to US subsidised imports, displacing roughly 1-2 million
   smallholder maize farmers and accelerating rural-to-US migration.
   This channel is excluded from the manufacturing-focused spec but
   is a first-order welfare effect.

4. **The China shock dwarfs the NAFTA effect.** From 2001 onward,
   Chinese WTO-accession-driven import competition disproportionately
   hit Mexican manufacturing (Utar-Ruiz 2013; Iacovone-Rauch-
   Winters 2013), reversing some NAFTA gains and complicating any
   single-treatment evaluation.

## What the framework still captures

Restricting the evaluation window to pre-2008 isolates the cleaner
NAFTA signal from the China shock. The synthetic control on
manufacturing value-added and merchandise-exports-share is a
defensible first-order test. The PARTIAL verdict path (exports up,
domestic value-added flat) operationalises the maquila-shell
critique cleanly. Aggregate-welfare and rural-employment channels
require separate specs.
