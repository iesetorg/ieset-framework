# Steelman: Finland Basic Income Experiment Effect

The strongest counter is that the Finnish RCT did not test UBI per se — it tested replacing conditional-unemployment-benefit with unconditional-cash for unemployed people, which is a narrow margin compared to the population-wide UBI proposals that motivate the policy debate. The treatment group remained on the same nominal benefit level; only conditionality was removed. Generalising the small-positive employment finding to a population-wide UBI requires assuming labour-supply elasticities are constant across the pre-employed and currently-employed populations, which the literature suggests they are not (Marinescu 2018 review). The wellbeing improvements are also self-reported and may reflect the Hawthorne effect of being a research subject rather than the cash itself. The 2-year duration is short relative to UBI's longer-horizon claims about entrepreneurial-risk-taking and educational-investment, neither of which the design tested. A defensible reading: the Finnish RCT is the cleanest available evidence on conditionality-removal for unemployed cohorts, but the headline "small positive employment + wellbeing improvement" should not be over-interpreted as endorsing population-wide UBI.
