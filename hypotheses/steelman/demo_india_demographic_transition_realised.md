# Steelman: India demographic transition realised vs projected

## Strongest case for the claim

India's working-age share rose from ~57% (1990) to ~67% (2020), a substantial dividend
window. Yet per-capita growth, while strong post-1991, fell short of Asian-tiger benchmarks
in working-age-translation terms. The hypothesis documents the under-utilisation gap and
points toward female LFP and informal-sector capture as candidate explanations.

## Strongest case against

Asian-tiger comparators (KOR, TWN, SGP) had different starting institutional configurations
(land-reform legacies, education systems) that India never had. Comparing dividend-translation
to that benchmark may set an unrealistic counterfactual; the right comparison is to other
South Asian or large-population late-transition economies.

## Key uncertainties

- Comparator-set choice.
- Female-LFP-specific channel quantification.

## What would change my mind

If India's per-capita growth attributable to working-age channel matches the South Asian
median (PAK, BGD, LKA), the under-utilisation framing should shift to "South Asia
generally" rather than India-specific.
