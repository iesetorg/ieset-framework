# Steelman — Chile 2001 Lagos labour reform

The strongest market-liberal counter-reading: Law 19,759 was a marginal recalibration on the heavily-flexibilised Pinochet-Plan-Laboral baseline, with the collective-bargaining-coverage rise of ~3pp small in absolute terms (from very low base) and reversed by post-2006 Bachelet policy continuation rather than the 2001 reform itself. Bensusán and Cook 2003 argue Chilean labour protections remain weak by Latin-American standards even after the Lagos reforms, suggesting the headline coverage rise overstates the bite. The employment-margin null is itself ambiguous — Chile's 2001-2006 employment-rate trajectory was dominated by the copper-price boom which would have suppressed any reform-induced labour-cost rise into noise. A clean refutation pathway: if a within-sector event-study on copper-mining (treated by collective-bargaining changes) shows null employment effect while non-copper sectors show no labour-reform-attributable change either, the entire claim collapses to "reform was too small to detect". The spec's near-symmetric prior reflects this — the test is genuinely informative only at the extremes.
