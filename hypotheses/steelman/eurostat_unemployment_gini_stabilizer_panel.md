# Steelman: Unemployment and Gini Stabilizers

European tax-transfer systems are explicitly designed to cushion unemployment
shocks, so disposable-income Gini may move little even when labour-market slack
rises. Measurement timing can also differ across labour and income surveys.

That hard-case setting makes the test informative. A positive result would show
that stabilizers are incomplete; a null would be evidence of meaningful
distributional insurance.
