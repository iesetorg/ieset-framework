# Steelman — US-China Phase One 2020

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **COVID confounding is severe.** The agreement's first year
   coincided with the deepest global trade collapse since the
   1930s. Attributing the under-fulfilment to Chinese non-
   compliance vs COVID demand collapse is genuinely hard.

2. **Structural commitments matter more than purchases.** The
   intellectual-property, market-access, and forced-technology-
   transfer chapters were the substantive content; aggregate
   purchase numbers were political theatre.

3. **WDI aggregate data cannot test the bilateral commitment.**
   The spec is honest that it can only test aggregate US-export
   step-up, not the bilateral US-China specific commitments.

## What the framework still captures

The conservative null prediction is the right test on the
available aggregate data. The qualitative literature finding is
disclosed honestly; the spec does not over-claim WDI-data
identification of the bilateral target shortfall.
