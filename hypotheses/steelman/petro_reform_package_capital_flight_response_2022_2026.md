# Steelman — Petro reform package / capital-flight response 2022-2026

**The strongest argument against the causal claim:**

The hypothesis is that the Petro reform package produces capital-flight signals (peso depreciation,
spread widening, dollarisation, portfolio outflows) beyond the synthetic counterfactual. The
opposing view is that the observed signals are dominated by the global EM cycle (Fed tightening
2022-2023, oil-price volatility 2022-2024) and that the Petro-specific component is small or
zero once those controls are included.

Specific objections the hypothesis must engage:

1. **2022-2023 EM-wide capital-flight cycle.** The Fed hiking cycle drove broad EM-currency
   depreciation and spread widening across LatAm. Peru, Mexico, Chile all saw spreads widen
   200-400 bp in 2022-2023; the COL widening of similar magnitude is hard to attribute to
   Petro-specific factors. The synthetic-counterfactual must extract the EM-cycle component
   cleanly, which is non-trivial when all donor-pool members are subject to the same shock.

2. **Oil-price collapse from 2022 high is the dominant peso driver.** Brent oil fell from
   ~$120 (mid-2022) to ~$70-80 (2023-2024); the COL peso, as a petro-currency, depreciated
   broadly with this move. Attributing the depreciation to Petro reform-package signals
   versus oil-price pass-through is a difficult identification problem. The hypothesis's
   oil-price control needs to do heavy lifting.

3. **Petro reforms have been substantially diluted in Congress.** The 2022 tax reform passed
   roughly as proposed but the labour-reform and pension-reform proposals have been
   substantially watered down or stalled in Congress. A capital-flight response calibrated
   to the announced reform package may be over-stated; investors who priced in the original
   package and then re-priced as Congress diluted it would show a partial reversal of any
   initial capital-flight signal.

4. **EMBI spread compressed in 2024-2025.** Through 2024-2025 the COL EMBI spread compressed
   meaningfully (from ~430 bp peak to ~250-280 bp). If the synthetic-counterfactual control
   for global EM-spread compression captures this, the COL-specific spread may be flat or
   slightly negative. A clean Petro-effect is hard to find in 2024-2025 spreads alone.

5. **Resident-deposit dollarisation has structural drivers.** Colombia's deposit dollarisation
   has been low-trend (single digits of total deposits) for decades, partly due to Banco de
   la República's strong inflation-targeting credibility. A Petro-era dollarisation rise from
   8% to 12% would be statistically significant but would not produce the kind of
   stress-signal the hypothesis frames. The threshold for "supported" on this channel is hard
   to set ex-ante in a way that distinguishes signal from noise.

**How this steelman should shape the result card:**

The v1 result card must:

(a) Report channel-by-channel CATT separately, with explicit oil-price and US-rate-cycle
    decomposition. A "supported" verdict on REER without the Brent control is meaningless.

(b) Include event-study windows around specific reform announcements (tax-reform passage,
    energy-transition speech, pension-reform proposal) so that capital-flight signals are
    associated with policy events, not just the post-2022Q3 indicator.

(c) Track the reform-package-completion ratio (proposed vs passed). If only the tax reform
    passed and other reforms stalled, the "reform package" treatment is partly null and the
    capital-flight test should be calibrated to actual implementation, not announced intent.

(d) Compare 2022-2023 (high-flight period) vs 2024-2026 (compressed-spread period) sub-windows
    so the result is not dominated by the early-Petro-shock window.

(e) Accept that the most likely outcome is "partial support: REER and early-spread-widening
    show signals, late-spread-compression and modest deposit dollarisation do not" and that
    the policy takeaway is "redistributive-reform announcements produce short-window capital
    flight that partly reverses as Congressional dilution is observed."
