# Steelman — CPTPP 2018 truncated agreement

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **US withdrawal gutted the agreement.** The TPP's headline gain
   was access to the US market under deeper integration provisions;
   the CPTPP without US access covers a much smaller share of
   global GDP and the provisions are correspondingly less binding.

2. **COVID-19 dominates 2019-2021.** The trade disruption swamps
   any small CPTPP signal. A clean identification window does not
   exist within the 2018-2024 sample.

3. **Pre-existing bilateral FTAs.** Many CPTPP member-pairs
   already had bilateral FTAs (CAN-MEX via NAFTA, JPN-MEX, JPN-AUS).
   The marginal liberalisation introduced by CPTPP is small relative
   to the pre-existing bilateral coverage.

## What the framework still captures

The honest descriptor is "small effect, hard to identify." The
spec is calibrated to that — using point-estimate sign as the
primary test rather than significance, and reporting CIs that
will likely span zero. The PARTIAL verdict path is the modal
expected outcome.
