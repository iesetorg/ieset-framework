# Steelman — Chile bilateral FTA cascade

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Commodity-driven mechanically.** Copper price cycles drove
   most of the export-share variation; the FTA cascade was
   secondary. Trade-openness rising during commodity booms is not
   evidence of FTA-induced trade creation.

2. **Pre-existing low-tariff baseline.** Chile already had low
   uniform tariffs from the 1979 reforms. The bilateral FTAs
   added marginal preferential access on top of an already-open
   regime; the marginal effect is small.

3. **Domestic industrial costs.** Chile's persistent
   deindustrialisation and copper-dependence are the welfare
   cost of the open-regionalism strategy; the trade-openness
   metric is silent on this.

## What the framework still captures

The descriptive comparison records a real and large pattern.
Disentangling commodity-cycle from FTA effects requires a
separate decomposition spec. The PARTIAL/REFUTED paths are
clean for the commodity-driven critique.
