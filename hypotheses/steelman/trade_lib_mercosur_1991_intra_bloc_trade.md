# Steelman — Mercosur 1991 trade trajectory

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Stagnation is policy, not failure.** Post-2003, Brazilian
   and Argentine governments deliberately slowed Mercosur deepening
   in favour of political-sovereignty preservation and South-South
   integration projects. The "stagnation" descriptor misreads a
   chosen trajectory as a failed one.

2. **Trade diversion vs creation.** The 1990s rise in intra-
   Mercosur trade was partly trade diversion away from non-bloc
   suppliers (Yeats 1998 critique). The descriptive claim that
   the 1990s saw real gains overstates the welfare effect.

3. **Macro instability dominates.** Argentina's 2001 collapse,
   Brazil's 1999 devaluation, and recurring inflationary cycles
   produce so much trade-share noise that any small Mercosur
   effect is swamped by the macro environment.

## What the framework still captures

The descriptive pre/post-1998 split is robust to the policy-
choice critique: it records the pattern without claiming it is
a Mercosur failure. The trade-diversion critique is genuine but
applies to all FTAs and is handled at the literature level rather
than within this single spec. The macro-instability point is
exactly why the descriptive estimator is preferred over a DiD that
would over-claim identification.
