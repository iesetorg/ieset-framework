# Steelman

GDP can rise without households feeling richer. The market-liberal claim is stronger if market institutions predict real consumption per person, not just output. Open trade, competition, and property rights should improve household welfare through cheaper goods, more job opportunities, and better capital allocation.

The opposing view is that market reforms can raise GDP while increasing insecurity or inequality, and that consumption gains may come from debt or commodity cycles. A credible test needs long windows, initial-income controls, and robustness to crisis outliers.
