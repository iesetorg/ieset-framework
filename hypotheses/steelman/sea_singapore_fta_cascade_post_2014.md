# Steelman — Singapore CECA / FTA cascade post-2014

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Trade openness is geography, not policy.** Singapore's >150% trade/GDP ratio reflects its position as a regional entrepôt. FTAs may marginally lower friction but the openness baseline is exogenous.

2. **Re-export distortion.** Singapore's exports include massive re-export volumes; gross trade share overstates the value-added trade activity. The 170% threshold may be reached mechanically without policy contribution.

3. **High-income East Asia peer pool is heterogeneous.** Hong Kong's 2019-2020 protests + 2020 NSL produced large idiosyncratic shocks; including HKG in the peer pool inflates SGP's apparent outperformance through HKG underperformance.

## What the framework still captures

The hypothesis is calibrated to "maintenance," not "policy-induced gain." If Singapore had failed to maintain its trade model post-2014, that would be informative. The growth-differential test is a moderate +0.3pp/yr threshold — passable on small differences but resilient to peer-pool noise. Re-export distortion is a known feature of Singapore data; the threshold is calibrated against historical Singapore baselines that include this pattern.
