# Steelman — Japan 1990 lost decade

## The strongest opposing argument

Japan 1990-2003 is sometimes decoupled into a 1990-1992 asset-price bust (which produced an immediate but moderate output effect) and a 1997-2002 banking-resolution crisis (Yamaichi, LTCB, Nippon Credit failures). Treating it as a single 13-year canonical case obscures that the lost-decade growth rate reflects demographic-and-investment-rate factors as much as banking-channel impairment. Hayashi-Prescott 2002 argue the lost decade is mostly a TFP slowdown story.

## What the framework still captures

The multi-metric checklist deliberately includes both market-price (Nikkei, house prices) and bank-balance-sheet (NPL, Laeven-Valencia coded 1997-2001) signals. Even granting Hayashi-Prescott, the asset-market and NPL metrics are unambiguous on their own and the joint pattern of bank-balance-sheet impairment + delayed resolution + decade-long sub-1% growth is the canonical "delayed bank cleanup" case the hypothesis identifies.
