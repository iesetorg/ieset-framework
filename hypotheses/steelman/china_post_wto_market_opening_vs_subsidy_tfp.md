# Steelman — China Post-WTO Market Opening vs Subsidy TFP

## Strongest version of the claim
China experienced its strongest total factor productivity acceleration in the decade following WTO accession in 2001, driven by market opening, foreign competition, and import of advanced technology. In contrast, the post-2010 period of heavy state subsidies, directed credit, and Made in China 2025 industrial policy is associated with weaker TFP growth, rising misallocation, and declining marginal efficiency of investment. The shift from market-liberal to state-directed mechanisms marks a turning point in China growth quality.

## Key evidence the claim would need
1. A clear TFP growth acceleration in 2001-2010 relative to 1990-2001 and 2010-2024, using multiple TFP measures (PWT, Brandt-Zhu, Kehoe-Ruhl).
2. Sectoral evidence that industries most exposed to foreign competition post-WTO (textiles, electronics assembly) showed larger TFP gains than protected sectors (steel, autos, finance).
3. Evidence that post-2010 subsidy-heavy sectors (renewables, semiconductors, EVs) show overcapacity, falling returns, and zombie-firm problems despite headline output growth.

## Best counterarguments
- **Base effects:** TFP growth necessarily slows as an economy approaches the frontier; the post-2010 deceleration may be mechanical rather than policy-driven.
- **Long gestation:** Subsidy-heavy sectors (semiconductors, batteries, EVs) have long investment horizons; their TFP may surge in the 2020s as scale effects kick in.
- **US-China trade war:** Post-2018 tariffs and technology restrictions independently hurt TFP by fragmenting supply chains; attributing the slowdown to domestic subsidies conflates two distinct shocks.
- **Infrastructure spillovers:** Post-2010 state investment in high-speed rail, 5G, and renewable energy may raise economy-wide TFP with long lags not yet visible in the data.
- **Measurement issues:** Official Chinese statistics are widely suspected of smoothing growth; true TFP may have been lower in the 2000s and higher in the 2010s than measured data suggests.

## Boundary conditions
- Claim is about TFP growth quality, not about GDP growth magnitude; China may sustain high GDP growth through capital deepening even with weak TFP.
- The post-WTO period (2001-2010) also saw massive state investment; disentangling market opening from state direction within the same period is difficult.
- Sectoral TFP variation may be more informative than aggregate TFP trends.

## Relation to existing hypotheses
