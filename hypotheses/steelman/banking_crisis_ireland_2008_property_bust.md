# Steelman — Ireland 2008 property-credit bust

## The strongest opposing argument

Ireland's 2008 collapse is sometimes framed as a regulatory failure (light-touch supervision under Irish Financial Services Regulatory Authority), but a competing view is that it was a small-open-economy / euro-membership story: ECB monetary policy set for the German cycle, Irish real interest rates negative throughout 2003-2007, and a credit-supply shock that any plausible domestic regulator would have struggled to brake without exchange-rate flexibility. Counting "Troika programme entered" as a breach blurs the line between crisis severity and policy response — Ireland chose not to default on senior bank bondholders, which made the fiscal cost much larger than it would have been otherwise.

## What the framework still captures

Even granting the euro-membership monetary channel, the Irish metric values are extreme on absolute terms (house prices halved, debt up 70+ pp). Pre-committing to a 4-of-5 threshold tests the canonical pattern without adjudicating the European-monetary-union counterfactual.
