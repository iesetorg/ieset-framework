# Steelman — Brazil PROER 1995-1997

## The strongest opposing argument

PROER is sometimes characterised as a stealth-bailout that socialised losses from politically-connected banks (especially Banco Económico). Counting "bank interventions occurred" as a metric does not separate "well-designed pre-emptive resolution" from "expensive crony bailout dressed up as resolution". The Laeven-Valencia coding (yes-systemic) cuts against the "preventive" framing: by their methodology, Brazil 1994 was a real systemic banking crisis, not a near-miss.

## What the framework still captures

The metric structure deliberately includes Laeven-Valencia coding as a yes (acknowledging the systemic classification) alongside the negative-control on real GDP growth (which was indeed positive throughout). The combination tests whether the canonical "systemic by external coding but contained on macro outcomes" pattern fits PROER, which is the substantive question.
