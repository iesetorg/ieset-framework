# Steelman — GCC oil-price decoupling 2014-2024

**Strongest arguments against the framework's framing:**

1. **Apparent decoupling is partly an oil-price-stability artefact.** Oil prices were broadly range-bound 2016-2019 ($40-70/bbl) and 2021-2024 ($70-100/bbl with one spike). When the input variable has lower variance, the regression beta is mechanically harder to estimate even if true sensitivity is unchanged. The "decoupling" claim could be data-period artefact rather than structural change.

2. **Public-sector pay and procurement smooths the cycle.** GCC governments smooth public-sector wage bills and capital-spending across oil cycles using SWF drawdown / debt issuance. This makes measured GDP less correlated with oil-price changes without underlying private-sector decoupling. The dependent-variable smoothing rather than independent-variable decoupling.

3. **VAT revenue is small in absolute terms.** GCC VAT rates are 5-15%, narrow base; revenue is on the order of 2-4% of GDP. This is meaningful but not transformative. Crediting VAT introduction with "fiscal-buffer institutionalisation" overstates a moderate revenue diversification.

4. **Non-oil GDP itself is partly oil-rents-financed.** Government spending on infrastructure, health, education funds construction and services activity; this "non-oil GDP" is not actually decoupled from oil rents — it is rentier-state intermediated demand. A clean test of decoupling requires identifying genuinely tradeable non-oil sectors, which is much smaller than headline non-oil GDP.

5. **2020 COVID is a non-trivial confound.** The 2020 simultaneous oil-price collapse (negative WTI April 2020) and pandemic shock biases the post-2014 covariance estimate strongly. Excluding 2020 is reasonable but choosing which outliers to exclude is judgment-laden. Both inclusion and exclusion specifications must agree for the decoupling to be robust.

6. **PIF / ADIA / Mubadala investing internationally returns dollar income to home economies.** These flows partially offset oil-price downcycles via investment-income upcycles, generating a portfolio-stabilisation effect that should be measured separately from "non-oil-sector expansion".

**How this should shape the v1 result card:**

- Report with and without COVID 2020.
- Report rolling 5-year correlations as a visualisation alongside sub-period beta ratio.
- Distinguish GDP smoothing from underlying private-sector decoupling.
- Acknowledge SWF-investment-income channel as a separate mechanism.
- Frame as "fiscal-policy-induced GDP smoothing with modest private-sector diversification" rather than fundamental decoupling.
