# Steelman — LatAm Bukele imitation effect / homicide security state

**The strongest argument against the causal claim:**

The hypothesis is that Honduras, Ecuador, and Peru — having imitated SLV's estado-de-excepción
model — produce statistically distinguishable homicide reductions AND institutional-quality
deteriorations relative to non-imitating LatAm peers. The opposing view is that the imitator
implementations are too varied (different durations, different geographic scope, different
prison-capacity backbones) to generalise; that gang-violence dynamics are heavily idiosyncratic
to local cartel structure; and that institutional-quality measurements have too much
measurement noise at 24-month horizons to detect a 0.10 SD effect cleanly.

Specific objections the hypothesis must engage:

1. **Implementation heterogeneity is large.** SLV's régimen relied on the CECOT mass-prison
   capacity and a coherent national-government-controlled narrative. Honduras's estado-de-
   excepción covers selected municipalities only, lacks comparable prison capacity, and
   coexists with Castro-administration-tolerated gang-truce dynamics. Ecuador's January 2024
   declaration came after the assassination of presidential candidate Villavicencio and the
   prison-takeover crisis; Ecuador's prison system is overwhelmed. Peru's estado-de-emergencia
   is geographically narrow (Lima-Callao) and politically contested. Treating these three as
   a single imitator cohort obscures critical differences.

2. **Homicide-rate dynamics are gang-structure-dependent.** SLV's homicide collapse was
   facilitated by the unique features of MS-13 / Barrio 18: relatively centralised, geographically
   concentrated, and previously party to politically-mediated truces. Mexican cartels, Ecuadorian
   gangs (Choneros, Lobos), and Peruvian organised crime are structurally different —
   poly-centric, narcotics-financed, transnational. A mass-incarceration intervention may not
   compress homicide-violence the same way because the underlying gang-economic-incentive is
   fundamentally different. The Mexican militarisation experiment (2006-) is the canonical
   counter-evidence.

3. **Institutional-quality measurement has long lags.** WGI updates annually with 12-month lag;
   V-Dem updates annually with 6-month lag; Freedom House annual. A 2022-imitator (Honduras)
   has at most 2-3 data points by 2024; Ecuador (2024) has 1-2 data points. Detecting a 0.10
   SD effect with this few observations and the donor-pool noise is statistically difficult.
   The hypothesis may be measurement-underpowered.

4. **Reverse-causality on the institutional-quality channel.** Countries with deteriorating
   institutional quality may be MORE likely to adopt estado-de-excepción in the first place
   (Ecuador's 2024 decision came amid a state-capacity crisis; Peru's 2023 declaration came
   amid Boluarte-administration legitimacy challenges). The treatment indicator may be
   confounded with pre-treatment institutional-quality decline. A clean test requires
   instrumenting the imitation decision — which the hypothesis does not currently specify.

5. **Joint-channel threshold may be too strict.** Requiring both channels to meet their
   thresholds creates a high bar. A "supported on benefit, mixed on cost" outcome is
   substantively important — it would indicate the model generalises with measured but
   smaller institutional cost than SLV experienced. The result-card framing should give
   sub-claim verdicts more prominence than the joint verdict.

**How this steelman should shape the result card:**

The v1 result card must:

(a) Report results unit-by-unit (HND, ECU, PER separately) before pooling. Heterogeneity
    across units is informative and the pooled result may obscure it.

(b) Distinguish geographic-scope (national vs municipal) and prison-capacity intensity
    of each implementation. A mass-incarceration treatment without prison capacity is a
    fundamentally different intervention.

(c) Compare gang-structure characteristics qualitatively across treated units (centralised
    vs poly-centric, narcotics-financed vs extortion-financed) and flag the SLV-MS13 specific
    features that may not generalise.

(d) Report institutional-quality channel results with explicit confidence intervals showing
    statistical-power constraints. A null on this channel may be measurement-underpowered
    rather than substantive.

(e) Accept that the most informative verdict is likely "homicide-channel supported in HND
    and ECU, weak in PER; institutional-channel supported in HND, mixed in ECU, weak in PER"
    — a heterogeneity pattern, not a clean joint verdict.
