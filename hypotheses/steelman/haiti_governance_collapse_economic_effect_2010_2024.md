# Steelman — Haiti governance collapse economic effect 2010-2024

**The strongest argument against:**

The hypothesis attributes Haiti's economic collapse to governance failure. The opposing view is that exogenous shocks (the 2010 earthquake, repeated hurricanes, cholera epidemic, the COVID shock, gang-related violence triggered by external arms flows) explain most of the trajectory and that governance is endogenous to those shocks.

Specific objections:

1. **2010 earthquake was a 7.0-magnitude exogenous shock.** Roughly 200,000 deaths and infrastructure damage worth ~120% of GDP imposed a baseline that no governance regime could quickly recover from. Treating the post-2010 trajectory as "governance failure" ignores the magnitude of the initial shock.

2. **External-actor failures.** The post-earthquake international-donor coordination was famously poor; the UN MINUSTAH mission introduced cholera; US-imposed firearms exports flowed to gangs. Many of the worst outcomes had non-Haitian-state causes.

3. **WGI scores are partly endogenous to outcomes.** WGI uses survey-of-experts data that incorporates economic outcomes; the institutional-quality decline may partly reflect rather than cause economic collapse.

4. **Emigration is a household-level adaptation.** Treating emigration as an indicator of "collapse" frames a labour-market option as failure.

5. **Pre-2010 trajectory was already weak.** Haiti's pre-2010 GDP-pc growth was negligible; the 2010-2024 decline reflects the loss of pre-existing infrastructure, not a deviation from a healthy trajectory.

**Result-card implications:**

(a) Show 2010 earthquake explicitly as separate from governance.
(b) Note external-actor causal contributions in commentary.
(c) Acknowledge endogeneity of WGI to economic outcomes.
(d) Treat emigration as adaptation, not pure outcome.
