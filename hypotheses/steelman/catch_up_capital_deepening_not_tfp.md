# Steelman

The market-liberal interpretation of developmentalist success is that much early growth comes from mobilizing savings, building infrastructure, moving labor into higher-productivity sectors, and importing existing technologies. These are real gains, but they may not prove that state direction creates durable innovation or frontier TFP growth.

The developmentalist response is that capital deepening and learning-by-doing are part of the process, not a weakness. A fair test should decompose growth rather than dismissing catch-up as unimportant.
