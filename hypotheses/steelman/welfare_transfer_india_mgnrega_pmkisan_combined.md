# Steelman: India MGNREGA + PM-KISAN Combined Effect

The strongest counter is that MGNREGA and PM-KISAN both suffer from substantial leakage and ghost-beneficiary issues that bias the headline ATT upward. CAG audits have repeatedly flagged MGNREGA muster-roll fraud at 15-25% of nominal person-days in several states; PM-KISAN had to launch farmer-card-Aadhaar reconciliation in 2020 to remove millions of ineligible recipients. The state-staggered rollout of MGNREGA is also non-random: Phase-1 districts were selected on backwardness criteria, biasing the early-treated subsample toward higher poverty-elasticity. The 2020-2022 COVID period blurs the post-window for PM-KISAN identification, and the post-2022 farmer-protest concessions further fragment the treatment definition. Imbert-Papp (2015) found MGNREGA's wage-floor effect was concentrated in lean-season states with weak private-employment options — generalisation to all-India is contested. A defensible reading: both programmes produce real rural-welfare effects but the complementary-channels claim is fragile given the leakage and selection issues, and the magnitude is closer to 1.5-2.5pp combined ATT than 3pp.
