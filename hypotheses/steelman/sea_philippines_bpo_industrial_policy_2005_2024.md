# Steelman — Philippines BPO industrial policy 2005-2024

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **English-language endowment, not policy.** The Philippines' English-language workforce is a colonial-era institutional inheritance, not a 2005 policy choice. PEZA tax incentives may matter at the margin but the comparative advantage is exogenous to 2005 industrial policy.

2. **AI displacement risk.** BPO success 2005-2019 is largely call-center and mid-skill back-office work. Generative AI threatens 30-50% of this employment by 2030. The "success" is fragile and may not survive the test window.

3. **Remittance dominance.** Filipino remittances (~10% of GDP) are larger than BPO (~7% of GDP). Reading PHL growth as BPO-led understates the remittance channel.

## What the framework still captures

Services share growth absorbs both BPO and remittance-funded domestic services. The +5pp threshold is large enough to detect meaningful structural shift; the +0.3pp/yr growth differential is calibrated against ASEAN-5 peers who have similar EM headwinds. AI-displacement risk is a forward-looking concern, not relevant to backward-looking 2005-2019 test.
