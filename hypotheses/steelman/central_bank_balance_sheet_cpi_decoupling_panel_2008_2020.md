# Steelman — Central-bank balance-sheet CPI decoupling, panel 2008-2020

**The strongest argument against this hypothesis:**

The 2008-2020 cutoff is the entire ballgame. Choosing 2020 as the endpoint excludes the 2021-2024 inflation episode, which the strongest mainstream rebuttal reads as the delayed pass-through of cumulative base-money expansion finally materialising once the COVID supply shock fired the cylinder. The decoupling claim is robust within the chosen window; the question is whether the chosen window is the right one.

Specific objections the hypothesis must engage with:

1. **Pre-registration of the cutoff matters.** A 2008-2020 sample produces a clean decoupling result. A 2008-2024 sample produces a much weaker decoupling result, with core CPI in USA/UK/EUR running at 5-7% for 18+ months in the late sample. Choosing the cutoff to maximise the decoupling finding looks like specification mining. The strongest defence is that 2008-2020 captures the QE-as-policy-tool window, and 2021-2024 conflates fiscal coordination, supply shocks, and tightening — but that defence is post-hoc and cannot be fully cleaned up.

2. **Monetary-multiplier collapse is the orthodox reading.** Even Friedman-school monetarists since 2010 have acknowledged that base-money-to-broad-money transmission collapsed under interest-on-excess-reserves. The decoupling pattern is therefore not contested; what is contested is the theoretical interpretation. PK endogenous-money reads it as confirmation of the framework; weak-monetarist reads it as a special-feature of IOER-era policy that does not generalise. Both readings fit the data.

3. **Inflation expectations anchoring is a credible-CB outcome, not an MMT outcome.** The hypothesis tests that 5y5y forwards stayed in the pre-2008 anchored range. They did, partly because the Fed/ECB/BoE/BoJ were perceived as committed to inflation targets. The anchoring is a *consequence* of credible inflation-targeting central banks, not evidence that monetary expansion is harmless. Removing the inflation-targeting commitment (as some emerging-market cases show) re-couples balance-sheet expansion and inflation.

4. **The four-institution panel is small and homogeneous.** All four institutions are advanced-economy, reserve-currency-or-near-reserve, with strong inflation-targeting credibility. Generalising the decoupling result to "currency-issuers" requires extending to non-anchored jurisdictions where the pattern would not hold. The spec narrows scope to advanced economies but the rhetorical generalisation in PK/MMT discourse does not respect that narrowing.

5. **Granger causality test is weak power.** With 12-year monthly data and slow CPI dynamics, Granger causality at h=12-36 months has limited power; the failure-to-reject is partly a power story rather than a confirmation of decoupling. Higher-frequency identification (announcement-window event studies) would be a stronger test.

**How this steelman should shape the result card:**

The result card must:

(a) Pre-register the 2008-2020 cutoff explicitly and report 2008-2024 as a robustness window with clearly worse decoupling.

(b) Report the inflation-anchoring channel as a credible-CB-policy effect, not as evidence that base-money expansion is harmless under any monetary regime.

(c) Note that the decoupling result holds in advanced anchored-expectations jurisdictions and does not extend to emerging-market non-anchored jurisdictions.

(d) Run the test with both core-CPI and headline-CPI; the headline result is more sensitive to oil and food prices and may be noisier.

(e) State that the decoupling finding is consistent with both PK endogenous-money and weak-monetarist money-multiplier-collapse readings; the data does not adjudicate.
