# Steelman — Argentina 2001-2002 corralito

## The strongest opposing argument

Argentina 2001-2002 is a hard-peg-collapse case where the banking-crisis component was largely a forced consequence of the currency-board failure plus pesification by decree (asymmetric conversion of dollar-denominated bank assets and liabilities at different rates). Treating the banking outcomes as an independent crisis severity signal double-counts: corralito and pesification ARE the crisis policy response, not consequences flowing through arms-length channels.

The 10%+ GDP decline also reflects multi-year contraction beginning in 1998 (Brazilian devaluation contagion, structural fiscal-federalism dysfunction) more than the 2001 banking event itself, so attributing the full peak-to-trough decline to 2001-2002 is overstated.

## What the framework still captures

Argentina 2001-2002 is the textbook hard-peg-collapse-plus-banking-suspension case and the multi-metric pattern is unambiguous on its face. The 5-of-6 threshold tolerates the question of how to attribute output decline across years while retaining sufficient discipline.
