# Steelman — India 1991 trade-share break

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **The break started in the mid-1980s.** Pro-business tariff
   adjustments under Indira Gandhi from 1980 and Rajiv Gandhi from
   1985 began the trade-opening trajectory before 1991. Using
   1991 as the structural break overstates the formal-package
   contribution.

2. **Currency depreciation drove much of the export response.**
   The 1991 rupee devaluation was a one-time price adjustment;
   the tariff schedule changes were the more durable policy
   shift, but the headline export-share jump in 1991-1993 was
   primarily devaluation-driven.

3. **Import shares moved more than export shares.** Indian
   trade-openness rose largely because imports rose under
   liberalisation; the export response was slower and more
   sectorally concentrated (services from late 1990s, not goods).

## What the framework still captures

The structural-break test is robust to all three critiques: it
records the magnitude without claiming pure causality. The
INFORMATIVE check (extending post-window to 2019) tests whether
the break is durable rather than a 1991-1993 devaluation blip.
The framework intentionally does not over-claim the mechanism;
that is for follow-on decomposition specs.
