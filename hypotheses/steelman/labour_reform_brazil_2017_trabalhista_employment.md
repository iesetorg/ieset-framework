# Steelman — Brazil 2017 trabalhista employment

The strongest counter-reading: Brazil's 2018-2019 was a fragile recovery from the 2015-2016 recession, dominated by Petrobras-corruption-investigation aftermath and the Bolsonaro-election political-cycle dynamics, neither of which the synthetic-control donor pool can absorb cleanly. DIEESE and CUT-affiliated research argues the intermittent-contract category is a re-labelling channel — workers who were previously informal or short-CLT-contract are now intermittente, which appears in the formal-employment statistics without representing a true labour-market gain. The informal-sector-paired outcome is the discipline: if formal employment rises while informal share is unchanged, the reform converted unmeasured-informal to measured-intermittent without expanding labour demand. IPEA evaluations 2019 and 2020 found minimal evidence that the reform expanded total hiring at the firm-level extensive margin. The COVID interruption truncates the post-period, so a v2 with a longer post-window (2022-2024 once Lula-government partial reversals are codified) would provide cleaner identification.
