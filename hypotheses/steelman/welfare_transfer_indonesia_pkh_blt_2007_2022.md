# Steelman: Indonesia PKH + BLT Combined Effect

The strongest counter is that BLT was politically motivated rather than welfare-optimised — its 2005/2008/2013/2014/2022 launches all coincided with politically-difficult fuel-subsidy-removal episodes, and the actual welfare design is a second-best compensation rather than a chosen instrument. Targeting accuracy via UDB (Unified Database) was poor in early waves, with leakage and inclusion-error rates above 30% in some provinces per BPS audit. PKH's school-enrolment effects are concentrated in primary-to-secondary transition where Indonesia's pre-existing enrolment gap was already narrowing, and lower-secondary attendance rose strongly in non-PKH provinces too due to the BOS school-block-grant programme launched 2005. Cahyadi-Hanna-Olken (2020) flagged that PKH's identification is contaminated by BOS rollout overlap. The 2020-2022 COVID period also produced near-universal cash transfers that contaminate the post-window identification of the structural PKH effect. A defensible reading: PKH and BLT both work but the channel-decomposition story over-claims clean identification given the BOS overlap and the political-driven BLT timing.
