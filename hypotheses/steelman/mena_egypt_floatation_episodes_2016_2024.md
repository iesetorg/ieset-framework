# Steelman — Egypt floatation episodes 2016/2022/2024

**Strongest arguments against the framework's framing:**

1. **Three "episodes" treated as one pattern is a category error.** The 2016 floatation was a textbook IMF-anchored cold-turkey adjustment from a 9 EGP/USD peg to a managed-float regime. The 2022 episode was a war-shock-driven response to capital outflow when EM portfolio investors fled after Russia's invasion of Ukraine. The 2024 episode was effectively bought out by the UAE's $35bn Ras El Hekma land deal which collapsed the parallel-rate premium without any real reform. Coding all three as "Egypt floats again" obscures that the political-economic content of each episode was different.

2. **The "managed float" is in scare quotes.** After each devaluation step, the CBE rebuilt a de facto peg at the new level, then defended it until the next FX crisis. This is sequential-step-devaluation, not floatation. The framework's monetary-regime classification should be honest about this — Egypt has not had a market-determined exchange rate at any point in the 2014-2024 window.

3. **Reserve "rebuilds" are partly Gulf deposits, not market accumulation.** Saudi/UAE/Qatari deposits at the CBE have been the primary mechanism of post-devaluation reserve recovery. These are political flows, not balance-of-payments adjustment via export/tourism rebound. The reserve-recovery test conflates two qualitatively different mechanisms.

4. **Inflation peaks are much higher than 8 ppts and persist.** The 2022-2024 episode pushed Egyptian CPI above 35% YoY at peak and core inflation remained double-digit through 2025. The 18-month dissipation criterion may be overly optimistic; the spec should test whether inflation returns to pre-event trend within 24 months as well.

5. **Tourism and Suez are confounding non-policy drivers.** The Suez Canal revenue collapsed in 2024 due to Houthi Red Sea attacks (-50% transits); tourism rebounded post-COVID then was hit by Gaza war proximity. Reserve rebuilds and BoP dynamics in the 2022-2024 window are not cleanly attributable to the floatation event.

6. **The IMF programme is the real treatment.** Each floatation was conditioned on IMF financing (2016 EFF, 2022 EFF top-up, 2024 EFF augmentation). Coding the floatation as the treatment when the IMF programme is the actual policy bundle is misspecification.

**How this should shape the v1 result card:**

- Report each episode's coefficient separately before pooling.
- Run the homogeneity test prominently and treat heterogeneity as the primary headline.
- Report reserves with-and-without estimated Gulf deposit inflows.
- Acknowledge the IMF-programme bundling explicitly.
