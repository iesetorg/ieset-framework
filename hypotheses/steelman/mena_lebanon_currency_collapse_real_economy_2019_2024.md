# Steelman — Lebanon currency collapse real-economy effect 2019-2024

**Strongest arguments against the framework's framing:**

1. **The "canonical extreme" framing risks self-fulfilling case selection.** Lebanon was selected because it is extreme; declaring 5-of-6 metrics confirms the case "is extreme" tells us nothing the selection didn't already encode. The spec needs an external benchmark — does Lebanon's collapse exceed Venezuela 2013-2020 or Zimbabwe 2007-2009 across these dimensions? Only then does "canonical-extreme" mean something.

2. **Statistics are partially fabricated or politically managed.** BdL stopped publishing some banking-sector data; CAS suspended CPI publication for periods; official GDP data is heavily disputed. Several thresholds rely on IMF Article IV staff estimates, which are themselves negotiated with Lebanese authorities. The data-foundation is weaker than the 5-of-6 framing implies.

3. **Diaspora remittances and informal-economy resilience are partially absent from headline GDP.** Lebanon's economy has long had a substantial diaspora-remittance buffer (estimated 20-30% of GDP pre-crisis) and large informal/dollarised cash economy. Headline real-GDP-pc decline overstates the welfare collapse for households with dollar income from abroad. The "65-70% household poverty" estimates are debated.

4. **The October 17 protest movement and political crisis are pre-existing institutional decay.** The collapse did not arise from a clean external shock; it was the materialisation of decades of political-elite extraction, sectarian governance, and Ponzi-style banking-sector engineering. Treating October 2019 as the treatment date risks anchor-bias; the underlying institutional rot started much earlier.

5. **Multi-rate regime measurement is fraught.** BdL has operated 4+ exchange rates (official 1507.5, customs 15000, Sayrafa, banking circulars 151/158). Computing "lira depreciation" requires choosing which rate; the unofficial market rate is the most defensible but data sources differ. The 95% threshold is met on any rate but precise magnitude is rate-dependent.

6. **Beirut Port explosion (August 2020) is a separate shock.** The port destruction caused an estimated $5-15bn damage and accelerated emigration. Attributing all 2020-2024 decline to monetary/banking collapse overlooks this independent disaster shock.

**How this should shape the v1 result card:**

- Report against external benchmarks (Venezuela, Zimbabwe) where possible.
- Disclose data-source choices and political-management caveats.
- Distinguish pre-explosion (Aug 2020) from post-explosion windows.
- Frame as "extreme historical case with substantial measurement uncertainty" rather than precision-quantified collapse.
- Acknowledge the diaspora-remittance welfare buffer as a memo item.
