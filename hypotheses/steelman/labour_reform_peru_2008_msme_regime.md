# Steelman — Peru 2008 MYPE regime

The strongest heterodox reading: the MYPE special regime is a regulatory-arbitrage tool that lowered the cost of formal status without addressing the underlying tax-and-social-security drivers of informality, so formalisation gains are largely a re-labelling of existing informal activity (microenterprise registration), not new employment. Chacaltana 2016 INEI evaluations show that MYPE-registered firms have very high rates of churn and that reported employee counts are often understated to remain within the size threshold, undermining the formal-employment share metric. The median-wage-paired outcome is the discipline: if formalisation shows up but wages also stagnate or fall, the regime has produced a low-quality formality. The 2008-2013 Peruvian copper boom is a major confound that the LATAM donor pool may not absorb. A v2 should use micro-data (ENAHO) to track within-firm transitions rather than the aggregate share, and should test whether MYPE-registered employees later transition to general-regime formal employment.
