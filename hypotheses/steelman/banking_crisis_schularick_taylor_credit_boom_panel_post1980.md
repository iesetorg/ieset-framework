# Steelman — Schularick-Taylor credit-boom panel post-1980

## The strongest opposing argument

The Schularick-Taylor result is real on the full 1870-2008 window but the post-1980 sub-sample is dominated by a small number of correlated events (Nordic 1991, Asia 1997, GFC 2008-2010, Eurozone 2010-2012). Conditional on these clusters, "credit boom predicts crisis" risks being a near-tautology: every post-1980 advanced-economy banking crisis was preceded by a credit boom because virtually every advanced-economy expansion of any duration was preceded by credit growth. The real causal question is which booms turn malign, and the credit-aggregate signal alone does not separate benign deepening from Ponzi-stage acceleration.

Selection bias also looms. Countries that liberalised credit markets are also countries that built deposit-insurance backstops, joined the Basel framework, and ran large open capital accounts — separating the credit-boom channel from the regulation-architecture channel within the post-1980 window is hard with country FE alone.

## What the framework still captures

The pre-registration commits to a specific odds-ratio threshold (>= 2.0) on a specific transformation (5-year change top quintile) on a specific outcome (5-year-forward Laeven-Valencia binary). That is enough discipline to falsify: a finding of OR ~ 1.0 would meaningfully refute the post-1980 generalisation of Schularick-Taylor, even if the full 1870-2008 result stands.
