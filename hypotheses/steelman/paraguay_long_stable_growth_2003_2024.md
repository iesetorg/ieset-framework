# Steelman — Paraguay long-stable growth 2003-2024

**The strongest argument against the case:**

The hypothesis treats Paraguay as a quiet success. The opposing view is that Paraguay's macro stability masks weak development outcomes and that the model is not generalisable.

Specific objections:

1. **Soybean monoculture and beef-export concentration is a single-shock vulnerability.** Paraguayan exports are heavily concentrated in 3-4 commodities. The 2012 drought (real-GDP fell 1.2%) and 2022 drought (real-GDP fell 0.8%) demonstrate that the apparent stability is contingent on weather. A volatility-metric averaged over 20 years smooths over these terminal shocks.

2. **Itaipú and Yacyretá hydropower revenues are not a policy success.** Paraguay's 50/50 ownership of Itaipú is fixed by treaty and the energy export to Brazil generates fiscal inflows independent of policy choices. Renegotiation of the Itaipú treaty in 2023 was politically contested but the revenue stream is largely automatic.

3. **Informality and labour-productivity stagnation.** Paraguay's informal-sector share is among the highest in Latin America (>60%). Formal-sector productivity growth has been weak. A "growth premium" that sits in commodity rents and large-project hydroelectric revenues without producing formal-sector productivity convergence is a thin success.

4. **Tax-haven adjacency carries spillover costs.** Paraguay's low effective tax regime attracts triangulation (re-export trade) and informal financial flows. The macro-stability picture obscures the underlying fragility of fiscal capacity.

5. **Institutional quality remains weak.** WGI Rule of Law and Control of Corruption scores remain in the bottom third of Latin America. The macro-stability success has not translated into institutional convergence.

**How this should shape the result card:**

(a) Report drought-year deviations explicitly.
(b) Show the Itaipú-revenue share of fiscal inflows separately.
(c) Note the informality and labour-productivity caveats in commentary.
(d) Treat the "long-stable growth" finding as descriptively true but cautiously generalisable.
