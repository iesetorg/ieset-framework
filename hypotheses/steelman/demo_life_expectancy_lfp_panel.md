# Steelman: Life expectancy gains × older-worker LFP

## Strongest case for the claim

Healthier older populations work longer; the Milligan-Wise (NBER) cross-country project
documents a robust health-LFP gradient at older ages. As life expectancy at 60 rises, so
does the share of older workers physically able to remain in the labour force. The
hypothesis should hold even controlling for statutory pension age because health enables
participation independently of policy.

## Strongest case against

(1) Pension-reform timing dominates: countries that raised retirement ages (DEU 2007, FRA
2010, GBR 2018) saw 55-64 LFP rise sharply for policy reasons. (2) Older-worker LFP is also
driven by pension wealth and savings adequacy, not health. (3) Selection: the same cohorts
benefiting from longevity gains are also the most-educated cohorts, who would work longer
anyway.

## Key uncertainties

- Whether pension-age control fully absorbs the policy channel.
- Health-quality of life-expectancy gains (healthy life years vs. raw years).

## What would change my mind

If statutory pension age fully absorbs the life-expectancy-at-60 coefficient, the residual
health-channel claim collapses.
