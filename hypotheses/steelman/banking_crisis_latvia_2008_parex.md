# Steelman — Latvia 2008 Parex bailout

## The strongest opposing argument

Latvia's 2008 crisis is often celebrated by hard-peg advocates as proving internal devaluation can work; alternatively, it is criticised by Krugman / Roubini as proving it is needlessly painful. Counting "real GDP decline >= 20%" as canonical-pattern confirmation can be read either as evidence of crisis severity (banking channel) or as evidence of unnecessary policy choice (peg defence) — the metric does not separate.

Parex specifically was an outlier: most of the Latvian banking system (Swedbank, SEB) was Swedish-parent-bank capitalised through the cross-border channel and so the headline "banking crisis" rests narrowly on the Parex nationalisation event.

## What the framework still captures

The 5-metric checklist mixes activity (GDP, unemployment), policy events (programme, Parex nationalisation), and bank-balance-sheet (credit decline) so the test is robust to the question of whether the headline distress was driven by Parex specifically or by Swedish-parent-bank deleveraging more broadly.
