# Steelman: Canada points-system immigration

## Strongest case for the claim

Canada's points system has selected on tertiary attainment for 50+ years. OECD DIOC data
consistently show Canadian foreign-born tertiary share in the top quartile of destination
countries. The system is the prototype for skill-based migration policy.

## Strongest case against

Canada's foreign-born tertiary premium may reflect English-language origin-country
selection (UK, India, Philippines) rather than the points system per se. Comparator
selection drives the result.

## Key uncertainties

- Selection vs. points-system causal share.
- Comparison to AUS/NZL (also points-based) vs traditional family-reunification systems.

## What would change my mind

If Canada's foreign-born tertiary share is similar to Anglo non-points-system destinations
(USA, GBR), the points-system causal effect is small.
