# Steelman: US EITC Cumulative Effect 1975-2020

The strongest counter is that EITC's documented effects rest disproportionately on the 1986 and 1993 expansions, both of which coincided with major macroeconomic and welfare-policy changes that contaminate identification of the EITC channel specifically. The 1996 PRWORA reform interacted with EITC expansions in ways that joint-modeled estimates struggle to disentangle, and the 2008-2010 GFC depressed labour-market opportunities for single mothers regardless of the 2009 ARRA EITC expansion. The cumulative-effect framing also treats post-2000 expansions as additive to earlier rounds, but diminishing-returns and target-group saturation suggest the marginal effect of each subsequent expansion is smaller than headline cumulative attribution implies. EITC's labour-supply effects are also concentrated on the extensive margin and may have offsetting intensive-margin effects (single mothers reduce hours to remain in the EITC plateau region) that net out in aggregate LFP measurement (Eissa-Hoynes 2006). A defensible reading: EITC produced meaningful but more modest cumulative effects than the panel-FE 6pp/4pp claim suggests, with effective causal share concentrated in the 1986-1993 window rather than smoothly accumulating across all expansions.
