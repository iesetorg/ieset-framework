# Steelman - currency_monetisation_consumer_price_effect

A fair test of this hypothesis should treat the claim as a falsifiable empirical statement, not as a slogan. The strongest version asks whether the registered outcome moves in the predicted direction under the stated sample, controls, and estimator, while allowing the result to be partial or refuted when the data do not support the claim.

Main alternative explanation: observed movement may reflect omitted institutions, business-cycle timing, measurement error, reverse causality, or country-specific shocks rather than the registered treatment. A credible replication should therefore report sample coverage, coefficient direction, uncertainty, and whether the chosen proxy really captures the named mechanism.
