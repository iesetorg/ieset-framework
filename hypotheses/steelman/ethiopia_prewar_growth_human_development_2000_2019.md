# Steelman: Ethiopia pre-war growth and human development

The strongest caution is measurement and durability. Ethiopia's official GDP
growth series has been debated, and the pre-2020 window excludes the conflict
and macro-financial stress that followed. Health improvements can reflect
baseline catch-up, donor-financed public health, and demographic transition
rather than the broader developmental-state model. A supported result should
therefore be interpreted as a pre-war descriptive pattern, not as proof of a
robust or politically sustainable growth regime.
