# Steelman — China LGFV debt evolution 2015-2024

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Counterpart to real assets.** LGFV debt funded subway lines, high-speed rail, urban infrastructure, and industrial parks — much of which produces utilisation value (subways carrying passengers, rail moving freight). Net-worth stress is overstated when only the liability side is measured.

2. **Currency mismatch is minimal.** Chinese debt is overwhelmingly RMB-denominated and held domestically. The Argentine / Greek / Turkish playbook for sovereign distress (FX mismatch, foreign holders) does not apply. Quantity-of-debt comparisons across countries are misleading when currency-of-issue differs.

3. **Capital controls + financial repression backstop.** China can socialise LGFV debt via the banking system at managed cost (deposit-rate cap, low household financial returns, four big SOE banks acting as utilities). The "debt overhang" framing presumes a market-clearing mechanism that does not bind.

## What the framework still captures

The descriptive test is on debt LEVELS, not on a sustainability prediction. Whether 30pp rise in corporate debt is "dangerous" or "fine" is a separate hypothesis (e.g. a future banking-stress canonical-case test). The current test only records the structural rise; downstream hypotheses can probe whether the rise translates into growth drag, banking stress, or inflation. Pre-registering the level test prevents motivated post-hoc recoding.
