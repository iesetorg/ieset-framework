# Steelman — India Aadhaar/UPI productivity gains

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Financial inclusion is not productivity.** Account ownership and digital-payment penetration are intermediate outcomes. The connection from "more bank accounts" to "higher TFP" requires productive use of credit and savings, not merely possession of accounts. Many JAM accounts are zero-balance.

2. **Smartphone penetration confound.** Peer EMs (Brazil PIX, Mexico CoDi, Kenya M-Pesa) had similar financial-inclusion booms over 2011-2021 driven by smartphone diffusion, not state digital-ID. The peer-differential test absorbs some of this but not all — Brazil's PIX (2020) is a closer analogue.

3. **Privacy / state-capacity costs.** Aadhaar centralises biometric data with weak legal protections. The productivity gain may come at a state-power cost not captured in the Findex measure.

## What the framework still captures

Findex is a high-quality cross-national measure with consistent methodology. The +20pp peer-differential threshold is calibrated to demonstrate India outperforms the EM trend, not just rides it. Productivity attribution is acknowledged as harder; the TFP-growth informative metric is reported but not dispositive. The hypothesis tests the observable outputs of the digital-stack rather than the harder TFP transmission.
