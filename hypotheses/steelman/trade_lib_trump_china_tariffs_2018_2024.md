# Steelman — Trump-Biden China tariffs 2018-2024

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Tariff-substitution-via-Vietnam/Mexico is itself trade
   diversion.** The aggregate-invariance pattern partly reflects
   transshipment and re-export through third countries (Chinese
   final goods entering via Vietnam or Mexico assembly), which
   means the policy has not actually reduced US-China economic
   integration; it has just hidden it.

2. **Welfare costs are large despite aggregate invariance.**
   Pass-through analysis shows tariff incidence fell on US
   importers and consumers, generating welfare losses that the
   trade-share metric is silent on.

3. **Industrial-policy effects are emerging post-2022.** The CHIPS
   Act, IRA, and bipartisan supply-chain-resilience push are
   layered on top of the tariff regime; isolating the tariff
   effect from the industrial-policy effect is hard.

## What the framework still captures

The aggregate-invariance prediction is conservative and falsifiable.
The composition-shift narrative is documented in the disclosure
without requiring the spec to test it on WDI data alone. Welfare
and industrial-policy effects are explicitly cross-referenced to
sibling specs.
