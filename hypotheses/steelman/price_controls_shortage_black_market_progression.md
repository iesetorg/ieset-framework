# Steelman — Temporary price controls have a legitimate stabilisation role

**The strongest arguments for price controls and against the full causal chain:**

1. **Short-window, narrow-basket price controls can prevent opportunistic pricing during supply shocks.** Hungary's 2022 food-fuel caps and many Covid-era essential-goods caps were intended as temporary measures during a specific price-shock, not as a permanent allocation regime. Within short windows and narrow baskets, the second/third-order chain has no time to materialise, and the first-order consumer protection is real. The hypothesis as stated risks conflating regime-level price controls (Venezuela LPJ) with narrow-temporary ones (HUN 2022).

2. **Price-gouging laws are universal and largely uncontroversial.** Most US states have anti-price-gouging statutes active during declared emergencies; these are price controls in a technical sense. They demonstrably reduce post-disaster exploitation without producing sustained black markets. A blanket "price controls cause the chain" claim is inconsistent with this widely-used policy tool.

3. **Venezuela's collapse is a monetary and oil-collapse story primarily, not a price-control story.** Venezuelan hyperinflation, currency collapse, and supply-chain breakdown were driven by fiscal dominance, PDVSA collapse, and sanctions. Attributing the "shortages + black market + welfare loss" chain to the LPJ specifically rather than to the macro collapse is an attribution reach; the control spec should separate these.

4. **Turkey's 2022 food-price caps were short, narrow, and politically theatrical more than binding.** Enforcement was sporadic, exceptions were widespread, and substitution between specifically-capped SKUs and un-capped variants was trivially easy. To count Turkey as a clean test of the causal chain overreaches; the "intervention" was weak and the second/third-order effects are hard to attribute.

5. **Argentina's Precios Cuidados was a negotiated-list rather than a statutory cap.** The program negotiated price freezes on a specific basket of agreed items with the largest retailers; un-listed goods floated freely. This is closer to a government-business accommodation than a classical price ceiling. Treating it as equivalent to VEN LPJ conflates regime types.

6. **Black-market premia are driven by foreign-exchange controls more than by goods-price controls.** In VEN, ARG, TUR, ZWE, parallel-market premia on CURRENCY (dolartoday, blue dollar, kara para) dominate the goods-price parallel markets. The identifier "price control causes black market" often measures FX control effects that co-move with goods controls without being caused by them.

7. **Availability data during price-control regimes is self-reported and unreliable.** VEN shelf-availability data from Datanalisis was heavily politicised; TUR food-availability statistics are noisy; HUN's retail-sales data shows some substitution but minimal absolute shortages. Distinguishing a real supply contraction from reporting bias is hard, particularly when the regimes that impose controls also control data release.

8. **Welfare calculus depends on distributional weights.** Even if aggregate welfare falls by X, if the first-order gain accrues to low-income households who face binding liquidity constraints and the second/third-order cost falls on middle/upper-income households willing to pay a premium, distributionally-weighted welfare can be positive. Utility-weighted accounting is not the same as aggregate surplus accounting.

**How this should shape the v1 result card when run:**

- Disentangle narrow-temporary price caps (HUN 2022, TUR 2022 food only) from regime-level price control (VEN LPJ).
- Separate FX controls from goods-price controls explicitly; do not attribute goods-parallel-market effects to goods-controls alone.
- Flag data reliability concerns per country (VEN especially), and report only triangulated figures.
- Report the aggregate welfare-loss estimate with a distributional-weight sensitivity analysis.
- Be conservative about attribution for the Venezuelan case: the macro collapse is doing most of the work.
- Do not blanket-reject price controls on the basis of the hypothesis; scope findings to regime-level binding controls lasting more than 18 months.
