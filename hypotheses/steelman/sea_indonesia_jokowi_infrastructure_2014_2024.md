# Steelman — Indonesia Jokowi infrastructure 2014-2024

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Commodity-cycle confound.** Indonesia is a major coal, palm-oil, nickel exporter. 2014-2019 was a commodity-price recovery period; 2021-2024 saw nickel boom from EV-battery demand. The peer-panel-differential test absorbs ASEAN common cycle but Indonesia's commodity exposure is distinctive.

2. **Capital-output ratio rising.** A +2pp GFCF share with no proportional growth gain implies a falling productivity of capital. Indonesian infrastructure may be eating capital at lower returns than the prior baseline.

3. **Nusantara is sunk-cost speculative.** The new-capital project is a large bet with uncertain payoff. Including it as a "success" channel is premature.

## What the framework still captures

The two-test structure separates input commitment (GFCF share) from output realisation (growth differential). If commodity cycle drove growth without infrastructure, GFCF share would not rise commensurately. If infrastructure push absorbed capital without payoff, growth differential would not appear. Both conditions together provide reasonable inference.
