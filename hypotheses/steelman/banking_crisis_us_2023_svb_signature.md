# Steelman — US 2023 SVB/Signature

## The strongest opposing argument

The March 2023 episode is too short (4-8 weeks of acute stress) and too contained (no Laeven-Valencia coding, no GDP impact, no broad credit contraction) to count as a "banking crisis" in the panel sense alongside GFC 2008 or Lebanon 2019. Including it stretches the category. A more defensible read is that the 2023 episode is a classical liquidity-run failure mode (uninsured deposits running on duration-mismatched balance sheets) and the BTFP backstop short-circuited the propagation path.

The negative-control framing (real GDP undisturbed) is also somewhat circular: if the framework says "the test is supported when the canonical pattern holds AND the macro signature is small", almost any policy-contained banking event will support it.

## What the framework still captures

Pre-registering that the 2023 episode is testable as a duration-mismatch + liquidity-backstop case rather than a credit-boom-bust + systemic-output-decline case is useful taxonomic discipline. The metric structure makes the contrast with GFC and Iceland explicit.
