# Steelman — India Modi-era growth 2014-2024

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Sub-window evidence supports acceleration.** 2014-2019 real GDP growth averaged ~7.0% vs 2004-2013 ~6.6% (in PPP-pc terms the gap is similar). The COVID years 2020-2022 are the only window where growth dropped, and 2023-2024 has rebounded to 7-8%. Including COVID drag in the "post" mean is a methodological choice that biases toward the no-acceleration finding.

2. **Quality of growth improved.** Even if levels are similar, the Modi-era growth shifted toward formal-sector employment (GST formalisation), reduced informal-cash dependence (UPI digitisation), and reduced bad-loan stockpile (IBC). These are structural improvements that don't show in headline GDP.

3. **Demographic dividend timing.** India's working-age share peaked in this window. The "Modi-era growth = UPA-era growth" framing under-credits the policy effort because the demographic tailwind was strongest pre-2014 and is now plateauing.

## What the framework still captures

The pre-registered test is symmetric: it can SUPPORT continuation (gap ≤ 0.5pp) or REFUTE via the +1.5pp acceleration threshold. The 2014-2019 sub-window is reported as informative. Channel decomposition (formalisation, financialisation) is the subject of separate hypotheses (Aadhaar/UPI, demonetisation). The continuation hypothesis is honest about what aggregate growth shows.
