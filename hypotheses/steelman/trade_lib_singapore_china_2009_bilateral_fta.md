# Steelman — Singapore-China FTA 2009 null hypothesis

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Aggregate ratios miss bilateral composition shifts.** The
   FTA may have shifted Singapore's bilateral trade composition
   toward China without changing aggregate trade-to-GDP ratios.
   Bilateral merchandise statistics would tell a different story.

2. **Service-trade and FDI channels.** The FTA included
   service-sector commitments and investment provisions; effects
   on Singapore as a Chinese-investment hub are not captured by
   merchandise trade ratios.

3. **HKG is an imperfect comparator.** Hong Kong has its own
   special relationship with the mainland (CEPA 2003) which
   provides preferential access; it is not a clean control.

## What the framework still captures

The descriptive null is robust to these critiques: it tests a
specific aggregate prediction and documents the result. Bilateral
composition and service-trade channels deserve their own specs;
this one explicitly scopes to aggregate trade-to-GDP and is
honest about that limitation.
