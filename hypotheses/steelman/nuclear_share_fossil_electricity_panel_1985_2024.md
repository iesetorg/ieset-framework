# Steelman: Nuclear share and fossil electricity displacement

Nuclear generation can reduce fossil generation, but endpoint comparisons are
not causal. Countries that built nuclear also changed industrial structure,
hydro capacity, gas access, demand growth, and climate policy. Fossil shares can
fall because total demand grows slowly or because hydro/renewables expand at the
same time. A supported descriptive result should be interpreted as a substitution
pattern in countries with rising nuclear shares, not as an isolated causal effect.
