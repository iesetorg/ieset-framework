# Steelman: Italy/Spain demographic stagnation

## Strongest case for the claim

ITA and ESP TFRs fell below 1.5 in the late 1980s/early 1990s. By the mid-2010s, working-age
contraction set in even before completed retirement of the boomer cohort. Decomposing
post-2008 per-capita growth gap to EU-15 should reveal a meaningful working-age share
contribution; a clean panel FE decomposition can separate this from productivity and capital.

## Strongest case against

Both countries also have severe productivity problems unrelated to demographics: ITA's TFP
has been falling since 1995 (Hall-Pessoa-Saliola); ESP has financial-cycle damage from
2008-2013 plus structural unemployment of 15%+. Demographic share of growth gap could be
swamped by these channels.

## Key uncertainties

- Productivity vs demographics relative contribution.
- Migration absorption (ESP received large inflows 1995-2008 that reversed post-crisis).

## What would change my mind

If working-age channel accounts for less than 10% of the growth gap, the demographic-
stagnation framing is too strong; the story is about productivity and crisis legacy.
