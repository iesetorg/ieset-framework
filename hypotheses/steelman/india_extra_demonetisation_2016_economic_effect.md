# Steelman — India demonetisation 2016 economic effect

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Formalisation jumpstart.** Demonetisation accelerated UPI adoption, formal banking, and digital payment infrastructure. The 2017-2024 surge in UPI volume (now world's largest real-time payment system) was plausibly catalysed by the 2016 cash crunch. A short-run growth dip may be a small price for medium-run formalisation gains.

2. **Tax base broadening.** GST (rolled out July 2017) plus demonetisation jointly produced tax-base expansion. Direct tax filers nearly doubled from ~37m (FY15) to ~73m (FY19). Tax-revenue / GDP did rise, though modestly.

3. **Counterfactual is ambiguous.** Disentangling demonetisation effect from concurrent shocks (GST rollout, NPA cleanup, global goods cycle) is non-trivial. Annual GDP-growth dip in FY16-17 is small (~0.5pp) and could reflect any of these.

## What the framework still captures

The dispositive test is on the short-run growth dip plus the medium-run tax-revenue channel. If the policy had a clear payoff, tax-revenue / GDP should rise visibly within 3 years. If the dip is real but the payoff is missing, the policy is at most a forced formalisation that paid in transitional cost. Pre-registering the no-payoff threshold prevents post-hoc upgrade of "formalisation gains" claims that may be untestable.
