# Steelman — ZLB state-dependent multiplier under PK framing

**The strongest argument against this hypothesis:**

The PK and NK readings of the ZLB-multiplier gap are observationally equivalent at the level of estimated impulse responses. A higher multiplier at the ZLB is consistent with both the Christiano-Eichenbaum-Rebelo log-linearised result (ZLB binds, no monetary offset) and the PK functional-finance reading (slack-regime demand creation). The hypothesis cannot distinguish them; it can only confirm the gap exists.

Specific objections the hypothesis must engage with:

1. **Mechanism agnosticism is unavoidable.** Both schools predict the same impulse response on real GDP, real consumption, and unemployment to a fiscal shock at the ZLB. The PK framing adds the prediction that the multiplier should remain high even when the central bank is operationally able to offset (e.g. before the ZLB binds, or above the ZLB if monetary policy is committed not to tighten); the NK framing predicts the multiplier-gap closes as soon as the ZLB stops binding. The test does not exploit this distinguishing prediction because most identified ZLB episodes coincide with active ZLB regimes.

2. **The cumulative-multiplier construction is sensitive to specification.** Ramey-Zubairy (2018) showed that earlier high-multiplier-at-ZLB findings (Auerbach-Gorodnichenko 2012, Blanchard-Leigh 2013) were sensitive to lag specification, sample window, and the particular fiscal-shock identification. Some specifications produce multiplier gaps below 0.5 even on the same data. The threshold (gap ≥ 0.5) the spec sets is at the median of the literature; reproduction is partly a function of which lag structure is used.

3. **OECD heterogeneity.** Within the panel, USA, JPN, GBR, EUR-Big4 had different ZLB durations and different fiscal-stance trajectories. ARRA 2009 and COVID-2020 are the only large clean shocks for USA; Japan has a longer ZLB period but smaller identified fiscal innovations. The panel result is driven by the largest economies; small-country contributions are noisy.

4. **Anchored expectations is doing work.** The spec requires inflation expectations ≤ 2.5% to enter the ZLB regime. This restriction is a methodological strength but it embeds the result: at slack with anchored expectations, both PK and NK schools predict high multipliers. The test cannot speak to what would happen at slack with unanchored expectations, which is where the PK functional-finance reading would diverge most sharply from NK.

5. **Heterogeneity in fiscal-shock composition.** Government consumption shocks (Ramey series) and tax shocks (Romer-Romer) produce different multiplier estimates; aggregating fiscal innovations into a single "shock" obscures this. The PK framing predicts that transfer-driven and public-investment-driven multipliers differ from defence-procurement multipliers; the test does not separate these.

**How this steelman should shape the result card:**

The result card must:

(a) Report multiplier gap with both narrative and forecast-error identification; concur if both methods produce gap ≥ 0.5.

(b) Decompose the panel result by country to identify whether the multiplier-gap is driven by USA, JPN, EUR, or all jointly.

(c) Note explicitly that the PK and NK readings cannot be distinguished by this test; the result supports either school.

(d) Report sub-period analysis (1995-2007 normal regime; 2008-2014 ZLB-anchored; 2015-2019 lift-off; 2020-2021 COVID-ZLB).

(e) Run sensitivity to the anchored-expectations cutoff (2.0%, 2.5%, 3.0%) and to the lag structure (4, 8, 12 quarters cumulative).
