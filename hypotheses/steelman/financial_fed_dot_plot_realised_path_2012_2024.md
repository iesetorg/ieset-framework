# Steelman — Fed dot plot vs realised path 2012-2024

## The strongest opposing argument

Comparing dot-plot 2-year-ahead projections to realised rates as if they were point forecasts is unfair: the SEP is officially a "modal projection conditional on appropriate policy" and reflects each member's view of what they would do given their economic forecast, not a unconditional rate forecast. Large differences between dot and realised path are therefore expected when the underlying economic data turns out differently from the forecast — that does not mean forward guidance failed.

The 2020-2021 pandemic and 2022 inflation surge are exogenous shocks that no rate-projection process could have anticipated; including them in the average inflates the headline error.

## What the framework still captures

The descriptive measurement of "did the dot-plot 2-year-ahead point forecast match the realised path" is what market participants and households actually use the dot plot for, regardless of how it is officially framed. A 100 bp average absolute error is a meaningful threshold — modest enough that low-noise periods (2014-2019 stable) could clear it, but loose enough that the spec does not pre-bake the conclusion that the dot plot is useless.
