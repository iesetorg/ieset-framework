# Steelman - heritage_financial_freedom_female_lfp_current_gap

The strongest pro-market interpretation is that Heritage financial freedom captures a real policy-regime difference: prices, entry, contracts, and capital allocation face fewer distortions in the high-score countries, so higher market scores should be associated with better female labour-force participation.

The strongest objection is that this is a cross-section. Richer countries may be able to afford better institutions, outcomes may feed back into policy scores, and geography, demography, resource endowments, or state capacity may confound the comparison. Treat this as a triage result until a panel, event, synthetic-control, or instrumented version is registered.
