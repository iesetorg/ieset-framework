# Steelman — Colombia-US FTA 2012

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Pre-existing access matters.** ATPDEA gave Colombian exporters
   substantial duty-free access to the US for two decades before
   the FTA. The formal agreement codified rather than expanded
   access.

2. **Oil-price volatility.** Colombia's exports are dominated by
   petroleum and coal; the 2014-2016 commodity collapse contaminates
   the post-treatment evaluation window.

3. **Distributional effects matter.** US-subsidised agricultural
   imports displaced Colombian rural production — the welfare cost
   is real but invisible in trade-openness ratios.

## What the framework still captures

The null is the right prediction and the descriptive test handles
it cleanly. Distributional and sectoral effects deserve separate
specs.
