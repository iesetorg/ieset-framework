# Steelman: Japan refusal-of-immigration counterfactual

## Strongest case for the claim

Japan's net migration rate has been near-zero through most of 1990-2020 while peers
absorbed substantial inflows. The mechanical counterfactual (apply OECD-mean migration
rate) yields a substantially different working-age trajectory. The implied growth gap is a
clean accounting exercise.

## Strongest case against

The counterfactual ignores absorption frictions, social cohesion costs, and Japan's
revealed preference for capital-deepening / automation as alternative path. The mechanical
exercise overstates the realisable gap.

## Key uncertainties

- Realisable migration substitute rate.

## What would change my mind

If a more conservative counterfactual (Japan-specific feasible migration rate, not OECD
mean) shrinks the gap to <2pp, the framing should be moderated.
