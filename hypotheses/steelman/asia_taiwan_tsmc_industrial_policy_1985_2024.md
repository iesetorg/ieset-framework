# Steelman — Taiwan TSMC industrial policy 1985-2024

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Geopolitical/security exception.** Taiwan's outsized R&D commitment is partly defence-driven (silicon shield logic). Treating it as generic industrial-policy success conflates security spending with growth policy.

2. **Successor risk.** TSMC's dominance is concentrated in a single firm and a single set of fabs. Concentration-by-design is a single-point-of-failure that does not reproduce a "broad industrial-policy" success — it produces a national champion.

3. **Selection bias in success stories.** Taiwan's success is highly publicised; failed industrial policies in similar small open economies (Argentina FONDEF, various African chip-fab attempts, Hungarian electronics) are not similarly headlined. The case is real but generalisable lessons are limited.

## What the framework still captures

The multi-metric checklist commits to six independent thresholds that span sectoral share, R&D, trade composition, income level, productivity, and industry-share. If only the headline TSMC dominance metric matched and the others didn't, the case would refute. The pattern-match approach handles canonical-case identification stronger than any single-channel test.
