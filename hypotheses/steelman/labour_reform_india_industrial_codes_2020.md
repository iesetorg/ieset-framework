# Steelman — India industrial labour codes 2020

The strongest counter-reading: the four Codes have not been operationalised at the centre even five years after enactment, and state-level rule notification has been a paper exercise without enforcement-machinery deployment. The treatment is therefore largely nominal, and any state-level DiD signal is likely to be picking up underlying state-economic trajectory rather than Code-implementation effect. CITU and AITUC analyses argue the Codes consolidated existing statutes without resolving the binding constraint on Indian labour formality (Industrial Disputes Act Chapter VB requirement to obtain government permission to dismiss workers in firms over 100 employees, which the IR Code retained at 300 with state opt-out potential). Since most state-rule notifications retained substantive provisions of prior law with minor procedural changes, the spec's identification of "treatment" is weak. A v2 should use the Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS) to construct micro-level firm-size-distribution responses around the size-threshold change in early-implementing states — that gives a sharp regression-discontinuity on the binding-margin firm-size-dismissal-permission threshold and is the cleaner test.
