# Steelman — South Africa NMW 2018

The strongest disemployment-prediction reading: South Africa's NMW at ~62% of median is well above the canonical 40-50% range where the modern Card-Krueger-Cengiz null effects hold. Bhorat-Kanbur-Stanwix 2017 ex-ante evaluations predicted significant disemployment in agriculture and domestic services where the wage floor was binding. The post-2019 South African unemployment rate rose to historic highs (>34%) and labour-force participation fell — both of which a synthetic-control donor pool that absorbs COVID may understate. NIDS and QLFS panel data show declines in domestic-worker employment specifically, consistent with the high-bite hypothesis. The bottom-decile wage outcome is the discipline: if real wages did not actually rise at the bottom (because of evasion via informalisation or non-compliance), the claim that the minimum wage is binding fails on its own terms. South Africa's enforcement infrastructure is weak and enforcement may bind less than the legal floor. A v2 should use sector-level event studies on agriculture and domestic-work specifically — the sectors with the highest pre-2019 fraction of workers below the new floor — for cleaner identification.
