# Steelman: Singapore CPF 2013-2024 Reforms

The strongest counter is that Singapore's elderly-poverty rate was overstated by the OECD harmonised methodology in 2014 due to non-comparability with CPF withdrawal-based wealth that does not appear as income in standard surveys. Adjusted for owner-occupied housing flows and CPF balance imputation, true elderly material deprivation in SGP was always lower than the OECD 41% headline suggests. The 2013-2024 reform sequence's apparent adequacy improvement therefore reflects measurement-correction as much as substantive policy effect. Silver Support Scheme covers only the bottom 30% of seniors and at modest amounts; CPF Life payout schedules remain criticised as insufficient for older cohorts with thin contribution histories due to interrupted female-labour-force participation. The 2025+ debate within the PAP government over whether CPF-Retirement-Sum increases pace housing-cost and healthcare-cost inflation suggests the adequacy-gap is not closed. A defensible reading: the 2013-2024 reforms are real and improved targeting at the lowest-income tail, but the headline 15pp+ elderly-poverty reduction depends substantially on measurement-methodology choices that pre-OECD-harmonised data did not surface.
