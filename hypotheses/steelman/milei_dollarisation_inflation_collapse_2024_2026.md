# Steelman — Milei dollarisation + inflation collapse 2024-2026

**The strongest argument against the causal claim:**

The hypothesis is that Milei's fiscal-surplus + monetary-contraction + de-facto-dollarisation
package collapsed Argentine inflation, eliminated the primary deficit, and compressed the
parallel-market gap without a catastrophic recession. The opposing view is that the early-2024
inflation collapse is largely a one-off price-level adjustment after the Dec-2023 maxi-devaluation,
that the fiscal surplus is unsustainable past the 2025 mid-terms, and that the parallel-market
compression depends on still-binding capital controls rather than genuine monetary credibility.

Specific objections the hypothesis must engage:

1. **The early disinflation is mechanical, not credibility-based.** The Dec-2023 peso devaluation
   from ~365 to ~800 instantly cleared the import-and-energy-subsidy backlog and produced a
   one-shot relative-price adjustment. Monthly inflation fell from ~25% to ~4% partly because
   the price-level shock had already happened and CPI carry had been front-loaded. A
   credibility-based disinflation would show falling inflation expectations alongside; the
   inflation expectations data (REM survey) lag the realised inflation, suggesting the public is
   still pricing in regime risk.

2. **Fiscal surplus depends on inflationary erosion of nominal pension obligations.** The 2024
   primary surplus was achieved partly by allowing real pensions and salaries to fall (the
   "licuadora" effect — high inflation eroding nominal-anchored fiscal liabilities). Once
   inflation falls and indexation kicks in fully, the same fiscal effort becomes substantially
   harder to maintain. A fiscal surplus produced by inflation-tax-on-pensioners is not the same
   as a structural fiscal correction.

3. **Cepo relaxation is incomplete; parallel-market compression is partial.** The blue-dolar gap
   compressed from > 100% to ~10-20% in 2024-2025 but the cepo (capital controls) was only
   partially lifted. A clean test of dollarisation credibility would require full cepo removal
   and observation of the gap response. The current "compressed gap under controls" state is
   not equivalent to an open-capital-account stabilisation.

4. **Output cost may exceed pre-registered threshold.** Real GDP fell ~3.5% peak-to-trough
   through 2024Q3 — close to the 4% threshold. If the recession deepens or the recovery
   stalls, the multi-metric verdict could flip from supported to mixed. The 2026 mid-terms are
   the critical test: a Peronist-coalition return to congress would re-price fiscal-sustainability
   risk and could unwind the FX-gap compression overnight.

5. **Comparison to Convertibilidad (1991-2001) is unfavourable.** Convertibilidad also collapsed
   inflation, ran fiscal surpluses for several years, and compressed the parallel gap to zero
   — and ended in the largest sovereign default in history. The structural-features argument
   (the same un-financeable provincial-fiscal-and-pension-system that broke Convertibilidad
   still exists) suggests the Milei stabilisation may be successful through 2026 but
   structurally unstable beyond.

**How this steelman should shape the result card:**

The v1 result card must:

(a) Report monthly inflation and *real-pension-purchasing-power* together — a stabilisation
    that runs a fiscal surplus partly via real-pension-erosion is a different policy than a
    fiscal-correction-via-spending-restraint package.

(b) Include the cepo-status indicator on the FX-gap chart. A compressed gap under capital
    controls is not equivalent to an open-account stabilisation.

(c) Report output cost and inflation gain on the same axis (sacrifice ratio) — Milei's output
    cost per point of inflation reduction is a meaningful metric versus historical
    stabilisations (Volcker, post-1993 Brazil-Plano-Real, post-2002 Brazil).

(d) Flag the 2025 mid-term election as a structural-instability inflection point. The
    multi-metric verdict in 2026 may need a 2027 re-run to determine sustainability.

(e) Accept that "supported on inflation collapse, mixed on output, uncertain on durability"
    is the most likely v1 verdict and that the policy takeaway is "shock therapy works for
    nominal anchoring; durability is a separate hypothesis."
