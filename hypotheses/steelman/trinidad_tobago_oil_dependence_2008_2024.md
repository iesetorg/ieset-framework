# Steelman — Trinidad and Tobago oil dependence 2008-2024

**The strongest argument against:**

The hypothesis frames TTO as a price-cycle-dominated economy. The opposing view is that TTO has more diversification than the resource-curse frame suggests, that the Heritage Stabilisation Fund worked as designed, and that the post-2014 contraction reflects upstream-reserve depletion rather than policy failure.

Specific objections:

1. **Diversification through downstream petrochemicals.** TTO has substantial methanol, ammonia, urea, and steel manufacturing built on cheap natural-gas feedstock. This is a real diversification of value-added; treating it as undifferentiated "hydrocarbon dependence" understates the economic complexity.

2. **HSF performed its design function.** The Heritage and Stabilisation Fund cushioned the 2015-2020 fiscal adjustment; without it, the fiscal contraction would have been sharper and procyclical.

3. **Reserve depletion is the structural story.** TTO upstream gas production declined from ~4.2 bcf/d (2010) to ~2.6 bcf/d (2023) — a real geological constraint, not a Dutch-disease artefact. The post-2014 macro trajectory may be reserve-driven more than price-driven.

4. **Currency stability held.** The TTD-USD rate has been stable; central-bank reserves remain adequate; no balance-of-payments crisis manifested. The "small-economy fragility" frame may be over-stated.

5. **Caribbean peer comparison is selective.** Excluding hydrocarbon peers (Suriname, Guyana) from the donor pool understates the structural similarity of resource-economy trajectories.

**Result-card implications:**

(a) Decompose hydrocarbon vs petrochemical value-added.
(b) Show HSF balance trajectory.
(c) Report upstream-reserve depletion alongside price.
(d) Acknowledge the post-2024 Dragon-field cross-border-gas options as policy-relevant tail.
