# Steelman - heritage_financial_freedom_electricity_access_income_region_robustness

The strongest pro-market interpretation is that Heritage financial freedom captures an institutional or policy-regime feature that matters even among countries with similar income levels and within similar broad regions, so it should still predict electricity access after those controls.

The strongest objection is over-control and omitted-variable instability: GDP per capita is itself partly downstream of institutions, while region fixed effects are coarse and can absorb meaningful historical/institutional variation. A causal version needs panel timing, reform episodes, instruments, or synthetic comparisons.
