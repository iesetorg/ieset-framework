# Steelman — EU 2007/2013 Balkan enlargement

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Emigration confound.** Bulgaria and Romania lost roughly 10-15%
   of their working-age population to Western European labour
   markets between 2007 and 2019. Per-capita GDP rises mechanically
   when the denominator falls; this is not a welfare gain. The
   spec's outcome (log GDP per capita) is partially mechanical.

2. **Crisis-window confound.** Both 2007 and 2013 accessions
   coincided with global recession or eurozone crisis. Any
   measured ATT mixes the accession effect with crisis-recovery
   dynamics; identifying the "pure" accession effect is
   genuinely hard.

3. **Croatia's tourism dependence.** Croatia's post-2013
   trajectory is dominated by tourism receipts (single-market
   access matters here), making the Croatian estimate a
   sector-specific story rather than a general accession effect.

## What the framework still captures

The relative-magnitude prediction (2007/2013 ATT < 2004 ATT) is
the right comparative test and is robust to most of these
critiques: emigration affected 2004 cohorts too, the GFC was
common, and the prediction is about the difference between
cohorts rather than the absolute level. The PARTIAL verdict path
(positive but similar magnitude) operationalises a heterodox
reading where institutional convergence dominates the crisis
timing.
