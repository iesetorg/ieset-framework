# Steelman — Israel 2008 minimum-wage rise

The strongest disemployment-prediction reading: the 2008-2011 Israeli minimum-wage rise occurred during a period of heavy non-compliance, particularly among Arab-Israeli employers and small-firm employers, so the population-level employment effect appears null because the wage floor wasn't actually binding for the marginal employer. Bank of Israel research division 2014 evaluations document substantial under-payment relative to the legal floor, particularly in the hospitality, construction, and domestic-service sectors. A clean test would distinguish formal-sector employment (where the floor binds) from informal/non-compliant employment (where it doesn't). The synthetic-control gap on aggregate employment may therefore be observationally consistent with a large disemployment effect among compliant firms and an offsetting expansion of non-compliant employment. The bottom-quintile wage outcome is the discipline: if compliant-firm wages rose but the bottom quintile shows no gain, non-compliance has substituted for legal employment. A v2 should use Bituach Leumi (National Insurance) administrative data which captures only compliant employment and would isolate the disemployment effect on legally-employed workers.
