# Steelman: Higher-ed expansion × growth (Korea, Taiwan, Ireland)

## Strongest case for the claim

KOR/TWN/IRL achieved both rapid tertiary-attainment expansion and rapid per-capita growth.
The cross-country panel allows separating these two countries' performance from comparators
who expanded at similar pace. Human-capital theory predicts a measurable channel.

## Strongest case against

KOR/TWN growth was overdetermined (industrial policy, savings, exports); IRL growth was
overdetermined (FDI inflows, EU access, low CT rate). Tertiary expansion was correlated
with these but may not be the driving channel.

## Key uncertainties

- Channel decomposition.

## What would change my mind

If country fixed effects are not above comparator-mean even with tertiary attainment
controlled, the human-capital story is overstated.
