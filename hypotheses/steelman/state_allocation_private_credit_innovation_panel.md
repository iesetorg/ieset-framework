# Steelman — state_allocation_private_credit_innovation_panel

The strongest counterargument is that government consumption is an imprecise
state-allocation proxy. High public spending can finance education, courts,
health, infrastructure, and basic research that complement private credit and
innovation rather than crowd them out.

Private-credit depth can also be excessive in credit booms, so more private
credit is not always better capital allocation. The relationship may be
nonlinear and institution-dependent.

A fair verdict should treat this as a broad market-process screen. Stronger
evidence would use directed-credit shares, state-bank ownership, procurement
allocation, and exogenous reform timing.
