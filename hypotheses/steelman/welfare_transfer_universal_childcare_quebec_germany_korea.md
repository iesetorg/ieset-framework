# Steelman: Universal Childcare Maternal LFP Effect — Quebec, Germany, Korea

The strongest counter is that the three jurisdictions exhibit fundamentally different identification problems and pooling them as cross-jurisdiction replication overstates evidential weight. Quebec's 1997 reform was launched alongside major restructuring of family-allowance system, making the childcare effect hard to disentangle from concurrent transfers — Lefebvre-Merrigan (2008) flagged this and the long-horizon Baker-Gruber-Milligan (2019) update found persistent negative child-development effects that complicate the welfare-evaluation framing even if maternal LFP rose. Germany's U3 Ausbau is contaminated by simultaneous Elterngeld parental-benefit reform 2007 and was rolled out unevenly across states, with eastern-state pre-treatment baselines reflecting GDR-legacy childcare-coverage rather than identifying off the federal entitlement. Korea's universal childcare 2013 was followed by acute fertility-rate collapse and labour-market non-response that suggest the maternal-LFP elasticity claim does not hold in Korean cultural-institutional context. A defensible reading: each jurisdiction has its own complications and the joint-pattern test obscures heterogeneity; the +4pp claim per-jurisdiction is at upper bound for Quebec, contested for Germany, and arguably refuted for Korea.
