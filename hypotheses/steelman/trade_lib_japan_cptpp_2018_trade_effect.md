# Steelman — Japan CPTPP/EPA 2019

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Structural low openness.** Japan has had structurally low
   trade openness throughout the post-war period, driven by its
   large domestic market and yen-as-funding-currency dynamics.
   FTAs do not reliably move the dial on this structural feature.

2. **Bilateral composition shift, not aggregate.** Japanese export
   composition has shifted toward CPTPP/EPA partners, but
   aggregate openness is dominated by US, Chinese, and
   inter-Asian flows that the CPTPP/EPA does not cover.

3. **COVID dominates.** 2020-2021 trade volatility swamps any
   small CPTPP/EPA signal.

## What the framework still captures

The point-estimate-sign threshold accommodates a small positive
effect honestly. The PARTIAL verdict path is the modal expected
outcome.
