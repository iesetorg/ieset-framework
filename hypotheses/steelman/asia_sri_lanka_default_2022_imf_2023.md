# Steelman — Sri Lanka 2022 default + IMF 2023

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Cyclical, not policy.** Inflation deceleration 2022→2023 in Sri Lanka tracked global commodity-price normalisation (oil, food). Crediting the IMF programme for the inflation drop overstates programme effectiveness; much of the disinflation was global.

2. **Reserves recovered via remittances + tourism.** Sri Lankan reserves rose with the 2023 tourism rebound (visa changes, security-improvement perception). The IMF programme was sufficient but not necessary.

3. **Real economy still in distress.** Inflation falling and reserves recovering are stabilisation indicators; real GDP per capita remained below pre-default level through 2023. Calling 2023 a "stabilisation success" understates the persistent welfare loss.

## What the framework still captures

The two-test structure separates contraction (2022) from stabilisation (2023). Neither test depends on attribution; both are pre-registered observable thresholds. If global cyclical factors drove the 2023 disinflation regardless of IMF, the test still passes — the framework records the stabilisation but does not assert IMF-programme causation. Causation is reserved for follow-on hypotheses comparing LKA to similar default-without-IMF cases.
