# Steelman — Bukele mass-incarceration homicide impact 2019-2024

**The strongest argument against the causal claim:**

The homicide-rate decline in El Salvador is one of the most dramatic in modern peacetime history — 103/100k in 2015 to 2.4/100k in 2023 — and the temptation to attribute it to the visible, dramatic mass-incarceration regime is strong. But a disciplined causal analysis must engage seriously with the alternative that the decline is materially not attributable to the régimen de excepción, CECOT, or Plan Control Territorial, but to (a) a pre-existing downward trend that began before Bukele took office, (b) an undisclosed government-gang negotiation documented by El Faro and InSight Crime, and (c) a demographic factor as the large mara-recruitment cohort of 1990s-born males aged out of gang-membership years.

Specific objections the hypothesis must engage:

1. **The decline predates the régimen.** Homicide rate was already falling from 103/100k (2015) under FMLN Sánchez Cerén to 51/100k (2018) and 38/100k (2019, Bukele's first half-year) to 19.7/100k (2020) and 17.6/100k (2021) — all before the Mar 27 2022 régimen de excepción declaration. More than 80% of the total 2015-2023 decline had already occurred by 2021. The placebo-in-time test at 2019-06-01 is the critical diagnostic: if the synthetic-control method assigns a similarly large causal effect to a hypothetical "Bukele inauguration" treatment date three years before the régimen, the régimen-specific causal claim collapses.

2. **El Faro's documentation of an undisclosed truce.** El Faro published in Sep 2020 detailed prison-visitor logs, leaked internal messages, and sources inside the Ministerio de Justicia documenting direct negotiations between Bukele-administration officials (Osiris Luna, Carlos Marroquín) and MS-13 and Barrio 18 leadership inside Izalco and Zacatecoluca prisons. The alleged terms: reduced homicides in exchange for improved prison conditions, release of specific members, and electoral support in the Feb 2021 mid-terms. If this negotiation was the operative mechanism from 2019 through early 2022, the post-Mar 2022 régimen was a coercive regime change applied to a homicide-rate that was already suppressed by negotiation, and the causal attribution to mass incarceration is substantially smaller than the raw pre-post comparison suggests.

3. **Demographic ageing of the mara cohort.** Latin American gang membership is concentrated in the 14-25 male age window. El Salvador's 1990s-born cohort (the core MS-13 and Barrio 18 recruitment pool) aged out of the core 14-25 bracket during 2015-2025. UN DESA WPP data show the male-15-29 population share declining ~2 percentage points in SLV over the decade. Some portion of the homicide decline is mechanically a demographic transition that would have occurred under any government.

4. **Data-reporting incentives under an authoritarian regime.** Under régimen de excepción, independent investigative press has been intimidated (El Faro relocated staff to Costa Rica 2023), the Instituto de Medicina Legal reports to the executive, and there are documented cases of homicide classification being moved to "death in confrontation with authorities" (which is reported separately). Cristosal and Human Rights Watch document in-custody deaths that are not captured in the homicide series. The raw homicide rate is therefore somewhat overstated as a crime-decline metric; the extortion-complaints secondary outcome is harder to manipulate.

5. **The synthetic-control donor pool may not match Salvadoran demography and gang-ecology.** Honduras, Guatemala, and the rest of the Central American peer group did not have the MS-13/B18 territorial-control structure at Salvadoran intensity. The peer-pool comparison may systematically overstate the treatment effect because the donor countries have structurally different crime dynamics.

**How this steelman should shape the result card:**

The v1 result card must:

(a) Report the placebo-in-time test (treatment at 2019-06-01) as a headline diagnostic, not a robustness footnote. If the 2019 placebo is more than 50% of the magnitude of the 2022 actual, the causal attribution to the régimen is qualified.

(b) Report the extortion-complaints secondary outcome with equal prominence to homicides. A homicide decline without an extortion decline indicates the decline is a body-disposal or reporting-manipulation artefact rather than a substantive crime reduction.

(c) Explicitly model the male-15-29 demographic share as a channel and report the residual homicide gap after demographic adjustment.

(d) Present the El Faro truce-evidence as a named alternative hypothesis and report a structural-break test around the Mar 26-30 2022 massacre — if the pre-massacre trajectory was already converging to zero, the régimen's causal share is smaller than the raw comparison implies.

(e) Accept that the result is likely to be "some causal effect of mass incarceration, with substantial uncertainty on the fraction" rather than a clean attribution of the full 98% decline to the régimen. The policy takeaway is conditional on the fraction, which bears directly on the rule-of-law tradeoff analysis.
