# Steelman: JOLTS Openings and Wage Pressure

The strongest version of the claim is not that job openings mechanically cause wages, but that unusually fast openings growth captures employers' unmet labour demand before wage contracts and posted pay fully adjust. If the labour market is genuinely tight, openings growth should precede broad average hourly earnings growth over a short horizon.

The strongest counterargument is compositional. Aggregate job openings may rise in low-wage sectors or because matching efficiency deteriorates, while average hourly earnings can move because of industry mix, hours, or inflation catch-up. Pandemic reopening is especially likely to contaminate the signal, so the primary test excludes the acute reopening window.
