# Steelman

Generous transfers can increase labour-force participation when they remove barriers to work. Health coverage, child allowances, housing support, and unemployment insurance may let workers search longer, avoid bad matches, and accept entrepreneurial risk. A welfare state can also raise female labour supply if it funds child care and parental support.

The strongest counterprediction is that transfer-heavy systems with high service quality do not reduce participation, and that work-incentive proxies add little explanatory power. If participation rises with social spending, the dependency mechanism is too simple.
