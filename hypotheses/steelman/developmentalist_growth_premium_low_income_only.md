This hypothesis gives developmentalism its strongest ground: early catch-up. State coordination may be highly effective when the economy lacks roads, electricity, basic education, and export capacity. The market-liberal challenge is that these gains may not generalize to complex middle-income and frontier contexts, where continued state direction can become rent allocation.

The strongest developmentalist reply is that capable states can evolve from infrastructure building to technology upgrading. The test should therefore look for whether market competition becomes more important with income stage, not assume it from the start.
