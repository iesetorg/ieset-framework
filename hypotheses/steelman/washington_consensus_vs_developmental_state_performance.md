# Steelman — Washington Consensus vs Developmental State performance (ARG 1991-2001 vs KOR matched decade)

**The strongest arguments against the developmentalist framing of this hypothesis:**

1. **Argentina 2001 was a fixed-peg crisis, not a privatisation crisis.** The Convertibility Plan's hard peg of the peso to the US dollar was a *deviation* from textbook Washington Consensus — orthodox liberal advice was a flexible exchange rate. When the real terms-of-trade shock arrived (Brazilian devaluation 1999, US dollar appreciation, soybean prices) the peg made internal devaluation impossible and forced the collapse. Attributing the 2001 bust to the privatisation-and-opening half of the Menem programme conflates two genuinely distinct policies. The 1991-1998 sub-period — pure privatisation/opening, before the peg cracked — actually saw respectable productivity growth and capital deepening.

2. **The level-matched KOR decade (1989-1999) is late-stage developmental state PLUS the IMF reform period — not the Park Chung-hee era the developmentalist school usually points at.** By 1989 KOR had already accumulated three decades of human-capital build-up, industrial-policy chaebol scale, and export-discipline experience. Attributing 1989-1999 KOR growth to "developmental state vs Washington Consensus" treats stock and flow asymmetrically: the comparator inherits the entire stock of past developmental-state investment but the policy label only references the contemporaneous flow. A like-for-like *flow* comparison would compare Menem-era ARG policy to e.g. KOR 1962-1972 Park-era policy — but those start at vastly different GDP-pc levels and cannot be matched.

3. **Bilateral matched-decade comparison is descriptive, not causal.** N=2, no controls, no identification strategy. Many other things differ between Korea and Argentina: geography, security environment (US security guarantee for KOR), demographics, education stock, regional growth spillovers (East Asian miracle), commodity dependence (ARG soy/beef vs KOR manufactured exports). A confident causal read of "developmental state beat Washington Consensus" cannot be sustained on this evidence alone — at most we can say the observed Argentine policy bundle 1991-2001 produced less cumulative real income growth than the observed Korean bundle over a comparable starting-level decade.

4. **The 2001 collapse was largely paid back.** ARG cumulative growth 1991-2001 looks bad because the window ends at the trough. Extending to 1991-2008 (full recovery) shows much smaller underperformance. The hypothesis intentionally picks an end-year that maximises the gap — the Menem decade ends at the bottom of the bust. Window-selection sensitivity is a real concern.

5. **The 1997 Asian crisis hit KOR hard inside the matched decade, and KOR still beat ARG.** This actually strengthens the developmentalist read — but it also means the comparison is between TWO crisis-decades, not a smooth-growth Korea vs a crisis-Argentina. The hypothesis would be cleaner with crisis-symmetric or crisis-excluded windows.
