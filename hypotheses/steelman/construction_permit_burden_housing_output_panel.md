The strongest counterargument is measurement. WDI construction value added
includes infrastructure and commercial building, not just housing, while Doing
Business permit days are stylized indicators for a representative project rather
than observed delays for actual homes.

Permitting may also improve safety, infrastructure coordination, and
environmental quality. A negative coefficient on housing output would not show
that all review is wasteful or that deregulation is always welfare-improving.

This candidate is a broad cross-country screen. It should be upgraded with
dwelling completions, local zoning or permit-processing reforms, and quality
outcomes before drawing strong policy conclusions.
