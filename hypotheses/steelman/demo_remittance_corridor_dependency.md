# Steelman: Migrant-corridor remittance dependency

## Strongest case for the claim

Remittances boost reservation wages and reduce labour-supply pressure in origin households.
Cross-country panel evidence (Acosta et al, Chami et al) finds negative labour-supply
effects for some corridors. The hypothesis tests whether the headline effect generalises
across the four canonical corridor countries.

## Strongest case against

Reverse causation is severe: economic stagnation drives migration, which produces
remittances, which correlate with low LFP without a causal labour-supply channel. Without
IV, the panel-FE estimate is biased.

## Key uncertainties

- Identification under reverse causation.

## What would change my mind

A panel finding with country fixed effects insufficient to isolate the channel; v2 needs
an instrument (oil-price for Gulf-corridors, US-economic-conditions for LATAM corridors).
