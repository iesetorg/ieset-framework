# Steelman

Gini compression may raise broad income growth by shifting purchasing power to households with higher marginal propensities to consume, increasing social trust, and reducing costly status competition. It may also reflect bargaining institutions that lift lower and middle wages directly.

The strongest counterprediction is that inequality compression predicts faster real household income growth even after GDP, employment, and productivity controls. If the coefficient is positive and large, redistribution is not merely changing shares; it is part of the growth mechanism.
