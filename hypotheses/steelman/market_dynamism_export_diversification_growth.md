# Steelman - market_dynamism_export_diversification_growth

Claim tested: Lower export-product concentration predicts faster subsequent real GDP per-capita growth and stronger high-technology export performance.

The strongest skeptical reading is that export diversification may be an outcome of state strategy, resource endowments, education, or multinational supply-chain placement rather than a product of market competition. Concentration can also be efficient for small countries with genuine comparative advantage. The hypothesis should be treated as a market-discovery screen only: if diversification fails to predict future growth after fixed effects and controls, it weakens the claim that competitive product discovery is a general long-run growth mechanism.
