# Steelman — EVFTA 2020

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **China-substitution trend dominates.** Vietnam's
   manufacturing-export gains in 2020-2024 are driven primarily
   by the broader China+1 supply-chain-diversification wave
   triggered by the 2018 US-China trade war and accelerated by
   COVID-19. Attributing those gains to the EVFTA confounds two
   distinct shocks.

2. **Donor pool members shared the same trend.** Indonesia,
   Thailand, Malaysia also benefited from supply-chain
   diversification, partly offsetting their use as a control;
   the SC gap may understate the EVFTA effect or pick up
   Vietnam's particular advantages (lower wages, geographic
   proximity to China supply chains) that are not EVFTA-related.

3. **Pre-existing CPTPP membership.** Vietnam ratified CPTPP in
   2019, six months before EVFTA. The post-2020 trajectory
   reflects both agreements; isolating the EVFTA bilateral effect
   from the CPTPP effect requires more careful identification
   than aggregate SC.

## What the framework still captures

The COVID-2020-2021-exclusion sensitivity is a clean test for the
supply-chain-reshuffling confound. The PARTIAL verdict path
(positive gap driven primarily by 2020-2021) operationalises the
heterodox reading. CPTPP-vs-EVFTA decomposition deserves a follow-on
spec.
