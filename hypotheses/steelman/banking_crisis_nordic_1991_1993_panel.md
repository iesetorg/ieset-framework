# Steelman — Nordic 1991-1993 banking crises

## The strongest opposing argument

The Nordic crises are sometimes treated as a clean case of "deregulation causes crisis" but the timing varies materially within the panel: Norway's crisis began 1988 (only 4 years after credit-market liberalisation in 1984-1985), Finland's was deepened by the simultaneous loss of Soviet trade in 1991 (an exogenous shock unrelated to deregulation), and Sweden's krona crisis followed an attempted ERM peg defence in November 1992. Treating these as a single "post-deregulation" panel risks attributing to deregulation the effects of Nordic-specific external shocks.

The eventual resolution model (Sweden's bank-by-bank workout via Securum / Retriva) is also held up as a model of effective crisis management — which complicates a "crisis severity is large" framing.

## What the framework still captures

The 4-of-5 multi-metric threshold leaves room for country-specific mechanism heterogeneity while testing the joint pattern. The pre-registration commits to specific thresholds (credit run-up >= 25 pp, GDP decline >= 5%) that are violated by no-crisis periods in the same countries.
