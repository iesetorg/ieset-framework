# Steelman — ChAFTA 2015

## The strongest opposing argument

1. **Commodity-price dynamics dominate.** Australian export
   variation is overwhelmingly driven by iron ore and coal price
   cycles. The 2014-2016 commodity collapse straddles the
   pre/post-treatment cutoff and contaminates identification.

2. **Pre-existing demand was the binding factor.** Chinese demand
   for Australian iron ore was at scale long before ChAFTA;
   tariff reductions on a few sectors were marginal to the
   bilateral relationship.

3. **Post-2020 reversal undermines the finding.** Even if a
   2016-2019 positive SC gap is detected, the 2020-2024 informal-
   sanctions episode reverses much of it. A clean trade-creation
   reading requires ignoring the policy-risk side that the FTA
   itself increased.

## What the framework still captures

The 2016-2019 window is the cleanest available pre-COVID, pre-
trade-tensions evaluation period. The synthetic-control gap is a
defensible first-order test. The PARTIAL/REFUTED paths cleanly
accommodate commodity-cycle and reversal critiques.
