# Steelman — energy_market_competition_renewable_capacity_panel

The strongest counterargument is that renewable deployment often depends on
state-created demand: auctions, feed-in tariffs, grid planning, tax credits, and
public transmission investment. Competition may work only inside a policy
architecture built by the state.

Fraser EFW and WGI proxies may not identify electricity-market competition
specifically. Countries can score well on general regulatory quality while
retaining public ownership, or score poorly while using competitive renewable
auctions effectively.

A supported result would indicate that contestability and renewable diffusion
co-move in panels. It would not by itself refute state-led clean-energy strategy
unless later designs isolate entry liberalization from subsidy and planning
instruments.
