# Steelman — BoE independence 1997 + macroprudential 2013

## The strongest opposing argument

Attributing post-1997 UK inflation stability to BoE independence over-claims: the UK joined the global "Great Moderation" inflation downshift driven by China's WTO accession (cheap-goods deflation), favourable demographics, and the global central-banking convergence on inflation targeting. The same metric improvement is observable in countries that did not change their central-bank governance arrangements (e.g. Australia, New Zealand had earlier reforms; Canada earlier too).

The post-2013 macroprudential remit is also too short and confounded with exceptional QE/QT cycles to support a clean pre-/post-2013 credit-cycle comparison.

## What the framework still captures

The 2-of-3 metric threshold (with 25% magnitude requirement and 10% significance) is sized to be cleared if the institutional-reform story is real and to fail if the apparent improvement is purely global-trend. The pre-registration to specific metrics and dates limits post-hoc cherry-picking.
