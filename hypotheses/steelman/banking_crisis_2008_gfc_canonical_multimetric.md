# Steelman — 2008 GFC canonical multi-metric

## The strongest opposing argument

A 7-metric checklist where the metrics are mutually correlated (output, unemployment, debt, house prices, credit) overstates independent confirmation. Knowing real GDP fell sharply already implies unemployment rose, debt rose, and credit contracted via well-known elasticities. The "5 of 7" threshold sounds disciplined but the metrics fail almost in lockstep, so passing the bar in 5 countries gives less independent information than the count suggests.

Single-cluster scope is also a concern: 9 advanced economies inside one synchronised event do not constitute independent observations. A meaningfully harder test would require the same multi-metric pattern in geographically and temporally separated crises (which is what other hypotheses in this series do).

## What the framework still captures

The GFC checklist is a calibration exercise. If a 7-metric, 9-country, pre-specified rule cannot decisively flag the most-studied banking crisis in modern macro, the framework is mis-tuned. Passing the GFC test is a necessary-but-not-sufficient condition for trusting the same multi-metric structure in less canonical cases (Iceland, Cyprus, Lebanon).
