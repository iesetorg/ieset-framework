# Steelman

The strongest market-liberal version of this claim is not that laissez-faire alone guarantees prosperity. It is that durable frontier status requires reliable private property, contract enforcement, open competition, and limits on discretionary allocation. Developmentalist tools can mobilize resources during catch-up, but once easy gains are exhausted, countries need decentralized information, firm entry, and capital discipline to keep reallocating resources toward higher productivity uses.

The strongest opposing view is that market-institution scores may simply proxy for prior wealth, colonial history, geography, or state capacity. Successful developmentalist states may also score well on some market dimensions after they become rich, so timing and baseline measurement are crucial.
