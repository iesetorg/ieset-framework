# Steelman — pcw100_us_mw_binding_premium_total_weekly_wage

## Strongest case for the claim

The strongest case is that a higher wage floor directly changes the price of low-wage labor, raising covered wages while potentially changing employment, entry, and poverty through income and substitution channels.

The registered treatment is **state minimum-wage premium above the federal floor** and the registered outcome is
**QCEW average weekly wage**. The top-versus-bottom contrast is reported even when the
regression is null or contrary.

## Strongest challenge

State and year effects absorb stable state differences and national shocks, but they do not absorb every state-specific trend or anticipation response. Policy measurement may also proxy for income, administrative capacity,
political coalitions, demographics, or prior growth. These are credible rival
explanations, not after-the-fact exceptions.

## Interpretation discipline

This is an associational screen. A supported result clears the preregistered
directional hurdle but does not establish a causal effect. A significant
opposite sign is refuting evidence for this registered broad claim. An
insignificant estimate is PARTIAL, not evidence of no effect. Missing coverage
or an inadequate policy contrast is INCONCLUSIVE and does not graduate.
