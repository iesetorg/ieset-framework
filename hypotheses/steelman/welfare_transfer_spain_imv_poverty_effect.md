# Steelman: Spain IMV Poverty Effect

The strongest counter is that low take-up reflects rapid programme launch under emergency conditions rather than fundamental design failure, and the take-up rate has improved materially as administration matured. By end-2023 take-up had risen toward 70% of estimated-eligible households as the Tarjeta Social Digital integration with regional autonomous-community programmes filled gaps in eligibility-data flow. AIReF's 2023 report acknowledged improved coverage and recipient-satisfaction. Severe material deprivation in Spain did fall from 8.3% in 2020 to 7.7% in 2022 (Eurostat ilc_mddd11), tracking better than the donor-pool average — suggesting some treatment effect even at lower take-up. The under-delivery framing also conflates an operational ramp-up problem (2020-2021) with structural design (means-test cliffs, regional-administration interaction) — the former is fixable, the latter requires deeper reform. A defensible reading: IMV under-delivered against unrealistic 2020-launch targets but is not structurally broken; the 24-month snapshot is too early to call the design failed.
