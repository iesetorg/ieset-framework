# Steelman: Brazil demographic transition × inequality

## Strongest case for the claim

Brazil's post-2000 inequality decline is the largest sustained decline observed in any
major emerging economy. Multiple channels contributed; demographic-supply tightening at
the bottom of the wage distribution is a plausible contributor in addition to Bolsa Familia
and minimum-wage policy.

## Strongest case against

The literature (Lustig, Cornia) attributes the decline overwhelmingly to social policy and
real-minimum-wage gains; demographic channel is rarely flagged as primary. Risk of
demographic share being statistical noise.

## Key uncertainties

- Identifying demographic-channel contribution separate from policy.

## What would change my mind

Demographic share <5% of Gini decline would confirm the consensus that policy did the work
and demographics were incidental.
