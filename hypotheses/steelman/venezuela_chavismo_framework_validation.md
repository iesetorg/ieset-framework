# Steelman — Venezuela Chavismo framework validation

**The strongest argument that the framework should NOT code the Venezuelan outcome as a clean institutional-policy failure:**

The Venezuelan collapse is overdetermined to the point that assigning causal weight to specific policy channels is methodologically unreasonable. The 2014-2016 oil price collapse was a ~60% decline in the price of Venezuela's dominant export; US financial sanctions from 2015 and secondary sanctions on PDVSA from 2019 restricted the economy's ability to repatriate oil revenues even to service essential imports; mass emigration produced a labour-force hollowing; and the 2017-2019 hyperinflation was a self-fulfilling monetary destruction once fiscal-monetary feedback entered the unstable regime. Picking "Chavismo institutional policy" as the causal explanation is a narrative choice the econometrics cannot validate.

Specific objections:

1. **The oil price crash is the biggest single proximate cause of the 2014-2019 collapse.** A Latin American donor pool controls partially for this but imperfectly — oil is a much larger share of Venezuelan GDP than of the donor-pool average. A clean counterfactual would require a cleaner oil-exposure match, ideally synthetic-control weights optimising on pre-crash oil share of GDP.

2. **US sanctions are a significant post-2015 treatment separate from Chavismo.** The framework's movement coding conflates pre-sanctions Chavismo (1999-2014) with sanctions-era Chavismo (2015-present). A clean analysis separates these two regimes.

3. **Hyperinflation is a proximate cause that could be coded as a separate movement.** The 2017-2019 hyperinflation episode has its own literature (Hanke, Krus, Reinhart-Rogoff) and specific policy drivers (fiscal-monetary fusion after the 2013 unification of exchange-rate regimes). Treating it as a consequence of 1999 Chavismo elides a specific proximate cause.

4. **The donor-pool's heterogeneity matters.** CHL, BRA, MEX, PER all had their own significant policy shifts during 1996-2023 (Lula-Dilma era Brazil, Peña Nieto Mexico, AMLO Mexico, left pink-tide events). The donor pool is not a stable counterfactual — it includes countries with their own policy swings. Panel FE absorbs time-average features but not heterogeneous trajectories.

5. **The distributional gains 2003-2012 are real and should appear in a complete analysis.** Poverty reduction, literacy, infant mortality improvements during the boom period are documented in UNDP HDI and WHO GHO data already in the repo. A validation analysis that reports only GDP per capita systematically under-represents what the movement actually did before the collapse.

**How this should shape the result card:**

Even in a framework-validation case where the expected direction is near-certain, the honest result card should:

- Report pre-2013 and post-2013 separately (Chavismo boom vs collapse).
- Acknowledge oil-price-crash decomposition in the 2014-2019 period.
- Note sanctions-era confound for post-2015 magnitude.
- Include HDI and health-outcome trajectories alongside GDP (distributional vs aggregate).
- Avoid crediting the framework's "institutional policy detection" for an outcome that was overdetermined by multiple simultaneous mechanisms.

The validation is strongest if the framework detects the aggregate effect while reporting honestly which components of it the spec can vs cannot separate.
