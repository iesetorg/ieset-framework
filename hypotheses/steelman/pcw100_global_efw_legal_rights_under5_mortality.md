# Steelman — pcw100_global_efw_legal_rights_under5_mortality

## Strongest case for the claim

The strongest case is that durable policy regimes affect contracting, price signals, investment horizons, market entry, and knowledge diffusion long enough to appear in the registered long-run outcome.

The registered treatment is **Fraser legal-system and property-rights score** and the registered outcome is
**under-five-mortality change**. The top-versus-bottom contrast is reported even when the
regression is null or contrary.

## Strongest challenge

Baseline controls and a long window reduce simple level confounding, but the cross-country comparison cannot isolate reform timing or eliminate reverse causality. Policy measurement may also proxy for income, administrative capacity,
political coalitions, demographics, or prior growth. These are credible rival
explanations, not after-the-fact exceptions.

## Interpretation discipline

This is an associational screen. A supported result clears the preregistered
directional hurdle but does not establish a causal effect. A significant
opposite sign is refuting evidence for this registered broad claim. An
insignificant estimate is PARTIAL, not evidence of no effect. Missing coverage
or an inadequate policy contrast is INCONCLUSIVE and does not graduate.
