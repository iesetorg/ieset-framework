# Steelman — Colombia 2002 Uribe labour reform

The strongest counter-reading: Colombia's 2002-2007 unemployment fall is a story of security improvement (FARC territorial retreat), oil-price boom, and the security-confidence-driven domestic-investment recovery — none caused by Law 789. Núñez 2005 evaluations of Law 789 and DANE follow-ups found the working-day extension's actual employment effect was minimal and concentrated on apprenticeship contracts (Contrato de Aprendizaje) which substituted for adult employment, partly explaining why the law was repealed in 2010 by the Constitutional Court. Gaviria 2004 argued the law's labour-cost reductions were small relative to the firm-level total cost of formalisation (taxes, parafiscales) which the law did not address. The informal-share-paired outcome is the discipline: if informality didn't fall significantly, the formalisation channel of the reform failed even if aggregate unemployment improved. A v2 should add an event-study around the 2010 partial repeal as a within-Colombia robustness window.
