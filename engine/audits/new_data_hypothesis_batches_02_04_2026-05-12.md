# New Data Hypothesis Batches 02-04 — 2026-05-12

These extend `engine/audits/new_data_hypothesis_unlock_plan_2026-05-12.md` with three more 30-item batches. The goal is not just volume: each item is anchored to newly landed official data and framed as a testable claim with a clear outcome, treatment, and likely estimator path.

## Batch 02 — OECD PDB, Eurostat Sectoral Accounts, Long-Horizon Productivity

Theme: long-run market execution, productivity, sectoral reallocation, and frontier convergence. Primary data: OECD `DSD_PDB`, Eurostat `nama_10_gdp`, Eurostat `nama_10_a10`, WDI controls.

1. `oecd_pdb_hours_worked_productivity_frontier_convergence_1950_2025` — countries with faster GDP per hour growth converge toward the frontier faster over 20-year windows.
2. `oecd_pdb_unit_labour_cost_export_share_drag_1970_2025` — rising unit labour costs predict weaker export performance after country and year effects.
3. `oecd_pdb_labour_productivity_wage_alignment_1970_2025` — real wage growth tracks labour productivity more tightly in open-market economies.
4. `oecd_pdb_capital_deepening_without_tfp_limit_1950_2025` — capital deepening alone predicts catch-up growth early, but not sustained frontier convergence.
5. `oecd_pdb_tfp_growth_frontier_income_persistence_1970_2025` — TFP growth, not investment share alone, predicts long-run frontier persistence.
6. `oecd_pdb_services_share_productivity_slowdown_1970_2025` — high services share predicts lower aggregate productivity growth unless services productivity also rises.
7. `oecd_pdb_manufacturing_productivity_spillover_panel_1970_2025` — manufacturing productivity growth predicts later economy-wide GDP/hour growth.
8. `oecd_pdb_public_sector_share_productivity_growth_panel_1970_2025` — larger public-sector employment shares predict slower market-sector productivity growth.
9. `oecd_pdb_gdp_hour_regulation_interaction_1970_2025` — productivity gains are weaker when product-market regulation is high.
10. `oecd_pdb_compensation_productivity_decoupling_oecd_1970_2025` — compensation/productivity decoupling is larger where labour-market adjustment is more constrained.
11. `eurostat_sectoral_va_reallocation_growth_eu_1995_2025` — economies shifting value added toward high-productivity sectors grow faster.
12. `eurostat_sectoral_employment_reallocation_unemployment_cost_1995_2025` — fast employment reallocation produces short-run unemployment costs before productivity gains.
13. `eurostat_manufacturing_va_energy_price_sensitivity_2007_2025` — manufacturing value-added share falls more where industrial electricity prices rise.
14. `eurostat_construction_share_growth_volatility_1995_2025` — construction booms predict later GDP-growth volatility and sectoral correction.
15. `eurostat_finance_share_productivity_tradeoff_1995_2025` — rising finance-sector value-added share does not reliably predict broad productivity gains.
16. `eurostat_public_admin_share_private_productivity_drag_1995_2025` — public-administration share growth predicts weaker private-sector productivity growth.
17. `eurostat_ict_sector_share_frontier_growth_1995_2025` — higher ICT-sector value-added share predicts faster GDP/hour growth.
18. `eurostat_tradeable_sector_share_resilience_1995_2025` — tradeable-sector-heavy economies recover faster after external shocks.
19. `oecd_pdb_investment_share_tfp_interaction_1970_2025` — investment predicts growth only when paired with positive TFP growth.
20. `oecd_pdb_output_gap_recovery_productivity_panel_1970_2025` — post-recession recoveries are stronger when productivity growth resumes quickly.
21. `oecd_pdb_labour_share_productivity_growth_panel_1970_2025` — labour-share declines do not automatically imply weak wage outcomes once productivity is included.
22. `oecd_pdb_small_open_economy_frontier_convergence_1970_2025` — small open economies converge faster than closed or large domestic-market economies.
23. `oecd_pdb_tax_wedge_productivity_growth_panel_1970_2025` — higher labour tax wedges predict weaker GDP/hour growth.
24. `oecd_pdb_hours_reduction_output_tradeoff_panel_1970_2025` — reductions in hours worked raise hourly productivity but may reduce output per capita.
25. `oecd_pdb_productivity_slowdown_pre_post_2005_panel` — productivity growth slowed structurally after 2005 across most OECD economies.
26. `oecd_pdb_post_2008_productivity_hysteresis_panel` — countries with deeper 2008 output losses experienced longer productivity scarring.
27. `oecd_pdb_export_competitiveness_unit_labour_cost_panel` — unit labour cost increases forecast weaker export competitiveness over five-year horizons.
28. `oecd_pdb_market_services_productivity_frontier_gap` — market-service productivity explains much of US-EU GDP/hour divergence.
29. `oecd_pdb_capital_stock_growth_misallocation_signal` — capital-stock growth without productivity growth predicts later investment correction.
30. `oecd_pdb_long_horizon_market_reform_productivity_compounder` — market reform episodes compound into larger productivity gains over 15-25 years than in five-year windows.

## Batch 03 — Welfare, Labour, Minimum Wage, Social Spending

Theme: welfare architecture, low-skill employment, labour-market rules, and whether intervention protects living standards or reduces work incentives. Primary data: OECD SOCX, OECD EPL, OECD low-education unemployment, ILOSTAT unemployment/LFP/wages, BLS QCEW.

31. `oecd_socx_unemployment_benefits_unemployment_duration_1980_2024` — higher unemployment-benefit spending predicts longer unemployment persistence after shocks.
32. `oecd_socx_family_benefits_fertility_panel_1980_2024` — family-benefit spending predicts higher fertility only when housing costs are controlled.
33. `oecd_socx_old_age_spending_growth_tradeoff_1980_2024` — higher old-age spending shares predict slower working-age income growth.
34. `oecd_socx_disability_spending_lfp_tradeoff_1980_2024` — disability-spending expansion predicts lower prime-age labour-force participation.
35. `oecd_socx_active_vs_passive_labour_policy_unemployment_1980_2024` — active labour spending predicts faster unemployment reduction than passive cash support.
36. `oecd_socx_social_spending_poverty_elasticity_1980_2024` — poverty reduction per point of social spending varies sharply by programme mix.
37. `oecd_socx_welfare_scale_tax_wedge_employment_panel` — large welfare states preserve employment only when tax wedges remain moderate.
38. `oecd_socx_automatic_stabiliser_recession_depth_panel` — social spending cushions recession depth but may slow post-shock employment recovery.
39. `oecd_epl_low_education_unemployment_1985_2019` — stricter employment protection predicts higher low-education unemployment.
40. `oecd_epl_youth_unemployment_1985_2019` — stricter employment protection predicts higher youth unemployment.
41. `oecd_epl_temporary_contract_dualism_unemployment_panel` — strict regular-contract protection predicts greater youth/low-skill labour-market dualism.
42. `oecd_low_education_unemployment_minimum_wage_bite_1981_2024` — high minimum-wage bite predicts higher low-education unemployment.
43. `oecd_low_education_unemployment_union_density_panel` — high union density predicts lower wage dispersion but higher low-education unemployment in rigid markets.
44. `oecd_low_education_unemployment_productivity_shield_panel` — productivity growth offsets low-skill unemployment risk from wage floors.
45. `ilostat_lfp_welfare_spending_prime_age_panel_1990_2027` — welfare spending predicts lower participation where activation policy is weak.
46. `ilostat_unemployment_trade_openness_adjustment_panel_1991_2027` — trade openness raises adjustment volatility but lowers long-run unemployment in flexible markets.
47. `ilostat_wage_growth_unemployment_tightness_panel_1990_2025` — wage growth accelerates when unemployment falls below country-specific thresholds.
48. `ilostat_wage_growth_productivity_interaction_panel_1990_2025` — wage gains are durable only when backed by productivity growth.
49. `ilostat_female_lfp_market_opportunity_panel_1990_2027` — female LFP rises faster in countries with services-sector growth and market openness.
50. `ilostat_youth_unemployment_epl_interaction_panel_1991_2027` — youth unemployment is highest where EPL is strict and growth is weak.
51. `bls_qcew_county_food_service_minimum_wage_growth_2014_2024` — counties facing higher minimum wages see slower food-service employment growth.
52. `bls_qcew_state_food_service_minimum_wage_growth_2014_2024` — state minimum-wage hikes reduce food-service employment growth relative to lower-bite states.
53. `bls_qcew_county_food_service_wage_floor_border_design` — minimum-wage effects are sharper in contiguous county-pair comparisons.
54. `bls_qcew_food_service_small_county_sensitivity` — low-population counties show larger employment responses to wage-floor changes.
55. `bls_qcew_food_service_recession_interaction` — minimum-wage employment effects are larger during weak demand years.
56. `bls_qcew_food_service_post_covid_recovery_wage_floor` — high minimum-wage states recovered food-service employment differently after 2020.
57. `oecd_socx_minimum_wage_low_skill_tradeoff_panel` — generous welfare plus high wage floors predicts worse low-skill employment than either alone.
58. `oecd_socx_open_economy_welfare_compatibility_panel` — high welfare spending is less employment-damaging in highly open economies.
59. `oecd_epl_productivity_growth_moderation_panel` — strict EPL is less costly where productivity growth is high.
60. `oecd_activation_spending_low_education_unemployment_panel` — activation-heavy welfare systems produce better low-education employment outcomes than transfer-heavy systems.

## Batch 04 — Energy Prices, Credit Cycles, Exchange Rates, Macro Fragility

Theme: price signals, nuclear/energy policy, macro-financial fragility, and whether distortions accumulate over long horizons. Primary data: Eurostat electricity prices, BIS credit/DSR/house prices/EER, WDI macro controls.

61. `eurostat_industrial_electricity_price_manufacturing_va_2007_2025` — higher industrial electricity prices predict lower manufacturing value-added share.
62. `eurostat_household_electricity_price_real_consumption_2007_2025` — higher household electricity prices predict weaker real consumption growth.
63. `eurostat_electricity_price_inflation_pass_through_2007_2025` — electricity-price rises pass through into inflation more strongly in high-energy-intensity economies.
64. `eurostat_nuclear_retention_industrial_price_panel_2007_2025` — nuclear-retaining countries have lower industrial electricity prices than phaseout peers.
65. `eurostat_renewable_share_electricity_price_transition_cost_panel` — rapid renewable buildout initially raises electricity prices unless balanced by dispatchable capacity.
66. `eurostat_energy_price_household_distribution_stress_panel` — household electricity prices predict worse distributional stress where incomes are low.
67. `eurostat_energy_price_export_competitiveness_panel` — industrial electricity-price increases predict weaker export competitiveness.
68. `eurostat_energy_price_services_vs_industry_reallocation` — high energy costs accelerate value-added shift away from manufacturing.
69. `eurostat_energy_price_unemployment_regional_panel` — energy-price shocks predict higher unemployment in energy-intensive economies.
70. `eurostat_electricity_price_volatility_industrial_exit_panel` — price volatility, not just price level, predicts industrial-share decline.
71. `bis_credit_gap_house_price_boom_bust_panel_1970_2025` — credit-gap expansions predict house-price booms followed by reversals.
72. `bis_credit_gap_dsr_joint_crisis_risk_panel_1999_2025` — credit gaps are more dangerous when DSR is also high.
73. `bis_household_dsr_consumption_slowdown_panel_1999_2025` — high household DSR predicts weaker consumption growth.
74. `bis_corporate_dsr_investment_slowdown_panel_1999_2025` — high corporate DSR predicts weaker investment growth.
75. `bis_credit_gap_unemployment_lag_panel_1970_2025` — high credit gaps predict unemployment increases after financial tightening.
76. `bis_credit_gap_policy_rate_interaction_crisis_panel` — credit gaps predict later stress more strongly when real rates were low.
77. `bis_credit_gap_current_account_interaction_panel` — credit booms with current-account deficits are more crisis-prone.
78. `bis_credit_gap_property_price_interaction_panel` — credit booms paired with house-price booms carry higher reversal risk.
79. `bis_dsr_income_growth_threshold_panel` — DSR becomes damaging once it exceeds country-specific income-growth thresholds.
80. `bis_dsr_credit_supply_contraction_panel` — high DSR predicts later private-credit contraction.
81. `bis_reer_appreciation_export_growth_panel_1964_2026` — REER appreciation predicts weaker export growth.
82. `bis_reer_appreciation_industrial_share_panel_1964_2026` — REER appreciation predicts manufacturing-share erosion.
83. `bis_reer_misalignment_current_account_repair_panel` — REER overvaluation predicts later current-account adjustment.
84. `bis_reer_devaluation_inflation_tradeoff_panel` — REER depreciation improves external balance but raises inflation risk.
85. `bis_reer_volatility_investment_drag_panel` — exchange-rate volatility predicts weaker private investment.

87. `bis_credit_gap_fx_appreciation_twin_boom_panel` — credit booms plus currency appreciation predict larger reversals.
88. `bis_house_price_reer_capital_inflow_cycle_panel` — house-price booms and REER appreciation jointly mark capital-inflow cycles.
89. `bis_financial_fragility_broad_scope_anomaly_reduction` — broad BIS financial fragility signals predict downturns more robustly than single-country narratives.
