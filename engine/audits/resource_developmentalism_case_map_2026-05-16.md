## Subtype Definitions

- `resource_statist_socialist`: resource rents or resource-sovereignty claims fused with nationalisation, public ownership, price/FX controls, one-party or revolutionary statism, or socialist planning.
- `resource_developmentalist`: resource rents used for state-led investment, industrial upgrading, downstreaming, sovereign-directed diversification, or public-investment drives without full socialist command-economy replacement.
- `market_open_resource_peer`: resource-rich cases using broadly open trade/investment regimes, macro stabilisation, private-sector development, or foreign-capital-led extraction as the main comparator category.
- `rule_bound_resource_manager`: resource-rich cases where fiscal rules, sovereign-wealth rules, independent monetary institutions, or strong legal constraints dominate discretionary industrial allocation.
- `resource_nationalisation_shock`: dated nationalisation, expropriation, resource-tax, or resource-sovereignty shock that should be modelled as an episode rather than a long regime scalar.
- `mixed`: cases combining resource-rent state direction with liberalisation, weak institutions, conflict, sanctions, post-conflict reconstruction, or other mechanisms that make a single scalar misleading.
- `uncoded`: resource-rich sample cases or obvious peers that are absent from the current positive treatment inventory or lack enough manual subtype coding to use as controls.

## Scalar Separation Rules

Cases that should remain eligible for the main positive treatment only after subtyping and phase timing:

- Core `resource_statist_socialist`: Algeria FLN, Angola MPLA, Iraq Ba'ath, Venezuela Chavismo, Ecuador Correa, selected Bolivia MAS years, selected Syria years.
- Core `resource_developmentalist`: Indonesia New Order oil-boom and post-oil deregulation phases, Malaysia NEP/Mahathir phases after resource-channel review, Kazakhstan Nazarbayev, selected Gulf diversification episodes such as Saudi Vision 2030 and UAE free-zone/sovereign-investment phases.

Cases that should be separated from the current scalar treatment:

- Rule-bound managers: Norway, Botswana, Chile copper fiscal-rule episodes.
- Market-open peers: Australia, Canada outside the NEP shock, Colombia market-continuity cases, Peru open mining episodes, Vietnam Doi Moi if no resource-funding channel is shown.
- Nationalisation/resource-sovereignty shocks: Venezuela Perez I, Bolivia 2006 hydrocarbons, Canada NEP, Mexico Lopez Portillo/Cantarell borrowing boom, Australia mining-tax episodes.
- WDI-threshold/nonresource developmentalists: China, Ethiopia, Rwanda, Vietnam, Cote d'Ivoire, Senegal, Kenya, Tunisia, Morocco, Tanzania, Myanmar unless a concrete resource-funding channel is manually established.
- Predation/conflict/sanction mixed cases: Nigeria, Zaire/DRC, Iran, Russia, PNG, Syria broad umbrella, Kuwait welfare-state umbrella.
- Uncoded obvious resource peers: Qatar, Oman, Bahrain, Libya, Trinidad and Tobago, Gabon, Cameroon, Zambia, Mongolia, Ghana resource episodes.

## Bottom Line

The current scalar should be treated as a screening proxy, not a treatment. The historical case map supports a subtype design with at least four explicit arms: statist/socialist resource regimes, developmentalist rent-funded industrialisers, market-open resource peers, and rule-bound resource managers. Resource-nationalisation shocks should be event-coded separately. The strongest immediate recoding priority is to remove rule-bound managers, WDI-threshold nonresource developmental states, and uncoded peers from the residual/control bucket before any hardened causal estimate is scored.
