# Heritage Market Methodology Reset - 2026-05-03

## Correction

- The Heritage controlled cross-sectional wave is a candidate-screening wave, not scoreboard evidence.
- No Heritage market-order result from this wave should be mapped directly into position `falsifiable_specific_claims`.
- No generated Heritage hypothesis should receive `covers_claims` from the post-estimation screen.
- The useful output is a pre-registration queue: write stronger hypothesis specs first, then run the registered designs, then evaluate scoreboard eligibility.

## Preserved Signal

- Baseline Heritage screen: 192 candidate runs, 138 supported, 35 partial, 19 refuted.
- Controlled income-and-region screen: 180 candidate runs, 75 supported, 89 partial, 16 refuted.
- Queue retained: top 50 controlled FDR-supported items plus 14 controlled FDR-refuted items.

## Required Promotion Path

1. Convert a queued item into a fresh, theory-specific hypothesis before estimation.
2. Use time-varying proxies where possible rather than a latest-year Heritage cross-section.
3. Prefer panel fixed effects, event-study, DiD, synthetic control, or local-projection designs over static cross-sectional gaps.
4. Include steelman objections and failure modes before running.
5. Run the registered design and only then consider scoreboard mapping if validation passes.

## Austrian And Free-Market Guardrail

- The claim must be theory-shaped, not merely "market score correlates with good outcome."
- Austrian-friendly hypotheses should focus on sound money, entrepreneurship, property rights, calculation, malinvestment, price controls, fiscal-monetary dominance, and intervention uncertainty.
- Ordoliberal-friendly hypotheses should focus on rule-of-law, competition order, monetary stability, fiscal rules, anti-corruption, predictable regulation, and market-compatible welfare institutions.
- Broad market-liberal hypotheses should distinguish free-market mechanisms from generic income, region, institutional-capacity, or short-run outcome correlations.

## Current Status

- Scoreboard mapping from the Heritage wave has been removed.
- The queue remains as a methodology-safe backlog for pre-registration and stronger tests.
