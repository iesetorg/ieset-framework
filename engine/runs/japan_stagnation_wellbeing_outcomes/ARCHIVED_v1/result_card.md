# Japan 1990-2020: stagnation vs wellbeing

**Verdict:** SUPPORTED — JPN 1990-2020 mean GDP/cap growth = 0.96% (<1.5%); 1 of 3 wellbeing indicators degraded vs OECD median (LE_rel=-0.16y; unemp_rel=+2.39pp; gini_delta=n/app).

## Method

Multi-metric checklist. JPN mean GDP/cap growth 1990-2020 (excl. 2020 COVID) vs the 1.5% stagnation threshold; LE/unemployment deltas relative to OECD median; Gini delta absolute. Primary supports the degrowth claim if stagnation holds AND <=1 of 3 wellbeing metrics degraded.

## Numbers

- JPN mean GDP/cap growth 1990-2020 (ex 2020): 0.96%
- JPN LE 1990→2020: 78.8→84.6y; OECD median: 76.1→81.9y; relative -0.16y
- JPN unemployment 1991→2020: 2.06→2.81pp; OECD median 7.29→5.64pp; relative +2.39pp
- JPN Gini early→late: None→32.9; delta n/a (sparse Gini series)
- Wellbeing indicators degraded: 1 of 3

## Caveats

- WDI panel does NOT include suicide rates, hikikomori, fertility rates, or hours-worked — the indicator basket is the favourable subset of "wellbeing".
- Life expectancy improved globally over the window; the OECD-relative test partially controls for the secular trend.
- Gini sparse series; endpoints have ±5y tolerance.
