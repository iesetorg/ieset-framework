# Policy-rate tightening predicts house-price cooling

**Verdict:** partial - one of the regression or raw-contrast gates clears, but not both.

## Predeclared Test

Policy tightening is a >= 2 pp rise in BIS central-bank policy rates over four quarters; outcome is 8-quarter forward real residential property-price log growth.

## Results

- Usable panel: **6,259 observations**, **37 countries**.
- Clustered FE coefficient: **-1.457** (SE 4.400, p=0.7405, 95% CI [-10.082, 7.167]).
- Raw high-minus-normal mean: **-6.248** (-5.078 vs 1.170); treated observations: **673**.

## Caveats

This is a compact local-data panel verdict. It is useful as a falsifiable first tranche, but it should not be read as a structural causal design without stronger identification.
