# Austrian & Ordoliberal 500-Hypothesis Backlog: Free Market vs Intervention

This backlog tests Austrian-school and Ordoliberal claims against interventionist alternatives using long-horizon panels, natural experiments, and cross-country comparisons. Every hypothesis references measurable institutional or policy variables, not ideology labels.

---

## Track A: Austrian Business Cycle — Credit Expansion & Busts (1–20)

1. `abct_credit_expansion_predicts_capital_goods_overinvestment`: Rapid credit growth relative to GDP predicts disproportionate investment-share growth in capital-goods sectors versus consumer-goods sectors over 3–5 year windows.
2. `abct_low_real_rate_capital_misallocation_tfp`: Sustained negative real policy rates predict subsequent total-factor-productivity slowdown in capital-intensive industries after controlling for cycle position.
3. `abct_housing_credit_boom_construction_share_reversal`: Countries with rapid mortgage-credit expansion show construction-sector output share rising then sharply reversing after credit contraction, exceeding the amplitude of non-housing sectors.
4. `abct_yield_curve_inversion_recession_prediction`: Yield-curve inversion (10Y minus 3M) predicts recession within 18 months more reliably in countries with active credit intermediation than in financially repressed economies.
5. `abct_m2_growth_asset_inflation_before_cpi`: Broad-money growth predicts asset-price inflation with a shorter lag than consumer-price inflation, and the asset-price gap closes via bust rather than smooth convergence.
6. `abct_central_bank_balance_sheet_expansion_zombification`: Large central-bank balance-sheet expansion predicts higher zombie-firm persistence and lower post-crisis productivity recovery than smaller interventions.
7. `abct_fed_funds_below_taylor_rule_malinvestment_2002_2007`: US federal funds rate below Taylor-rule prescriptions predicts disproportionate growth in durable-goods orders and construction employment relative to trend.
8. `abct_china_credit_growth_empty_infrastructure_tfp`: China's state-directed credit booms predict lower marginal TFP in infrastructure and real estate than in credit-constrained private manufacturing.
9. `abct_eurozone_periphery_credit_boom_bust_amplitude`: Eurozone periphery countries with largest pre-2008 private-credit expansions show deepest post-2008 output collapses and slowest recoveries.
10. `abct_quantitative_easing_equity_wealth_effect_asymmetry`: Quantitative-easing episodes predict larger wealth gains for top wealth deciles than bottom half, without proportional consumption or wage spillovers.
11. `abct_commodity_supercycle_credit_fueled_duration`: Credit-fueled commodity booms show sharper price reversals and longer investment-hangover periods than demand-driven booms.
12. `abct_shadow_banking_growth_regulatory_arbitrage`: Rapid shadow-banking growth predicts systemic risk accumulation and sharper subsequent deleveraging than bank-credit growth under similar macro conditions.
13. `abct_corporate_debt_boom_share_buyback_vs_capital`: Credit-boom periods show rising share of corporate borrowing directed to buybacks and acquisitions rather than capital expenditure, especially when rates are held below natural rate.
14. `abct_bank_lending_standard_easing_predicts_default_wave`: Easing of bank lending standards predicts higher non-performing loan ratios 3–4 years later, with the lag shorter in less regulated banking systems.
15. `abct_interest_rate_floor_duration_capital_structure_distortion`: Prolonged near-zero policy rates predict flattening of yield curves and compression of credit spreads that distort maturity matching in financial intermediaries.
16. `abct_emerging_market_dollar_debt_boom_currency_crisis`: Rapid foreign-currency corporate debt accumulation predicts sharper currency crises and deeper output contractions than local-currency debt accumulation.
17. `abct_credit_gdp_gap_housing_affordability_wedge`: Countries with largest credit-to-GDP gaps above trend show largest divergences between house-price growth and wage growth during the boom phase.
18. `abct_monetary_expansion_entrepreneurial_error_clustering`: Startups founded during periods of rapid monetary expansion show higher subsequent failure rates and lower profitability than cohorts founded during tighter conditions, controlling for cycle.
19. `abct_austrian_recession_depth_credit_quality_link`: Recessions preceded by larger credit-quality deterioration show deeper output drops and slower employment recovery than inventory-cycle recessions.
20. `abct_soviet_style_planned_investment_cycle_amplitude`: State-planned economies show larger investment-cycle amplitude and more frequent shortage-glut alternation than market economies at similar development levels.

## Track B: Austrian Capital Theory & Interest Rates (21–40)

21. `austrian_roundaboutness_rising_wage_productivity`: Countries with higher real wage growth show lengthening of production processes (higher capital-to-labour ratios, longer gestation periods) and rising labour productivity over 20-year windows.
22. `austrian_time_preference_savings_investment_link`: Lower time preference (higher savings rates) predicts higher quality of investment (less speculative, more capital-deepening) and stronger long-run TFP growth.
23. `austrian_artificially_low_rate_short_termism`: Prolonged periods of negative real rates predict shorter CEO tenure, higher quarterly-earnings guidance frequency, and lower R&D intensity relative to buybacks.
24. `austrian_intertemporal_discipline_sovereign_debt`: Countries with harder budget constraints and market-priced sovereign debt show more sustainable capital structures and fewer crisis-driven investment halts than countries with perpetual monetisation.
25. `austrian_hayek_triangle_construction_durables_priority`: During credit expansions, orders for long-gestation goods (construction, heavy machinery, durables) rise faster than short-gestation goods (nondurables, services), then fall faster in contraction.
26. `austrian_natural_rate_estimation_policy_rate_gap`: Estimated natural-rate gaps (using Laubach-Williams or market-implied methods) above zero predict stronger subsequent investment quality than large negative gaps.
27. `austrian_capital_consumption_infrastructure_maintenance`: Countries with high public-investment surges but weak maintenance spending show net capital consumption (deteriorating infrastructure quality) over 15-year panels.
28. `austrian_savings_glut_quality_capital_flows`: Current-account-surplus countries with high domestic savings show more stable cross-border investment flows and fewer sudden stops than deficit countries relying on foreign borrowing.
29. `austrian_hurdle_rate_compression_risk_premium`: Sustained low policy rates compress hurdle rates in corporate capital budgeting, predicting subsequent waves of low-return project cancellations and write-downs.
30. `austrian_structure_of_production_services_vs_goods`: As economies develop with market price signals, the ratio of employment in higher-order goods (capital equipment, business services) to lower-order goods rises; state-directed industrial policy distorts this ratio.
31. `austrian_real_wage_capital_complementarity`: Rising real wages predict capital-labour substitution and labour-productivity growth in market economies; wage-floor regimes predict capital-labour substitution without employment growth.
32. `austrian_entrepreneurial_error_clustering_boom_sectors`: Sectors with largest employment and investment gains during credit booms show disproportionate bankruptcies and capacity utilisation collapses during busts.
33. `austrian_golden_age_capital_structure_1950_1973`: Post-war economies with less financial repression and freer capital markets show more sustainable capital deepening than those with credit rationing and directed allocation.
34. `austrian_interest_rate_normalisation_valuation_correction`: Periods of rapid interest-rate normalisation predict larger corrections in long-duration assets (tech equities, real estate, venture capital) than in short-duration assets.
35. `austrian_capital_theory_energy_transition_duration`: Market-priced energy transitions show more efficient capital reallocation and less stranded-asset accumulation than subsidy-driven transitions with administratively set deadlines.
36. `austrian_time_structure_public_pension_systems`: Pay-as-you-go public pension systems reduce visible private savings but do not substitute for capital formation, predicting lower capital-per-worker than funded systems.
37. `austrian_roundaboutness_agricultural_intensification`: Agricultural systems with secure property rights and market interest rates show progressive mechanisation and chemical-input deepening that outpaces collectivised systems.
38. `austrian_malinvestment_knowledge_intensity`: Credit booms in knowledge-intensive sectors produce more severe post-bust talent misallocation (skilled workers in defunct firms) than in traditional manufacturing.
39. `austrian_capital_maintenance_vs_gross_investment`: Countries that report high gross investment but low net investment (high depreciation rates) show weaker long-run productivity growth than countries with lower gross but higher net investment.
40. `austrian_hayekian_knowledge_problem_infrastructure`: Infrastructure projects chosen by central planning show larger cost overruns and lower usage rates than projects selected by user-fee revenue signals.

## Track C: Austrian Entrepreneurship & Market Process (41–60)

41. `austrian_kirzner_entrepreneurship_alertness_profit`: Higher profit-rate dispersion across firms predicts stronger subsequent entry and faster convergence to equilibrium in industries with low regulatory barriers.
42. `austrian_discovery_process_regulatory_stagnation`: Industries with heavy pre-market approval (pharmaceuticals, energy, transport) show slower product-cycle turnover and fewer disruptive innovations than lightly regulated sectors.
43. `austrian_competition_as_discovery_procedure_tfp`: Industries with higher firm churn (entry plus exit rates) show faster quality-adjusted price declines and higher TFP growth than industries with high incumbent protection.
44. `austrian_schumpeterian_creative_destruction_employment`: Higher rates of job destruction from firm exit are associated with faster subsequent job creation and lower long-run unemployment in flexible labour markets.
45. `austrian_entrepreneurial_error_learning_speed`: Countries with faster bankruptcy resolution and lower stigma show faster recycling of entrepreneurial talent into new ventures after failure.
46. `austrian_market_process_price_dispersion_convergence`: Retail price dispersion for identical goods declines faster in countries with transparent pricing, low transport barriers, and unregulated entry than in fragmented protected markets.
47. `austrian_knowledge_problem_soviet_queue_rationing`: Soviet-type economies show persistent queueing and shadow pricing that indicates larger knowledge-problem severity than measured by official shortage indices alone.
48. `austrian_trial_and_error_tech_diffusion`: Technology adoption curves are steeper in countries with venture-capital markets and permissive bankruptcy than in countries with state-picked national champions.
49. `austrian_entrepreneurial_wage_premium_risk_taking`: Economies with higher returns to successful entrepreneurship (lower capital-gains tax, stronger property rights) show more high-growth firm formation.
50. `austrian_local_knowledge_decentralised_planning`: Cities with more devolved zoning and permitting authority show faster housing-supply responses to demand shocks than cities with centralised state planning.
51. `austrian_market_process_food_shortage_prevention`: Agricultural economies with private grain-trading networks show smaller famine mortality during weather shocks than economies with state grain monopsonies.
52. `austrian_discovery_tourism_niche_specialisation`: Tourism destinations that allowed market-determined specialisation (ecotourism, medical, cultural) show faster visitor-revenue growth than state-planned mass-tourism developments.
53. `austrian_error_correction_market_vs_planned`: Market economies correct inventory and capacity imbalances faster (shorter duration of excess supply) than planned economies with soft budget constraints.
54. `austrian_entrepreneurship_immigrant_founding_rate`: Countries with lower regulatory barriers to firm entry show higher immigrant entrepreneurship rates and faster immigrant income convergence.
55. `austrian_market_process_university_spinoff_rate`: Universities in market-oriented innovation ecosystems produce more faculty and student spinoffs per research dollar than universities in state-directed research systems.
56. `austrian_knowledge_problem_healthcare_waiting_lists`: Centralised healthcare planning produces longer waiting lists for elective procedures than decentralised systems with price signals and competitive referral.
57. `austrian_discovery_retail_format_evolution`: Countries with unrestricted retail-format competition (hypermarkets, e-commerce, discounters) show faster consumer-price declines and wider product variety than restricted-format countries.
58. `austrian_alertness_arbitrage_wage_gap_closure`: Wage gaps between regions or demographic groups close faster in economies with high labour-market turnover and low mobility barriers than in rigid systems.
59. `austrian_market_process_environmental_quality`: Environmental quality indicators improve faster in countries with pollution-trading markets and clear liability rules than in countries with command-and-control permitting.
60. `austrian_entrepreneurship_rate_tax_progressivity`: Progressivity of personal income taxation predicts lower self-employment and firm-entry rates, especially at the top marginal rate where upside returns are compressed.

## Track D: Austrian Monetary Theory — Cantillon Effects & Distribution (61–80)

61. `austrian_cantillon_effect_asset_holder_gain_first`: Monetary expansion episodes predict rising wealth shares for asset holders before wage earners, with the lag proportional to financial-market depth.
62. `austrian_inflation_tax_poor_savings_erosion`: Higher inflation volatility predicts larger declines in real bank-deposit balances for low-wealth households relative to high-wealth households with diversified assets.
63. `austrian_monetary_expansion_housing_inequality`: Rapid monetary expansion predicts larger increases in house-price-to-income ratios and rent burdens for young cohorts than for incumbent homeowners.
64. `austrian_sound_money_gini_stability`: Countries with lower and more stable inflation over 30-year windows show smaller increases in disposable-income Gini than countries with high and volatile inflation.
65. `austrian_cantillon_sovereign_debt_holders_bailout`: Large-scale central-bank sovereign-debt purchases predict negative real returns for new savers while protecting incumbent bondholders and leveraged asset owners.
66. `austrian_inflation_regressive_consumption_basket`: Higher inflation predicts larger welfare losses for households with consumption baskets skewed toward food, transport, and housing (non-durables) than for asset-heavy households.
67. `austrian_money_creation_bank_credit_asset_bias`: Money creation dominated by bank credit flows predicts asset-price inflation; money creation dominated by fiscal transfer flows predicts goods inflation.
68. `austrian_currency_devaluation_imported_inequality`: Sharp currency devaluations raise imported-goods prices for consumers before raising export-sector wages, widening the consumption-wage gap temporarily.
69. `austrian_zlb_duration_pension_fund_squeeze`: Prolonged zero-lower-bound policy predicts rising pension-fund underfunding and higher contribution demands on younger workers relative to retired beneficiaries.
70. `austrian_gold_standard_price_stability_distribution`: Countries on classical gold-standard or currency-board arrangements before 1914 or 1990s show lower inflation variance and smaller wealth-share volatility than fiat-standard peers.
71. `austrian_crypto_adoption_inflation_hedge_demand`: Countries with higher realised inflation and stricter capital controls show higher cryptocurrency adoption as a share of remittances and savings.
72. `austrian_monetary_neutrality_long_run_non_neutral`: Money is long-run neutral in price level but not in capital structure; countries with stop-go monetary policy show permanently lower capital-per-worker than stable-money peers.
73. `austrian_seigniorage_development_state_crowding`: High seigniorage financing of government spending predicts lower private financial-deepening ratios and weaker long-run investment.
74. `austrian_cantillon_urban_rural_gap`: Monetary expansion transmitted through financial centres predicts widening urban-rural income gaps before wage catch-up in peripheral regions.
75. `austrian_dollarisation_inflation_stability`: Dollarised or currency-board economies show lower inflation persistence and smaller post-shock inflation variance than independent-float peers with weak central-bank credibility.
76. `austrian_helicopter_money_consumption_burst_not_investment`: Direct-to-household monetary transfers predict consumption surges but not sustained investment or productivity improvements.
77. `austrian_inflation_expectations_de_anchor_cost`: Once inflation expectations de-anchor, the output cost of re-anchoring exceeds the cost of maintaining a credible low-inflation regime from the start.
78. `austrian_monetary_regime_credibility_duration`: Countries with longer uninterrupted low-inflation regimes show lower inflation-risk premia in long bond yields and lower nominal interest rates at given real rates.
79. `austrian_fiat_vs_commodity_money_crisis_amplitude`: Post-1971 fiat-standard recessions show larger amplitude and more frequent financial crises than Bretton Woods or pre-1914 recessions, controlling for structural change.
80. `austrian_central_bank_independence_inflation_bias`: Countries with less independent central banks show higher average inflation and larger post-crisis inflation overshoots than independent-central-bank peers.

## Track E: Ordoliberal Competition Order (81–100)

81. `ordoliberal_pmr_competition_tfp_link`: Lower product-market regulation scores predict higher total factor productivity growth across OECD panels after controlling for education and capital deepening.
82. `ordoliberal_cartel_prohibition_market_entry`: Countries with stronger cartel prohibition and leniency programmes show higher firm-entry rates and lower incumbent profit persistence.
83. `ordoliberal_state_aid_control_efficiency`: EU member states with stricter state-aid discipline show higher aggregate TFP and lower zombie-firm shares than member states with extensive national subsidies.
84. `ordoliberal_competition_agency_independence_enforcement`: Competition-agency independence from political interference predicts stronger antitrust enforcement and lower market-concentration trends.
85. `ordoliberal_network_industry_unbundling`: Vertical unbundling of network industries (telecom, energy, rail) predicts lower consumer prices and higher service quality than state-integrated monopolies.
86. `ordoliberal_competition_policy_patent_quality`: Stronger competition enforcement predicts higher patent quality (citations per patent) and fewer defensive patent thickets.
87. `ordoliberal_market_power_labor_share_decline`: Rising industry concentration predicts declining labour-share of value added where competition-policy enforcement is weak, but not where it is strong.
88. `ordoliberal_public_procurement_competition_savings`: Open competitive tendering in public procurement predicts lower cost per unit of infrastructure and fewer cost overruns than negotiated or sole-source contracting.
89. `ordoliberal_sme_access_finance_competition`: Countries with stronger competition in banking (lower concentration, fewer entry barriers) show lower SME lending spreads and faster credit growth.
90. `ordolibliberal_digital_platform_gatekeeper_rules`: Platform markets with ex-ante interoperability and data-portability requirements show lower switching costs and more challenger entry than markets with ex-post antitrust only.
91. `ordoliberal_local_monopoly_utility_regulation`: Municipal utilities with regulated third-party access and benchmarking show lower costs than municipally protected monopolies without competitive yardsticks.
92. `ordoliberal_pharmaceutical_parallel_import_balance`: Countries that balance patent protection with parallel-import competition show lower drug prices without detectable reduction in new-drug launches.
93. `ordolibler_retail_price_maintenance_prohibition`: Prohibition of resale price maintenance predicts lower consumer prices and faster online-retail penetration than permissive RPM regimes.
94. `ordoliberal_merger_control_type_II_error`: Lax merger control in concentrated industries predicts subsequent price increases and quality degradation, validating stricter Ordoliberal blocking thresholds.
95. `ordolibler_professional_services_deregulation`: Deregulation of professional-services entry (legal, accounting, pharmacy) predicts lower fees and higher employment without quality degradation.
96. `ordoliberal_competition_culture_cross_country`: Countries with longer competition-policy traditions show lower markups and higher pass-through of productivity gains to real wages.
97. `ordoliberal_state_monopoly_replacement_private_entry`: Replacement of state monopolies with regulated private entry predicts lower prices and higher investment than continued state operation or fragmented municipal provision.
98. `ordoliberal_cross_border_service_liberalisation`: EU Services Directive implementation predicts faster productivity growth in previously protected service sectors (retail, construction, business services).
99. `ordoliberal_innovation_market_structure_inverted_u`: The relationship between competition and innovation follows an inverted-U with the peak at moderately competitive markets; overly lax or overly strict intervention both reduce R&D.
100. `ordoliberal_competition_order_constitutional_embeddedness`: Constitutional or legal entrenchment of competition principles predicts more durable competition policy across political cycles.

## Track F: Ordoliberal Monetary & Fiscal Rules (101–120)

101. `ordoliberal_independent_central_bank_inflation_variance`: Central-bank independence indices predict lower inflation variance and lower average inflation after controlling for initial income and exchange-rate regime.
102. `ordolibler_price_stability_mandate_lower_uncertainty`: Countries with price-stability-only or low-inflation mandates show lower macroeconomic uncertainty indices and higher long-run investment than dual-mandate peers.
103. `ordoliberal_debt_brake_fiscal_sustainability`: Constitutional debt-brake rules predict lower debt-to-GDP drift and fewer fiscal crises than discretionary balanced-budget targets.
104. `ordoliberal_fiscal_rules_countercyclical_quality`: Fiscal rules with cyclical-adjustment clauses predict better automatic-stabiliser performance than strict nominal targets that force pro-cyclical austerity.
105. `ordoliberal_monetary_policy_rule_predictability`: Central banks that follow explicit monetary-policy rules (Taylor-type or inflation-targeting) show lower bond-yield volatility and higher investment predictability.
106. `ordoliberal_sound_money_preference_population`: Countries with historical hyperinflation experience show stronger legal protections for central-bank independence and lower average inflation decades later.
107. `ordoliberal_federal_fiscal_transfers_disincentive`: Large unconditional fiscal transfers from central to subnational governments predict weaker subnational fiscal effort and higher deficits than conditional or matching grants.
108. `ordolibler_tax_base_broad_rate_low_growth`: Tax systems with broad bases and low rates predict higher long-run investment and less tax-avoidance industry than systems with narrow bases and high rates.
109. `ordoliberal_golden_rule_public_investment`: Golden-rule constraints (borrow only for investment) predict higher public-investment quality and lower white-elephant incidence than unconstrained deficit rules.
110. `ordoliberal_off_budget_vehicle_transparency`: Off-budget infrastructure vehicles and public-private partnerships with opaque accounting predict higher final costs and more renegotiation than on-budget competitive tendering.
111. `ordoliberal_subnational_bankruptcy_discipline`: US states with municipal-bankruptcy provisions show lower long-run subnational debt accumulation and faster fiscal adjustment than states without.
112. `ordoliberal_inflation_targeting_credible_anchor`: Inflation-targeting adoption predicts lower inflation persistence and faster convergence to target than money-growth or exchange-rate targeting in emerging markets.
113. `ordoliberal_fiscal_council_independence`: Independent fiscal councils predict more accurate macro forecasts, lower deficit bias, and smaller forecast errors than councils under executive control.
114. `ordoliberal_tax_automatic_indexation_bracket_creep`: Countries without automatic tax-bracket indexation show higher real tax burdens and lower labour-supply growth than indexed countries during inflationary periods.
115. `ordolibler_pension_fund_privatisation_savings`: Pension privatisation (notional or funded) predicts higher domestic savings and deeper capital markets than pay-as-you-go systems, but only with credible regulatory frameworks.
116. `ordoliberal_state_owned_bank_lending_quality`: State-owned banks show lower loan quality, higher directed-lending ratios, and weaker post-crisis recovery than private banks after controlling for macro conditions.
117. `ordoliberal_central_bank_transparency_forecast_accuracy`: Higher central-bank transparency indices predict lower private-sector forecast dispersion and smaller monetary-policy surprises.
118. `ordolibler_currency_board_crisis_resilience`: Currency-board arrangements predict lower currency-crisis frequency and faster post-crisis recovery than intermediate exchange-rate regimes with weak credibility.
119. `ordoliberal_fiscal_consolidation_expenditure_vs_revenue`: Expenditure-based fiscal consolidations predict more durable debt reduction and smaller output costs than revenue-based consolidations.
120. `ordoliberal_monetary_union_fiscal_discipline_requirement`: Monetary-union membership without fiscal discipline mechanisms predicts larger sovereign-debt crises than membership with enforcement.

## Track G: Ordoliberal Social Market Economy (121–140)

121. `ordoliberal_social_market_not_dirigiste_growth`: Post-war West Germany's social-market model produced faster TFP and consumption growth than France's dirigiste planning during 1950–1980, after controlling for reconstruction catch-up.
122. `ordoliberal_welfare_state_market_flexibility_complement`: Countries with generous welfare states and flexible product/labour markets (Denmark, Netherlands) outperform countries with generous welfare and rigid markets in long-run employment and TFP.
123. `ordoliberal_co_determination_without_rent_seekng`: German-style co-determination predicts lower strike frequency and stronger manufacturing export performance than adversarial industrial relations, provided competition policy prevents rent extraction.
124. `ordoliberal_vocational_training_market_linkage`: Apprenticeship systems with strong employer-chamber involvement predict lower youth unemployment and better wage-employment matching than state-run vocational schools without market linkage.
125. `ordolibler_housing_market_social_housing_balance`: Countries that combine market-rate housing with portable housing allowances show lower homelessness and faster labour mobility than countries with large project-based social-housing estates.
126. `ordoliberal_healthcare_sickness_fund_competition`: German-style competing sickness funds with risk-adjustment predict lower cost growth and higher patient satisfaction than single-payer systems at similar income levels.
127. `ordoliberal_environmental_tax_shift_double_dividend`: Revenue-neutral environmental tax shifts (carbon tax plus payroll tax cut) predict lower emissions without net employment loss compared to command-and-control regulation.
128. `ordoliberal_labour_market_activiation_works`: Active labour-market policies with strong conditionality and rapid job-search support predict faster return to employment than passive benefit systems.
129. `ordoliberal_rental_market_tenancy_balance`: Tenancy laws that balance security of tenure with landlord property rights predict larger rental sectors, lower homelessness, and lower house-price volatility than extreme pro-tenant or pro-landlord regimes.
130. `ordoliberal_education_voucher_outcome_mixed`: School vouchers or tax-credit scholarships predict modest gains in graduation rates and test scores for disadvantaged students when combined with accountability rules, but not when unregulated.
131. `ordoliberal_minimum_wage_youth_disemployment`: High minimum wages relative to median predict larger youth disemployment and slower job-entry rates, especially when combined with high employer social contributions.
132. `ordoliberal_collective_bargaining_extension_trap`: Sectoral collective-bargaining extension to non-signatory firms predicts higher unemployment in small firms and reduced entry in covered sectors.
133. `ordoliberal_paternal_leave_flexible_design`: Flexible parental-leave designs (transferable between parents, part-time options) predict higher female labour-force participation and smaller gender wage gaps than rigid maternal-only leave.
134. `ordoliberal_energy_transition_market_instrument`: Carbon pricing with border adjustments predicts lower abatement cost and faster innovation than technology-specific subsidies and bans.
135. `ordoliberal_pension_age_indexation_sustainability`: Automatic pension-age indexation to life expectancy predicts lower old-age dependency cost and higher elderly employment than fixed retirement ages.
136. `ordolibler_unemployment_insurance_savings_accounts`: Individual unemployment-insurance savings accounts predict shorter unemployment duration and less seasonal cycling than pure pooled benefits.
137. `ordoliberal_disability_reform_work_incentive`: Disability-benefit reforms that tightened eligibility but expanded vocational rehabilitation predict higher employment rates among formerly disabled cohorts.
138. `ordoliberal_competition_healthcare_quality`: Hospital markets with more competing providers and outcome transparency show lower mortality and shorter waiting lists than centralised planning.
139. `ordoliberal_transport_infrastructure_user_pricing`: Road and congestion pricing predicts more efficient transport investment and lower peak congestion than tax-funded capacity expansion.
140. `ordoliberal_social_assistance_phase_out_design`: Social-assistance programmes with gradual benefit phase-out predict higher employment take-up and lower poverty traps than programmes with sharp cliffs.

## Track H: Free Market vs Intervention — Growth & Convergence (141–170)

141. `market_reform_episode_gdp_per_capita_persistence`: Market-oriented reform episodes lasting 15+ years predict stronger GDP-per-capita persistence into subsequent decades than interrupted or reversed reforms.
142. `state_led_planning_growth_fade_25yr`: State-led five-year planning economies show faster initial growth but sharper fade after 25 years than market economies with similar initial human capital.
143. `price_liberalisation_agricultural_output_surge`: Agricultural output surges most sharply in years following price liberalisation and de-collectivisation, controlling for weather.
144. `privatisation_method_mass_voucher_vs_trade_sale`: Mass-voucher privatisation predicts weaker long-run productivity than trade sales or management buyouts, but both outperform continued state ownership.
145. `trade_openness_manufacturing_productivity_spillover`: Unilateral trade liberalisation predicts faster manufacturing productivity growth through import competition and export discipline.
146. `capital_account_liberalisation_institutional_threshold`: Capital-account liberalisation predicts faster growth only above rule-of-law and regulatory-quality thresholds; below them it raises crisis frequency.
147. `market_exchange_rate_alignment_trade_growth`: Countries with market-determined exchange rates closer to purchasing-power parity show stronger trade-volume growth than countries with sustained overvaluation.
148. `state_bank_credit_allocation_growth_quality`: Higher state-bank credit share predicts lower loan quality and weaker long-run TFP growth than private-bank-dominated systems.
149. `regulatory_quality_fdi_spillover`: Stronger regulatory quality predicts larger FDI productivity spillovers to domestic firms through supply-chain linkage.
150. `tax_complexity_avoidance_industry_growth`: Higher tax complexity predicts larger tax-advisory and avoidance industries and lower productive investment share.
151. `corruption_state_ownership_interaction`: State ownership has negative growth effects where corruption is high and neutral or mixed effects where governance is strong.
152. `market_entry_deregulation_entrepreneurship_surge`: Deregulation of entry permits and licensing predicts higher firm-entry rates and lower informal-sector share.
153. `judicial_independence_contract_enforcement_investment`: Judicial independence predicts stronger investment growth, especially in contract-intensive sectors (finance, business services, complex manufacturing).
154. `property_rights_agricultural_investment`: Stronger property-rights protection predicts higher agricultural investment and faster yield growth, especially for tree crops and soil conservation.
155. `exchange_rate_volatility_investment_horizon`: Higher real exchange-rate volatility predicts shorter investment horizons and lower capital intensity in tradable sectors.
156. `financial_deepening_growth_non_linear`: Financial deepening predicts growth up to a threshold; beyond it, credit booms predict crisis risk that offsets deepening gains.
157. `infrastructure_privatisation_efficiency_gain`: Privatised infrastructure with regulated third-party access shows lower operating costs and higher investment than state operation.
158. `subsidised_industrial_zone_export_performance`: Export-processing zones with market-compatible rules show stronger export and employment growth than zones with heavy state direction and local-content requirements.
159. `unilateral_tariff_cut_consumption_welfare_gain`: Countries that cut tariffs unilaterally show larger consumption welfare gains than countries that maintained protection, even without reciprocity.
160. `state_pension_paygo_vs_funded_growth`: Transition from pay-as-you-go to funded pensions predicts higher national savings and capital deepening, but transition costs matter for fiscal sustainability.
161. `agricultural_export_ban_price_instability`: Countries that impose agricultural export bans during price spikes show larger domestic price volatility and lower long-run farm investment.
162. `energy_subsidy_reform_efficiency_gain`: Removal of fossil-fuel and electricity subsidies predicts more efficient energy use and faster renewable adoption than continued subsidy.
163. `state_monopoly_export_commodity_price_duty`: State commodity-marketing boards capture lower farm-gate prices and show more rent-seeking than private auction markets.
