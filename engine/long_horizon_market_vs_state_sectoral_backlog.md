# Long-Horizon Market vs State Sectoral Backlog: Agriculture, Technology, Healthcare, Housing

This backlog extends the market-prosperity vs state-direction test suite into four concrete sectors where interventionist and market-liberal claims sharply diverge. Every hypothesis is designed for multi-decade panels or long-difference natural experiments. All map to measurable policy/institution variables, not ideology labels.

---

## Track A: Agriculture & Land (1–25)

1. `collectivisation_agricultural_output_collapse_panel`: Countries that imposed large-scale agricultural collectivisation experienced deeper and more persistent declines in agricultural value added per worker than countries that maintained private or household tenure over comparable periods.
2. `household_responsibility_system_china_agricultural_surge`: China's agricultural output growth accelerated most sharply in provinces where the household responsibility system replaced collective farming earliest, controlling for weather and pre-reform output.
3. `land_titling_productivity_long_run_latam`: Countries that completed broad land-titling programs show stronger long-run agricultural productivity growth than countries with similar farm sizes but insecure tenure, after controlling for initial income.
4. `price_controls_food_output_decline_panel`: Countries that maintained agricultural price controls or state procurement floors below market-clearing levels for more than a decade show slower growth in agricultural value added than market-priced peers.
5. `smallholder_vs_plantation_yield_frontier`: Smallholder-dominant agricultural systems converge to higher total factor productivity in agriculture over 30-year windows than plantation-dominant systems, after controlling for crop mix and land quality.
6. `agricultural_subsidy_distortion_misallocation_panel`: Higher OECD-style agricultural subsidy intensity predicts weaker within-sector labour productivity growth over 20-year windows after controlling for capital deepening.
7. `water_markets_drought_resilience`: Regions with tradable water rights or water markets show smaller agricultural output losses during severe drought episodes than regions with administrative allocation.
8. `gm_crop_adoption_yield_convergence`: Countries that permitted genetically modified crop cultivation without prolonged moratoria experienced faster yield convergence to frontier agronomy over 1996–2024 than countries with bans or heavy regulatory delay.
9. `land_reform_compensation_investment_recovery`: Market-compatible land reforms with compensation show stronger post-reform agricultural investment and TFP recovery than expropriatory reforms, within a 25-year window.
10. `export_openness_agricultural_diversification`: Agricultural-export liberalisation predicts faster diversification into higher-value crops over 20-year windows than import-substitution agricultural policy.
11. `fertiliser_subsidy_environmental_cost_productivity`: Persistent fertiliser subsidies raise short-run yields but predict soil degradation and slower long-run TFP growth after 15 years.
12. `herder_sedentarisation_productivity_decline`: State-mandated sedentarisation of pastoral communities predicts long-run declines in livestock productivity per hectare compared to mobile grazing or market-based rangeland management.
13. `agricultural_services_privatisation_adoption_speed`: Privatisation of seed, extension, and veterinary services predicts faster adoption of improved varieties and lower livestock mortality than state-monopoly service provision.
14. `farm_size_regulation_inefficiency_panel`: Countries that imposed hard ceilings on farm size show lower agricultural labour productivity after 20 years than countries with market-determined scale, controlling for land abundance.
15. `agricultural_land_tax_productivity_incentive`: Higher land-value taxation on agricultural holdings predicts stronger long-run productivity growth than output-price subsidies, by encouraging efficient land use.
16. `cooperative_vs_state_procurement_milk_grains`: Producer cooperatives in dairy and grains achieve higher long-run total factor productivity than state procurement monopsonies, after controlling for capital access.
17. `irrigation_market_pricing_water_efficiency`: Irrigation districts with volumetric water pricing show higher crop-value-per-cubic-metre over 15-year panels than districts with flat-fee or free water allocation.
18. `forestry_concession_length_investment_sustainability`: Longer market-secure forestry concessions predict higher replanting investment and sustainable yield maintenance than short-term state licences.
19. `aquaculture_license_market_density_disease`: Aquaculture zones with tradable licensing and zoning show lower disease incidence and higher long-run output growth than zones with open-access or arbitrary permit allocation.
20. `ethanol_mandate_food_price_distortion`: Countries with binding ethanol mandates show higher food-price volatility and reduced non-fuel agricultural investment after 10-year horizons.
21. `cotton_monopsony_state_board_vs_market`: Abolition of state cotton-marketing boards predicts higher farmer prices, increased area under cotton, and faster yield growth within a decade.
22. `dairy_quota_abolition_output_response`: Removal of production quotas in dairy markets predicts output expansion and price reductions without persistent farm-income collapse, within a 10-year window.
23. `precision_ag_tech_adoption_property_rights`: Countries with stronger farm-level property-rights protection show faster adoption of precision-agriculture technologies over 2000–2024.
24. `agricultural_insurance_market_vs_state_indemnity`: Crop-insurance markets with actuarial pricing and private participation predict faster recovery from weather shocks than state indemnity schemes with political pricing.
25. `soil_carbon_market_payment_conservation`: Regions with private soil-carbon credit markets or conservation-payment contracts show stronger soil-health indicators over 10-year panels than command-and-control conservation set-asides.

## Track B: Technology, Innovation & Intellectual Property (26–50)

26. `patent_strength_innovation_output_non_linear`: Moderate patent protection predicts stronger long-run patenting and total factor productivity than very weak or very strong protection, but the TFP-maximising threshold is closer to market-oriented IP regimes than to weak or compulsory-licencing regimes.
27. `state_rd_spillover_vs_crowding_out_panel`: Public R&D complements private R&D in basic science and general-purpose technologies but crowds out private R&D in applied commercial sectors over 15–20 year windows.
28. `venture_capital_depth_frontier_innovation`: Higher venture-capital investment as a share of GDP predicts faster frontier total factor productivity growth and more high-impact patenting over 20-year panels.
29. `spectrum_auction_vs_administrative_allocation_telecom`: Countries that moved from administrative spectrum allocation to market auctions show faster mobile-broadband penetration, lower prices, and higher sectoral productivity within a decade.
30. `state_owned_tech_enterprise_productivity_drag`: State-owned or state-controlled firms in technology sectors show lower labour productivity growth and weaker patent quality than private competitors after controlling for scale and access to finance.
31. `open_source_vs_proprietary_software_innovation_rate`: Open-source-dominant software ecosystems generate faster incremental innovation and lower security-vulnerability persistence than closed-source monopoly ecosystems, but the effect is strongest when intellectual-property enforcement is clear enough to support dual-licencing business models.
32. `regulatory_sandbox_fintech_diffusion`: Introduction of regulatory sandboxes for financial technology predicts faster fintech adoption, lower remittance costs, and higher financial-inclusion rates than prescriptive licensing regimes.
33. `platform_competition_dissipates_monopoly_rent`: Technology markets with sustained multi-platform competition show lower prices, faster quality-adjusted improvement, and higher developer-entry rates than markets with winner-take-most structure reinforced by data exclusivity or interoperability barriers.
34. `industrial_policy_semiconductor_subsidy_geographic_spillover`: Location-targeted semiconductor subsidies raise local employment but do not produce positive TFP spillovers to non-subsidised firms in the same commuting zone over 10-year windows.
35. `compulsory_licensing_pharma_innovation_reduction`: Episodes of broad compulsory pharmaceutical licensing predict weaker subsequent domestic R&D spending and fewer new molecular entities from affected firms over 10–15 year windows.
36. `net_neutrality_repeal_investment_speed`: Jurisdictions that repealed net-neutrality rules show faster fixed-broadband investment and speed upgrades than jurisdictions that maintained common-carrier regulation, after controlling for population density.
37. `cryptocurrency_legal_clarity_entrepreneurship`: Countries that provided clear property-rights and tax treatment for digital assets earlier show higher blockchain-related startup density and venture financing over 2015–2024.
38. `tech_transfer_restrictions_slow_catch_up`: Countries that imposed strict local-content or technology-transfer requirements on foreign direct investment show slower total factor productivity convergence in high-tech sectors over 20-year windows.
39. `standard_essential_patent_disclosure_speed`: Jurisdictions with faster standard-essential patent disclosure and fair-reasonable-and-non-discriminatory enforcement show earlier 5G/WiFi equipment availability and lower royalty stacks.
40. `university_ip_bayh_dole_style_commercialisation`: University technology-transfer offices with strong patenting and licensing incentives produce more spinoffs and licensed revenues than state-retained or professor-retained regimes, but only where capital markets can fund translation.
41. `data_localisation_cost_cloud_adoption`: Mandatory data-localisation requirements predict higher cloud-computing prices and slower enterprise cloud adoption than cross-border-permissive regimes over 5–10 year windows.
42. `ai_compute_cluster_market_allocation_efficiency`: Market-priced energy and land for data centres predict more efficient compute-per-watt and faster model-training throughput than subsidised or state-directed cluster placement.
43. `state_mandated_tech_standard_lock_in`: Governments that imposed unique national technology standards without international interoperability show slower consumer adoption and higher long-run obsolescence costs than market-harmonised standard adopters.
44. `pharma_parallel_trade_price_innovation_tradeoff`: Markets that permitted parallel trade in pharmaceuticals show lower consumer prices but weaker subsequent launch sequencing and fewer patented launches over 10-year panels.
45. `electric_vehicle_mandate_vs_market_adoption_path`: Consumer subsidies and charging-network markets predict more durable electric-vehicle adoption than production quotas or manufacturer-mandate regimes after subsidy withdrawal.
46. `space_launch_privatisation_cost_reduction`: Privatisation of satellite launch services and reusable-rocket competition predict steeper cost-per-kilogram declines than state-monopoly launch programmes over comparable periods.
47. `gene_therapy_approval_speed_market_access`: Jurisdictions with adaptive licensing and outcome-based reimbursement for gene therapies show faster patient access and larger clinical-trial pipelines than jurisdictions with rigid cost-effectiveness thresholds and price caps.
48. `quantum_computing_state_picking_vs_ecosystem`: Countries that directed quantum funding through broad ecosystem grants and prizes show more startup formation and patent diversity than countries that concentrated funding on a few state-championed labs.
49. `cybersecurity_liability_market_insurance`: Markets with clear cybersecurity-liability rules and cyber-insurance development show faster enterprise security investment and fewer large breaches than regimes with vague liability or state-mandated compliance checklists.
50. `autonomous_vehicle_regulatory_competition_safety`: Jurisdictions that permitted bottom-up regulatory competition across states or provinces show faster testing-mile accumulation and lower disengagement rates than jurisdictions with top-down prohibition-then-permission regimes.

## Track C: Healthcare Systems & Outcomes (51–75)

51. `market_healthcare_supply_mortality_amenable_panel`: Countries with higher private-hospital bed share and physician-market entry show larger declines in amenable mortality over 20-year panels than countries with rigid state monopoly provision at similar income levels.
52. `drug_price_control_pipeline_innovation_decline`: Countries with stringent drug-price controls or external reference pricing show fewer new molecular entity launches per capita and weaker domestic biopharma R&D over 15-year windows.
53. `certificate_of_need_hospital_access_restrictions`: US states with Certificate-of-Need laws show slower growth in hospital capacity, higher prices, and weaker mortality amelioration than non-CON states over 1975–2024.
54. `scope_of_practice_expansion_health_access`: Jurisdictions that expanded nurse-practitioner and pharmacist scope of practice show faster primary-care access growth and lower emergency-department utilisation than restrictive-licensing jurisdictions.
55. `health_savings_account_preventive_spending`: Countries with substantial health-savings-account or catastrophic-plus-savings designs show stronger long-run cost growth containment without worse age-adjusted mortality than comprehensive public-insurance peers.
56. `medical_tourism_competitive_pressure_home_prices`: Countries that permitted cross-border medical tourism and recognised foreign hospital accreditation show faster domestic price convergence to international benchmarks and shorter waiting lists.
57. `telemedicine_deregulation_rural_access`: Jurisdictions that deregulated telemedicine licensing and reimbursement during 2010–2024 show larger improvements in rural specialist-access and chronic-disease management than jurisdictions that maintained in-person-only requirements.
58. `physician_supply_cap_residency_constraint_mortality`: Countries with state-capped medical-school or residency places show slower physician-supply growth and weaker mortality improvement in ageing populations than countries with market-responsive training capacity.
59. `pharma_parallel_approval_faster_access`: Jurisdictions with mutual recognition or streamlined parallel drug approval show faster time-to-market for new therapies and lower mortality from treatable conditions.
60. `dialysis_market_competition_outcome_quality`: Kidney-dialysis markets with multiple competing providers and outcome-based reimbursement show lower mortality and hospitalisation rates than single-payer or capitated monopsony models.
61. `vaccine_procurement_advance_market_commitment_speed`: Advance-market-commitment and market-pulled vaccine procurement predicts faster pandemic-vaccine availability than state-manufacturing or cost-plus procurement alone.
62. `direct_primary_care_subscription_cost_stability`: Markets with significant direct-primary-care or subscription-based primary care show lower total cost of care growth and higher patient-reported access over 10-year panels.
63. `imaging_equipment_market_entry_wait_times`: Countries that liberalised medical-imaging equipment licensing and private-clinic entry show larger reductions in diagnostic-imaging wait times than countries with state-rationed capital budgets.
64. `generic_substitution_mandate_savings_no_harm`: Mandatory generic substitution at pharmacy level predicts large pharmaceutical spending reductions without detectable increases in adverse events or hospitalisation over 10-year panels.
65. `long_term_cinsurance_market_vs_unfunded_benefit`: Countries with private long-term-care insurance markets show lower unfunded public-liability accumulation and more flexible supply responses to ageing than pay-as-you-go public benefit systems.
66. `hospital_ownership_nonprofit_vs_state_efficiency`: Non-profit and for-profit hospital competition predicts higher labour productivity and lower cost per adjusted discharge than state-owned hospital systems at similar income and case-mix levels.
67. `biosimilar_approval_speed_market_entry`: Faster biosimilar regulatory approval and interchangeability designation predict steeper biologic-price declines and higher volume uptake without detectable immunogenicity signals.
68. `ophthalmology_laser_surgery_market_price_trajectory`: Cataract and refractive surgery markets with high private competition show steep long-run real-price declines and rapid technology adoption compared to state-provision waiting-list systems.
69. `mental_health_parity_deregulated_provider_entry`: Jurisdictions that deregulated mental-health provider credentialing and expanded digital-therapy markets show larger reductions in untreated depression prevalence than jurisdictions with psychiatrist-only gatekeeping.
70. `organ_transplant_market_incentive_cadaveric_supply`: Countries with opt-out organ donation and compensation-for-expenses frameworks achieve higher cadaveric organ procurement rates and shorter transplant wait lists than opt-in or pure-altruism regimes.
71. `mri_ct_density_regulatory_entry_correlation`: Countries with lower barriers to diagnostic-imaging clinic entry show higher scanners per capita and lower cancer-stage-at-diagnosis over 15-year panels.
72. `retail_clinic_minute_clinic_cost_substitution`: Markets with unrestrictive retail-clinic and pharmacy-clinic entry show lower emergency-department utilisation for low-acuity visits and lower total payer spending.
73. `pharma_patent_term_restoration_launch_timing`: Jurisdictions with patent-term restoration for regulatory-review delay show earlier launch of innovative therapies and fewer strategic launch delays.
74. `healthcare_tax_exclusion_employer_crowd_out`: Large tax subsidies for employer-sponsored insurance predict higher healthcare-spending shares of GDP but do not produce lower age-adjusted mortality than individual-market or voucher regimes over 20-year windows.
75. `midwife_birth_center_autonomy_outcomes`: Jurisdictions with autonomous midwife and birth-centre practice show lower caesarean rates and comparable or better neonatal outcomes than jurisdictions with mandatory physician supervision.

## Track D: Housing & Urban Development (76–95)

76. `rent_control_housing_supply_destruction_panel`: Cities that imposed or maintained stringent rent control show slower growth in the private rental stock, higher market rents for uncontrolled units, and longer commuting times over 20-year panels.
77. `zoning_deregulation_housing_affordability`: Jurisdictions that upzoned or deregulated single-family-only zoning show faster housing-permit growth and lower real rent growth than comparable jurisdictions that maintained restrictive zoning.
78. `housing_voucher_mobility_vs_project_concentration`: Tenant-based housing vouchers predict better long-run neighbourhood quality, educational attainment, and earnings for children than project-based public housing in high-poverty census tracts.
79. `land_value_tax_vacant_lot_utilisation`: Jurisdictions with higher land-value taxation or split-rate property taxes show lower vacancy rates and faster redevelopment of underused parcels than jurisdictions with pure improvement taxes.
80. `growth_boundary_urban_house_price_inflation`: Urban growth boundaries or greenbelts predict steeper house-price-to-income ratios and lower construction-employment growth than market-responsive metropolitan expansion over 20-year windows.
81. `minimum_lot_size_housing_cost_elasticity`: Municipalities with larger minimum-lot-size requirements show lower housing-supply elasticity and larger house-price responses to demand shocks.
82. `inclusionary_zoning_unit_offset_cost`: Mandatory inclusionary-zoning requirements predict offsetting reductions in overall permissible density and net zero or negative affordable-unit production compared to market-rate-plus-fee regimes.
83. `building_height_limit_downtown_productivity`: Cities with strict building-height restrictions near transit nodes show lower agglomeration productivity and higher office rents than cities with market-height or transferable-development-right regimes.
84. `historic_preservation_excess_cost_adaptation`: Broad historic-district designation predicts higher maintenance costs, lower energy-efficiency retrofit rates, and reduced household turnover than targeted landmark protection.
85. `social_housing_privatisation_tenant_wealth`: Right-to-buy or social-housing privatisation programmes that transferred units to sitting tenants at discounts predict higher subsequent household wealth accumulation and home-maintenance investment than continued state ownership.
86. `construction_permit_time_gdp_cost`: Countries with longer construction-permit approval times show higher construction costs, lower housing-output elasticity, and larger informal-housing shares over 15-year panels.
87. `property_tax_limit_municipal_fiscal_health`: State-level property-tax limits predict slower local infrastructure investment, reduced public-service quality, and offsetting sales-tax increases without lower overall tax burdens.
88. `rental_unrestricted_eviction_rent_response`: Jurisdictions that maintained cause-based or unrestricted eviction procedures show lower equilibrium rents and higher rental-housing turnover than just-cause-eviction jurisdictions.
89. `cooperative_housing_vs_state_developer_efficiency`: Housing cooperatives and community land trusts achieve lower per-unit operating costs and higher resident satisfaction than municipally owned social housing at similar income and building-age profiles.
90. `mortgage_interest_deduction_homeownership_rate`: Countries with large mortgage-interest tax deductions do not show higher long-run homeownership rates than countries with neutral tax treatment, but do show higher house-price-to-income ratios.
91. `floor_area_ratio_incentive_transfer_density`: Cities with transferable development rights or floor-area-ratio bonuses show faster redevelopment of obsolete industrial land and higher overall residential density than cities with fixed FAR.
92. `short_term_rental_regulation_hotel_price_spillover`: Cities that banned or severely restricted short-term rentals show higher hotel-price growth and lower tourist-volume growth than cities with registration-and-tax regimes.
93. `parking_minimum_abolition_housing_cost`: Cities that abolished parking-minimum requirements near transit show lower per-unit construction costs and faster multifamily permitting than cities that retained mandates.
94. `adaptive_reuse_regulation_lightness_supply`: Jurisdictions with lighter adaptive-reuse and change-of-use permitting show faster conversion of obsolete office stock to residential and lower vacancy rates during demand shifts.
95. `infrastructure_financing_impact_fee_growth`: Municipalities that funded infrastructure via impact fees rather than general taxation show faster permit growth and less opposition to new development, because costs are capitalised into prices transparently.

## Track E: Cross-Sector Market Mechanisms (96–100)

96. `price_signal_sectoral_reallocation_speed`: Countries with stronger market-price signals and lower sectoral-entry barriers show faster labour reallocation from declining to expanding sectors during terms-of-trade shocks.
97. `privatisation_method_competition_vs_revenue`: Mass-privatisation programmes that emphasised competitive restructuring before sale produced stronger long-run productivity gains than revenue-maximising sales to insider owners or state-retained golden shares.
98. `regulatory_predictability_cross_sector_investment`: Jurisdictions with stable, rules-based regulation show higher cross-sector private investment and faster technology adoption than jurisdictions with frequent discretionary intervention, after controlling for initial income.
99. `market_entry_uniform_code_productivity`: Countries with unified national building, sanitary, and product standards show faster cross-regional firm entry and higher multi-establishment productivity than countries with fragmented subnational regulation.
100. `sector_neutral_tax_vs_exemption_cumulation`: Tax systems with low rates and broad bases predict stronger long-run investment and employment growth across agriculture, technology, healthcare, and construction than systems with high rates and sector-specific exemption cumulation.
