# Long-Horizon Market Prosperity vs Developmentalist Catch-Up Backlog

This backlog operationalizes a specific double-crux:

- Developmentalist policies may produce relatively easy catch-up wins when countries are far from the frontier and have large infrastructure, literacy, electrification, or basic-capability gaps.
- The stronger market-liberal claim is that durable prosperity, frontier innovation, and high-income persistence over 25-75 year windows come more from market competition, property rights, openness, price signals, capital discipline, and limited discretionary state allocation than from permanent developmental-state steering.

Use these as candidate hypotheses. Promote only after checking data availability, scope alignment, and whether the claim maps better to Austrian, Classical Liberal, Chicago Monetarist, Ordoliberal, Institutional, Developmentalist, Social Democratic, or Empirical Pragmatist positions.

## Track A: Catch-Up vs Frontier Persistence

1. `frontier_income_persistence_market_institutions_1960_2024`: Countries in the top quartile of market-institution scores in 1960 retain high-income frontier status through 2024 more often than countries with high state-directed-credit intensity but weaker property-rights scores.
2. `catch_up_growth_fades_after_middle_income_threshold`: Developmentalist catch-up economies show faster growth before reaching 40 percent of US GDP per capita, but their growth premium shrinks or reverses after crossing that threshold.
3. `frontier_tfp_market_liberal_panel_1970_2024`: Among OECD and high-income Asian economies, long-run TFP growth is higher where product-market regulation and state ownership are lower.
4. `high_income_escape_market_openness_1950_2024`: Countries escaping middle income into high income are more likely to combine export openness with domestic market competition than export promotion with persistent domestic protection.
5. `long_run_consumption_frontier_market_score_1970_2024`: Household consumption per capita converges more strongly to the US frontier in countries with higher long-run economic-freedom scores.
6. `developmentalist_growth_premium_low_income_only`: The growth advantage of developmentalist policy packages is concentrated among low-income countries and disappears in upper-middle-income or high-income samples.
7. `frontier_real_wage_growth_market_competition_1980_2024`: Real wage growth at the frontier is stronger in countries with more competitive product markets and lower barriers to firm entry.
8. `catch_up_capital_deepening_not_tfp`: Developmentalist catch-up gains are explained more by capital deepening and labor reallocation than by sustained TFP acceleration.
9. `market_reform_duration_growth_persistence`: Market-oriented reform episodes that persist for at least 20 years generate more durable GDP-per-capita gains than state-led industrial-policy episodes of similar initial intensity.
10. `frontier_income_volatility_state_allocation`: High state-directed allocation is associated with larger boom-bust cycles after middle-income status, even when early catch-up growth is strong.

## Track B: East Asian Double-Crux

11. `korea_post_chaebol_liberalisation_frontier_growth`: South Korea's post-1997 liberalisation and governance reforms explain a larger share of post-crisis frontier convergence than earlier heavy-and-chemical industrial policy.
12. `taiwan_sme_competition_vs_state_planning_growth`: Taiwan's long-run productivity performance is better predicted by SME competition and export-market discipline than by direct state planning intensity.
13. `japan_miti_success_then_stagnation_panel`: Japan's MITI-era catch-up is positive, but post-1990 stagnation is associated with protected sectors, zombie lending, and weak domestic competition.
14. `singapore_state_capacity_market_openness_combo`: Singapore's long-run prosperity is better predicted by extreme trade openness, rule of law, and competitive services than by state ownership alone.
15. `hong_kong_no_industrial_policy_frontier_comparison`: Hong Kong's long-run income convergence without classic industrial policy matches or exceeds developmentalist Asian comparators after controlling for initial income.
16. `malaysia_developmentalist_plateau_1990_2024`: Malaysia's post-1990 growth plateau is associated with protected national-champion policy and ethnic allocation constraints after early catch-up gains.
17. `thailand_middle_income_plateau_state_coordination`: Thailand's middle-income plateau is associated with weaker competition and lower institutional quality despite earlier export-led development.
18. `china_post_wto_market_opening_vs_subsidy_tfp`: China's strongest TFP acceleration occurs after WTO-linked market opening, while later subsidy-heavy state direction is associated with weaker TFP growth.
19. `vietnam_doi_moi_private_sector_growth_share`: Vietnam's post-Doi Moi growth is more strongly associated with private-sector entry and trade openness than with SOE expansion.
20. `east_asia_export_discipline_vs_domestic_protection`: East Asian success cases outperform because export discipline forced market tests, while protected domestic industrial policy predicts weaker long-run productivity.

## Track C: Liberalisation Episodes and Long Windows

21. `estonia_market_reform_30yr_income_convergence`: Estonia's radical market reform predicts stronger 1991-2024 GDP-per-capita convergence than more gradual post-Soviet reform comparators.
22. `poland_market_transition_30yr_growth`: Poland's sustained market transition predicts stronger long-run income growth than CEE countries with weaker competition and slower privatisation.
23. `czech_slovak_market_transition_comparison`: Czech and Slovak long-run prosperity differences are better predicted by market institutions and FDI openness than by state industrial strategy.
24. `new_zealand_reform_long_run_productivity_recheck`: New Zealand's 1984-1993 liberalisation improves long-run macro stability and tradables productivity, but may not improve aggregate productivity enough to support strong market claims.
25. `ireland_market_opening_fdi_frontier_1987_2024`: Ireland's long-run convergence is better predicted by trade openness, tax competitiveness, and FDI entry than by classic industrial planning.
26. `uk_thatcher_market_reform_40yr_services_frontier`: UK market reforms predict stronger long-run services productivity and employment than comparable European corporatist regimes.
27. `sweden_1990s_market_reform_recovery`: Sweden's post-crisis market reforms and fiscal rules predict stronger 1995-2024 growth than its pre-crisis state-expansion model.
28. `canada_market_liberalisation_vs_state_industry_1988_2024`: Canada's long-run prosperity after FTA/NAFTA is more associated with market openness than with national industrial-policy initiatives.
29. `australia_hawke_keating_reform_long_run`: Australia's long expansion after Hawke-Keating reforms is better predicted by market liberalisation than by sector-specific state direction.
30. `chile_market_reform_long_horizon_with_democracy`: Chile's long-run income convergence is stronger after market reforms plus democratic institutional repair than under earlier state-led import substitution.

## Track D: Industrial Policy Plateau and Failure Modes

31. `national_champions_long_run_productivity_drag`: National-champion sectors show lower 20-year productivity growth than comparable competitive sectors after initial scale-up.
32. `state_credit_allocation_zombie_firm_persistence`: Persistent state-directed credit predicts higher zombie-firm shares and lower TFP growth over 15-30 year windows.
33. `subsidy_duration_declining_returns_panel`: Industrial subsidies have positive early output effects but declining or negative productivity effects when maintained beyond 10 years.
34. `import_substitution_long_run_export_underperformance`: Import-substitution episodes underperform export-open episodes in 25-year GDP-per-capita and productivity windows.
35. `industrial_policy_without_exit_discipline_failure`: Industrial policy episodes without clear exit or export-discipline rules generate weaker long-run outcomes than episodes with hard performance conditions.
36. `state_owned_enterprise_share_growth_plateau`: Higher SOE shares predict slower long-run productivity growth once basic infrastructure and literacy gaps are controlled.
37. `politicised_credit_election_cycle_growth_drag`: Politicised development-bank lending around election cycles predicts weaker subsequent productivity and higher nonperforming loans.
38. `protected_infant_industries_fail_to_mature`: Infant-industry protection that persists longer than 15 years predicts lower export competitiveness than time-limited protection.
39. `resource_developmentalism_rent_seeking_trap`: Resource-funded developmentalist states produce early public-investment gains but weaker long-run diversification than market-open resource peers.
40. `industrial_policy_corruption_interaction`: Industrial policy effectiveness depends on governance; in low-rule-of-law samples, state allocation predicts corruption and lower long-run growth.

## Track E: Competition, Entry, and Firm Dynamics

41. `firm_entry_rate_long_run_productivity`: Higher firm-entry rates predict stronger 20-year productivity growth across OECD and emerging-market panels.
42. `business_dynamism_frontier_income_growth`: Frontier economies with higher business dynamism maintain stronger real income growth than those with low churn and high incumbent protection.
43. `product_market_regulation_tfp_30yr_panel`: Lower product-market regulation predicts higher long-run TFP growth after controlling for education, capital deepening, and initial income.
44. `competition_policy_enforcement_innovation`: Stronger antitrust and competition enforcement predicts higher patent quality and productivity growth over 20-year windows.
45. `licensing_burden_income_mobility`: Occupational and business licensing burdens predict weaker entrepreneurship and lower intergenerational income mobility over long horizons.
46. `startup_density_frontier_prosperity`: Startup density predicts frontier prosperity better than industrial-policy spending among high-income economies.
47. `incumbent_subsidy_market_share_persistence`: Subsidies to incumbent firms predict persistent market concentration and weaker productivity growth.
48. `bankruptcy_law_efficiency_capital_reallocation`: Faster insolvency and bankruptcy resolution predicts stronger post-shock productivity recovery than discretionary rescue policy.
49. `price_signal_distortion_capital_misallocation`: Price controls and directed input subsidies predict higher capital misallocation and lower long-run productivity.
50. `sectoral_competition_services_productivity`: Services-sector competition predicts long-run prosperity better than manufacturing-specific industrial policy in high-income economies.

## Track F: Institutions and Property Rights

51. `property_rights_long_run_income_frontier`: Property-rights protection predicts 40-year income growth more strongly than state investment share.
52. `rule_of_law_market_reform_complementarity`: Market reforms produce durable gains only when rule-of-law scores exceed a minimum threshold.
53. `contract_enforcement_fdi_productivity_spillovers`: Contract enforcement predicts whether FDI produces productivity spillovers rather than enclave effects.
54. `judicial_independence_growth_persistence`: Judicial independence predicts lower investment volatility and stronger long-run growth after reform episodes.
55. `expropriation_risk_investment_horizon`: Higher expropriation risk predicts shorter investment horizons and lower capital intensity in tradable sectors.
56. `regulatory_predictability_frontier_innovation`: Regulatory predictability predicts frontier innovation better than direct public R&D subsidies.
57. `corruption_state_allocation_growth_interaction`: State allocation has negative long-run growth effects when corruption is high, but neutral or positive effects when corruption is low.
58. `decentralized_property_rights_agricultural_productivity`: Secure private or household land-use rights predict stronger agricultural productivity than collective allocation systems over long windows.
59. `policy_uncertainty_private_investment`: Higher policy uncertainty predicts lower private investment and slower long-run productivity after state-led reform shifts.
60. `constitutional_fiscal_rules_growth_stability`: Fiscal rules and institutional constraints predict more durable prosperity than discretionary state investment surges.

## Track G: Trade, Openness, and Global Integration

61. `trade_openness_long_run_income_convergence`: Trade openness predicts long-run GDP-per-capita convergence more strongly than industrial-policy intensity.
62. `unilateral_tariff_liberalisation_growth_20yr`: Countries undertaking unilateral tariff liberalisation experience stronger 20-year productivity and consumption growth than protected peers.
63. `export_complexity_market_access_vs_subsidy`: Export complexity rises more after market access and competition reforms than after subsidy-only industrial policy.
64. `wto_accession_productivity_spillover_panel`: WTO accession predicts productivity spillovers through competition and market access, especially when domestic entry barriers fall.
65. `fdi_opening_supplier_productivity`: FDI openness predicts domestic supplier productivity growth when paired with competition, but not when paired with protected national champions.
66. `capital_account_openness_institutional_threshold`: Capital openness supports long-run prosperity only above institutional-quality thresholds; below them it raises crisis risk.
67. `services_trade_liberalisation_frontier_growth`: Services trade liberalisation predicts high-income productivity better than goods-sector industrial policy after 1990.
68. `global_value_chain_participation_upgrade`: GVC participation predicts income upgrading when firms can enter and exit freely, but not when rents are reserved for protected incumbents.
69. `export_promotion_without_protection`: Export promotion combined with domestic competition outperforms export promotion combined with domestic protection.
70. `tariff_protection_duration_growth_drag`: Longer protection duration predicts weaker subsequent export competitiveness and lower consumer welfare.

## Track H: Welfare, Fiscal Burden, and Long-Run Prosperity

71. `tax_burden_frontier_growth_non_linear`: At high-income levels, very high tax burdens predict weaker long-run productivity growth unless paired with unusually high state capacity and labor flexibility.
72. `welfare_state_market_flexibility_complement`: Large welfare states sustain prosperity when paired with market flexibility, openness, and fiscal discipline, not when paired with rigid product and labor markets.
73. `public_spending_composition_growth`: Public investment in basic infrastructure has strong catch-up returns, while persistent transfers and subsidies have weaker long-run growth effects.
74. `debt_overhang_private_investment_30yr`: High public-debt overhang predicts lower private investment and slower prosperity gains over 30-year windows.
75. `pension_forced_saving_capital_deepening`: Forced-saving pension systems raise capital deepening in catch-up periods but do not guarantee frontier productivity growth.
76. `transfer_expansion_work_incentive_long_run`: Transfer expansions without work incentives predict lower labor-force participation over long windows relative to negative-income-tax or earned-income designs.
77. `fiscal_consolidation_credibility_growth`: Credible fiscal consolidation predicts stronger long-run investment and income growth than repeated discretionary stimulus in mature economies.
78. `government_consumption_share_tfp`: Higher government-consumption shares predict weaker TFP growth after controlling for public investment, education, and health.
79. `tax_simplicity_entrepreneurship_panel`: Simpler tax systems predict higher entrepreneurship and small-firm growth over 20-year windows.
80. `welfare_architecture_market_openness_nordic`: Nordic prosperity is better explained by welfare plus openness and labor flexibility than by welfare-state size alone.

## Track I: Human Prosperity, Migration, and Revealed Preference

81. `net_migration_revealed_preference_market_institutions`: Net migration flows favor countries with stronger market institutions after controlling for income, language, and conflict.
82. `quality_adjusted_consumption_market_liberal_panel`: Quality-adjusted household consumption growth is stronger in market-liberal economies than in state-directed peers over 30-year windows.
83. `housing_supply_flexibility_real_wage`: Real disposable wage growth is higher where housing supply is flexible, even if headline GDP growth is similar.
84. `consumer_choice_variety_trade_market_reform`: Consumer variety and price-adjusted welfare improve more after trade and competition reforms than after state industrial policy.
85. `life_satisfaction_income_market_institutions`: Life satisfaction gains at high income are more associated with income growth, employment opportunity, and institutional trust than with state-planning intensity.
86. `brain_drain_state_directed_economies`: Skilled emigration is higher from state-directed economies with weak market opportunity, even when public investment is high.
87. `diaspora_return_market_reform`: Diaspora return rates rise after credible market and property-rights reforms.
88. `middle_class_asset_building_market_depth`: Middle-class asset accumulation is stronger where capital markets are deeper and property rights more secure.
89. `informality_market_entry_barriers`: Informality persists where entry barriers and regulation remain high, even when the state invests heavily in development projects.
90. `poverty_exit_durable_jobs_vs_transfer`: Durable poverty exit is more strongly associated with private job creation than with transfer expansion alone over 15-25 year windows.

## Track J: Adversarial and Boundary Tests

91. `market_reform_without_state_capacity_failure`: Market reform without minimum state capacity fails to produce long-run prosperity, protecting against naive laissez-faire claims.
92. `free_market_shock_therapy_social_cost`: Rapid market liberalisation under weak institutions produces large short-run welfare losses that may persist for at least a decade.
93. `industrial_policy_high_governance_success`: Industrial policy succeeds over long windows in high-governance states with export discipline, challenging a blanket free-market claim.
94. `public_rd_frontier_innovation_complement`: Public R&D complements private-market innovation at the frontier, especially in defense, health, and general-purpose technologies.
95. `infrastructure_gap_state_investment_high_return`: State-led infrastructure investment has high returns in countries below basic access thresholds, confirming developmentalist catch-up logic.
96. `market_openness_inequality_backlash_risk`: Market openness without adjustment institutions raises inequality or regional divergence enough to trigger policy reversal.
97. `natural_monopoly_private_failure`: Privatization of natural monopolies without strong regulation fails to improve long-run consumer welfare.
98. `financial_liberalisation_crisis_risk`: Financial liberalisation without prudential regulation raises crisis risk and can erase long-run market-reform gains.
99. `labour_flexibility_security_complement`: Labor-market flexibility improves long-run prosperity only when paired with retraining, relocation, or income-smoothing institutions.
100. `developmental_state_to_market_transition_success`: The strongest long-run performers are countries that use developmentalist tools for catch-up, then transition toward market competition, fiscal discipline, and rule-bound institutions before frontier convergence stalls.

## Promotion Notes

- Best first runnable wave: items 1-10, 21-30, 41-50, 61-70, and 91-100 because they can use broad panels and existing WDI, Penn World Table, OECD PMR, Fraser/Heritage, V-Dem/WGI, WIPO, World Bank WITS, and migration datasets.
- Strongest scoreboard targets: Classical Liberal, Austrian, Ordoliberal, Chicago Monetarist, New Institutional, Developmentalist, Social Democratic, and Empirical Pragmatist.
- Key design guardrail: separate low-income catch-up windows from high-income/frontier windows. Otherwise developmentalism gets easy wins from basic-capability closure while market-liberal claims get unfairly tested in contexts where state capacity is not yet built.
