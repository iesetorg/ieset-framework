# Steelman — Schröder Agenda 2010

**The strongest argument against the framework's positive coding of Agenda 2010:**

The post-2005 German outperformance attributed to the Hartz reforms is substantially a product of trade-weighted competitiveness within a fixed-rate euro area, not of labour-market liberalisation per se. Germany entered the euro at a favourable parity, wage moderation pre-dated Hartz (Bündnis für Arbeit 1999), and German export performance benefited disproportionately from Chinese growth demand for capital goods. Attributing the outperformance to Hartz specifically commits a sequencing error.

Specific objections the framework must engage with:

1. **Within-euro real exchange-rate advantage.** Germany's post-2000 current-account surplus is partly structural, driven by productivity differentials fixed into the euro at entry. Labour-market reform contributed at the margin; monetary geography did the heavy lifting.

2. **Distributional costs were real.** The Hartz IV working-poor category expanded. Real wages at the bottom stagnated or declined through 2009. The "success" metric (aggregate unemployment fall) obscured the compositional shift toward low-wage and part-time employment.

3. **Export-dependence vulnerability.** The reform package tilted Germany toward an export-heavy model that produced large surpluses in good years but extreme vulnerability to Chinese demand shocks and euro-area demand weakness. The 2022–2025 energy shock exposed the downside.

4. **Counterfactual unclear.** Southern European labour-market reforms of similar content (Spain 2012, Italy 2014/2016) have not produced comparable outcomes, suggesting the German result depended on institutional complements (dual-track vocational training, works councils, Kurzarbeit) that the Hartz reforms neither created nor targeted.

**How this steelman should shape the result card:**

Linked hypotheses should run decompositions that separate (a) euro-entry real exchange-rate effects, (b) pre-Hartz wage moderation, (c) Hartz-specific labour-market elasticity, (d) global demand composition. Only the residual attributable to (c) counts as evidence for the movement's coding. The framework should report all four components transparently, not the aggregate.
