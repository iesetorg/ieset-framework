# Steelman — Colombia market-continuity era (Uribe / Santos / Duque, 2002-2022)

**The strongest arguments against coding 2002-2022 as an unambiguous market-continuity success:**

1. **Security-policy gains came with documented human-rights costs.** The "falsos positivos" extrajudicial-killings scandal, DAS intelligence abuses during the Uribe administration, and paramilitary-successor groups ("Bacrim") all reflect that the homicide-rate decline was achieved partly through methods the framework's institutional-quality axis cannot endorse without qualification. Coding institutional.rule_of_law as strongly positive overstates the uncontested portion.

2. **The 2016 FARC peace accord is only partially implemented.** The rural-reform chapter (Punto 1 — Reforma Rural Integral) and the illicit-crop-substitution chapter (Punto 4) missed almost every schedule target under the Duque administration, and coca cultivation expanded after 2017. Treating the 2016 accord as a completed institutional achievement overstates its measurable effect.

3. **Distributional outcomes were mediocre at best.** Gini coefficient declined modestly (~0.57 to ~0.52) across the 20-year window but remained among the highest in the OECD convergence pool; real wages for the bottom deciles stagnated; the 2019 and 2021 paro nacional cycles reflected political exhaustion of the continuity coalition. The era produced macroeconomic stability without distributional transformation.

4. **Hydrocarbons dependence was not meaningfully reduced.** Oil + coal + derivatives remained ~40% of exports throughout the period. The 2014-2016 commodity cycle exposed the structural vulnerability; Colombia absorbed it better than Venezuela but the dependency was a choice, not a binding constraint on the policy architecture.

5. **The Duque 2018-2022 sub-period is arguably a distinct movement.** COVID response (Ingreso Solidario ~1.2% of GDP, historic fiscal deficit), 2021 tax-reform social protests that forced legislative retreat, and capital-markets modernisation are qualitatively different from Uribe-era security-and-opening or Santos-era peace-and-trade agendas. A v1.1 split may be warranted.

**How this should shape the result card:**

- Report the homicide-rate decline and the falsos-positivos / paramilitary-successor trajectories jointly so the institutional-quality coding is honest.
- Present distributional outcomes (Gini, labour share, rural poverty headcount) alongside the aggregate growth and FDI record. The era's aggregate record is substantially stronger than its distributional record.
- Treat this movement as the before-Petro baseline but separate Uribe / Santos / Duque sub-phases in follow-up runs if post-v1 analysis identifies material heterogeneity.
- Acknowledge that the 20-year aggregate includes the 2014-2016 commodity-cycle compression, the 2020 COVID compression, and the 2021 protest cycle; the headline averages smooth over substantial within-period variation.
- The US FTA 2012 and EU FTA 2013 entry-into-force effects on trade composition and productivity are separately testable under trade_liberalisation_growth_effect.
