# Steelman — Norway handlingsregel 2001

**The strongest argument against the framework's market-liberal coding:**

Norway's outcomes are driven by oil, not by the handlingsregel. The fiscal rule is a symptom of unusual circumstances (hugely valuable resource rents + small homogeneous society + pre-existing Scandinavian institutional trust), not a causal lever that other resource-rich countries could replicate to similar effect. Crediting the rule itself for Norwegian post-2001 performance imports a causation claim the counterfactual doesn't support.

Specific objections:

1. **Resource-curse counterfactual is limited.** The cases cited as counter-examples (Venezuela, Nigeria, Angola) differ from Norway on dozens of institutional dimensions pre-existing the oil. The rule didn't create Norwegian institutional quality; Norwegian institutional quality created the rule.

2. **Commodity-price boom confound.** 2001-2014 coincided with a multi-decade commodity super-cycle. Norwegian fiscal headroom grew from oil-price windfalls independent of the rule's discipline. Clean identification of the rule's effect versus the oil-price exogeneity is not available on this sample alone.

3. **Cross-party consensus is the real story.** The rule works because Norwegian politics sustained cross-party commitment to it across governments. Other countries that have tried similar rules (UK 1997, Chile 2001, Germany 2009) had mixed adherence when political pressure mounted.

4. **The Fund's 3% spending limit is effectively discretionary.** Governments have repeatedly drawn more than 3% in acute periods (2009, 2020) without institutional consequences. "Rule-based" in letter; political-norm-based in practice.

5. **Small-population scale effect.** Norway's ~5M population means even modest-by-global-standards resource rents are massive per-capita. The outcomes don't generalise to larger countries even if the architecture did.

**How this should shape the result card:** credit the rule's outcome coincidence but avoid attribution language that implies transferability. Separate the oil-rent-capture mechanism (a specific Norwegian geological fact) from the rule-based-discipline mechanism (potentially more transferable, but untested on most candidate cases).
