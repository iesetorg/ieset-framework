# Steelman — Greek fiscal dominance post-euro 2001

**The strongest argument against the framework's fiscal-dominance coding:**

The Greek narrative as "cheating on statistics + fiscal irresponsibility + inevitable crisis" is the post-hoc Northern-European morality tale. It understates the role of eurozone design flaws, Northern-European creditor incentives to lend recklessly, and the specific mechanism of Troika-imposed austerity that deepened the crisis. Coding Greece as the paradigmatic "fiscal dominance" case risks moralising a structural failure.

Specific objections:

1. **Creditor responsibility is systematically understated.** German and French banks lent heavily to Greece knowing or should-have-known the fiscal trajectory. The 2009-2010 crisis was as much a creditor-sentiment cascade as a Greek solvency event. Assigning causation purely to Greek policy omits the creditor-side role.

2. **Fiscal statistics misreporting was shared.** Goldman Sachs currency swaps (2001-2002) were a private-sector-engineered mechanism; they were legal under eurozone accounting standards at the time. "Greece misreported" elides that the structures were sold to Greece by institutions that knew exactly what they were for.

3. **The 2001-2009 boom was mostly imported from cheap euro-area capital.** Greek domestic demand expanded because euro-area bond markets treated Greek debt as near-German risk. This pricing error is an ECB-framework / market-pricing failure as much as a Greek policy failure.

4. **Post-2010 Troika austerity was pro-cyclical and contributed to the depth of the collapse.** Greek GDP fell ~25% 2008-2013. Attributing the 2010+ crisis primarily to pre-2010 fiscal choices ignores the policy response's contribution to the magnitude.

5. **Greek reforms DID happen under Troika conditions.** Pension reform 2010 + 2015 + 2016, labour market reform 2010-2012, public-sector retrenchment. The coding of the 2001-2010 period must be distinct from the 2010-2018 period; both are real.

**How this should shape v3:** treat the 2001-2010 period as the fiscal-dominance movement the spec codes; treat the 2010-2018 Troika period as a separate movement (`greek_austerity_troika_2010_2018`, to be added). Do NOT attribute post-2010 GDP collapse to 2001-2009 policy alone; the Troika response is a distinct causal input. The framework's claim should be: the 2001-2009 trajectory made a crisis likely; the 2010+ response shaped its form.
