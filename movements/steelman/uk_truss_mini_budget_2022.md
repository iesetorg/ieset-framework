# Steelman — Truss mini-budget 2022

1. **Market rejection was partly about process, not content.** Announcing ~£45bn unfunded tax cuts + ~£60bn energy spending without OBR forecast and while BoE was tightening into inflation was a credibility-signalling failure, not a market judgement on whether the tax cuts themselves were warranted. A similar content package with OBR-certified costings + phased implementation might have been priced differently.
2. **The LDI pension-fund spiral was a derivative crisis of UK financial regulation, not of the mini-budget specifically.** Liability-driven investment strategies had accumulated leveraged gilt exposure under prior monetary conditions; gilt-yield movement (any large move) would have triggered the same unwinding. The mini-budget was a catalyst; the underlying fragility was pre-existing.
3. **The 49-day duration means causal attribution of later UK outcomes to this movement is weak.** Most of the tax cuts were reversed within weeks. The framework's interest in this movement is the boundary case — a market-liberal content package that failed at execution — rather than as a large causal contributor to 2022-2024 UK performance.
