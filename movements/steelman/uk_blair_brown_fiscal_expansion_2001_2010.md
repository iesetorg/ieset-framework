# Steelman — UK Blair–Brown fiscal expansion

1. **NHS capacity gains were real and durable.** Waiting times halved 2001-2010; new-build hospitals, modern diagnostic equipment, GP access improvements. Coding the decade as "pro-cyclical fiscal drift" elides genuine service improvements the private sector would have struggled to provide at the same speed.
2. **Child/pensioner poverty reduction was substantial.** Tax credits + pension credit halved pensioner poverty and materially reduced child poverty. These are welfare outcomes that don't show up in log GDP per capita but matter for the framework's broader living-standards claim.
3. **The structural deficit entering 2008 was ~3% GDP, not catastrophic** — the crisis-era borrowing was mostly crisis response (bank bailouts, automatic stabilisers), not pre-crisis fiscal mismanagement. The framing of "no countercyclical buffer" over-attributes 2008-2010 deficit growth to Brown-era discretion.
