# Steelman — Lula third term (Brazil, 2023-present)

**The strongest arguments that Lula 3.0 should not be coded as a clean PT-expansionist return:**

1. **The PT's industrial-policy legacy is more heterogeneous than the caricature.** The Nova Indústria Brasil plan (2024) is explicit in conditioning BNDES credit on export and technology-diffusion metrics, echoing the East Asian export-discipline pattern rather than the Dilma-era national-champion pattern (2011-2016) that produced Pronatec / Brasil Maior's mixed outcomes. A content-focused coding must distinguish conditioned from unconditioned industrial policy rather than assuming all PT industrial policy is equivalent.

2. **The Rousseff era (2011-2016) is analytically distinct and should not be allowed to bleed into Lula 3.0 coding.** Rousseff's nova matriz econômica combined fiscal expansion with administrative-price interventions (electricity, fuel) and a directed-credit surge that produced the 2014-2016 recession; Haddad / Tebet's 2023-2024 framework explicitly repudiates those instruments. Conflating Lula 3.0 with the PT expansionist-interventionist archetype mis-codes the policy content.

3. **The Bolsonaro counterfactual is the correct comparator, not the Temer-Meirelles reform trajectory.** A realistic 2023+ no-Lula scenario includes continuation of Bolsonaro-era fiscal volatility (emendas explosion, Teto breaches via PECs, Auxílio Brasil pre-election expansion) rather than a stylised fiscal-conservative counterfactual. Against the realistic counterfactual, the Marco Fiscal represents re-institutionalisation of discipline, not abandonment of it.

4. **Central bank independence is preserved de jure and — through the Galípolo nomination — de facto on the operational margin.** LC 179/2021 statutory independence is untouched; Galípolo is Haddad's nominee but his public rate-path decisions through 2025 were tighter than the market's ex-ante expectation. Political rate-path pressure did not produce policy accommodation.

5. **Only ~2 years of evidence are available at the time of spec drafting.** Assessment is unavoidably premature. A pre-registration that commits to later re-evaluation (v1.1 at 2027) is honest about the epistemic state. Judging early-term GDP and unemployment outcomes as proof of success would be symmetrical to judging Petro's early-term slowdown as proof of failure — neither is methodologically clean at 2-year horizons.

**How this should shape the result card:**

- Pre-register the specific fiscal-framework compliance test (primary-balance trajectory vs the Lei Complementar 200/2023 band) rather than a general "fiscal discipline" assessment.
- Separate Lula 3.0 from Rousseff-era PT coding explicitly.
- Publish the central-bank-independence indicator trajectory (operational autonomy proxies, rate-path deviation from Taylor) alongside fiscal indicators.
- Report industrial-policy allocation with conditionality coding (conditioned on exports / R&D milestones vs unconditioned sectoral transfer).
- Acknowledge the counterfactual realism problem: a no-Lula 2023 baseline is not Cardoso-tripod, it is Bolsonaro-2022 trend extrapolation.
